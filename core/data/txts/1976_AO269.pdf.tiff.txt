<subject>SUBJECT: Policies on the Rural Health Practice Program for Medical and Nursing Graduates</subject>

<body>
In accordance with the verbal order of His Excellency, President Ferdinand E. Marcos, the following fundamental policies for the Rural Health Practice Program for Medical and Nursing graduates are hereby promulgated for the strict compliance by all concerned:

1. The Rural Health Practice Program was conceived by President Marcos to augment existing health services and medical manpower in the rural areas.

2. The Rural Health Practice Program shall be under the management of the Program Director. He may appoint a Committee to help him supervise the Program.

3. The Committee shall assist in the planning, supervision, and coordination of the activities of the Program.

4. The Rural Health Practice Program for medical and nursing graduates shall be a pre-requisite for licensure in the Professional Regulations Commission.

5. The Rural Health Practice Program shall be for a duration of six months.

6. The Rural Health Practice Program shall have two schedules during the year: January to June and July to December.

7. The Rural Health Practice Program shall cover all aspects of health care services in rural communities.

8. Participants of the Program shall draw lots to determine their place of assignment.

9. Participants while serving in their respective assignments shall be under the supervision of Department of Health personnel in the area.

10. The Rural health and hospital personnel in the places where participants are assigned shall assist the latter in their housing and transport needs and help look after their health and welfare.

11. The participants of the Program shall be paid transportation expenses from the place of origin to their place of assignment and back.

12. The participants of the Program shall be entitled to a monthly stipend of P 300.00 except in hardship areas where an additional amount may be paid as recommended by the Program Director and approved by the Secretary of Health.

13. The participants shall be covered by a group term life, accident and hospitalization insurance.

14. The participants shall be entitled to seven days sick leave with stipend, while serving their six- month term.

15. The Participants shall be required to submit a monthly report of their activities through their respective supervisors.

All other previous orders inconsistent with this Order are hereby rescinded or otherwise amended accordingly.

This Order shall take effect immediately.
