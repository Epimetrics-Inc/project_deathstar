I. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE 

The Republic Act No. 10633, The General Appropriations Act of FY 2014, allocated Php 3.1938 for the Medical Care Assistance Program of the Department of Health. The ﬁnancial assistance shall be utilized for the grant of assistance to patients seeking consult or being admitted in a government hospital. The Republic Act further provides that the beneﬁciary shall comply with the requirements of DOH and health care institutions/providers to be entitled for the assistance. 

Administrative Order No.2010—0026 dated September 13, 2010 was issued to set the guidelines that will implement the Medical Health Care Assistance Program of the Department of Health. With the additional funds provided under RA 10633, it has become imperative to enhance this order to expand the coverage and improve its implementation. 

The revisions in this Administrative Order shall ensure a more effective and efﬁcient system for managing and monitoring the Medical Health Care Assistance Program of the Ofﬁce of the Secretary and a regular replenishment process for said funds allocated to DOH Retained "Hospitals and reimbursement proCess for Local Government Unit (LGU) hospitals and State University and College (SUC) managed Hospitals. 

II. SCOPE 

This policy shall apply to all ofﬁces involved in the implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program in the Central Ofﬁce, Regional Ofﬁce, and DOH Retained Hospitals of the Department of Health and other concerned health facilities. 

III. OBJECTIVE 

This Policy and Guidelines are promulgated to prescribe rational management and monitoring of the allocation and utilization of lndigency Fund by DOH Central Ofﬁce, Regional Ofﬁce, and DOH Retained Hospitals of the Department of Health and other concerned health facilities. 

IV. DEFINITION OF TERMS 

1. Health Facility — refers to DOH retained hospitals, LGU hospitals and SUC Hospitals. 

2. Indigency Fund — represents the special fund provided for in the General Appropriation Act intended for the medical care assistance of indigent patients referred by the Ofﬁce of the Secretary or his representative. 

3. Indigent Patient — refers to a patient or a member of a family who cannot provide for the basic food and non food-threshold. Their income is below the latest Per Capita Poverty Threshold of the region in which the hospital is located. It also refers to a patient who is listed folassiﬁed as poor in the DSWD website for the National Household Targeting system for Poverty Reduction or individuals in crisis as maybe certiﬁed by a DSWD/LGU social worker. 

4. DOH Retained Hospitals — refers to all other hospitals under the management and operation of the DOH which are not classiﬁed as Specialty Hospitals and Special Hospitals 

5. LGU Hospitals — refers to hospitals under the management and operation of the Local GovernmentUnit such as but not limited to provincial, district and city hospitals 

6. Medical Health Care Assistance Program (MHCAP) — refers to the program that will manage the Indigency Fund of the DOH 

7. Public Assistance and Complaint Unit (PACU) — refers to the ofﬁce in the DOH Hospitals that will coordinate the implementation of the MHCAP at their level 

8. Public Assistance Unit (PAU) Central Ofﬁce — refers to the ofﬁce in the DOH Central Ofﬁce that will implement the MHCAP 

9. Specialty Hospitals — refers to the four (4) corporate hospitals namely, Philippine Heart Center (PHC), National Kidney and Transplant Institute (N KTI), Lung Center of the Philippines (LCP) and Philippine Children Medical Center (PCMC). 

10. Special Hospital — refers to the hospitals under the DOH that are primarily engaged in the provision of speciﬁc clinical care and management such as National Children’s Hospital, Philippine Orthopedic Center, etc. 

11. State University and College (SUC) Hospitals— refers to hospitals under the management and operation of the State University and College such as but not limited to Philippine General Hospital and West Visayas State University Medical Center - 

12. Sub-Allotment — refers to a fund transferred to operating units. 

13. Far Flung Hospitals - refers to DOH hospitals located far from the Regional Ofﬁces, e.g. Batanes General Hospital under the R0 for Metro Manila and Far North General Hospital and Training Center under R0 for CAR. 

14. Walk in patient - refers to a patient directly seeking assistance from the Public Assistance Unit or PACU due to unaffordable health needs. 

15. Web-Enabled Public Assistance Information System (WEB PAIS) - refers to the information system used by the DOH to track the implementation of the MHCAP 

16. Working Poor — refers to a patient or family with a fulltime work as source of income but resources are insufﬁcient to meet their treatment expenses in full.

V. GENERAL GUIDELINES 

1. The Medical Health Care Assistance Program shall utilize Funds from the Assistance to Indigent Patients which is in a line item in the General Appropriations Act under the Ofﬁce of the Secretary and/or any available fund from the Ofﬁce of the Secretary, as identiﬁed by the Secretary of Health, and is allocated to all recipient hospitals. 

2. The Public Assistance Unit (PAU) of the Central Ofﬁce shall be responsible for the overall management and administration of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program. 

3. DOH Retained Hospitals shall create Public Assistance and Complaint Unit (PACU) under the Billing Section and shall function as the counterpart of the 

4. The Medical Social Work Department/Unit of the Hospitals shall be responsible for implementing the program at the hospital level. 

5. The Finance Service of the hospital shall be responsible for monitoring the utilization of the fund and preparing the appropriate ﬁnancial report for submission to the PAU of Central Ofﬁce. 

6. The Medical Health Care Assistance Program shall be staffed by personnel exemplifying courtesy and excellent service to all patients at all times. 

7. The Medical Health Care Assistance Program shall adopt a system of management that is effective and efﬁcient. 

8. The Medical Health Care Assistance shall support universal coverage of PHIC. 

VI. IMPLEMENTING MECHANISM 

A. Eligible Beneficiaries 

The program beneﬁciaries are individuals who are either: 

1. Walk In /Referred Patients who have been classiﬁed as indigent patients, working poor or those determined to be unable to afford the speciﬁed cost of medical treatment. 

2. Patients listed/classiﬁed as indigent are those: 

a. In the DSWD website for the National Household Targeting System for Poverty Reduction and LGU list; 

b. Patients classiﬁed as indigent or individual in crisis by the public social worker of the LGU ”or DSWD or the Barangay Captain in which the patients resides; and 

c. Patients certiﬁed as indigent or working poor or individual in crisis by the Medical Social Work Department (MSWD) of the health facility where patient sought consultation or is admitted/conﬁned. 

3. Patients certiﬁed by the Secretary of Health, Head Executive Assistant, Chief of Hospitals and Head of the Central Ofﬁce PAU or their duly authorized representatives as having met the above criteria for eligibility.

D. Allocation Use and Replenishment of the Indigency Fund 

1. The Central Ofﬁce PAU shall determine the amount of Indigency Fund to be allocated and transferred/sub-allotted/ distributed to Health Facilities. 

2. The DOH Retained Hospitals shall only receive replenishment of their Indigency Fund upon submission and approval of the Utilization Report prepared in adherence to prescribed guidelines. 

3. The LGU Hospitals and SUC managed Hospitals shall only receive reimbursement of their Indigency Fund upon submission and approval of the Utilization Report prepared in adherenCe to prescribed guidelines. 

4. No money/cash shall be released directly to patients or any relatives. 


E. Fund Utilization Report 

Participating agencies shall submit a Monthly Fund Utilization Report on or before the 7th day of the ensuing month. 

VII. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES 

1. Public Assistance Unit at the Central Office 

a. Screen and assess the eligibility of patients seeking the medical assistance based on the documentary requirements submitted; 

b. Monitor the implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program at the DOH Central Ofﬁce; 

c. Responsible for the overall management of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program nationwide and as such; 

c.1. Coordinate and collaborate with the DOH Hospitals for all ﬁnancial and administrative concerns in implementing the program;

c.2. Recommend to Finance the amount of fund to be sub «- allotted or transferred to the different hospitals;

c.3. Review and evaluate Utilization Reports submitted by the DOH Hospitals and other health facilities;

c.4. Consolidate reports submitted by all units on PHIC membership and referred non- PHIC members. -

c.5. Consolidate Annual Inventory Reports of Approved Medical Assistance submitted by the DOH Specialty, Special and Retained Hospitals and submit the report to the Secretary of Health with viable recommendations for the improvement of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program.

c.6. Conduct Medical Health Care Assistance Program evaluation on a semi-annual basis. 

2. DOH Retained Hospitals 

a. Enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with LGU Hospital and SUC Hospital for the implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program. 

b. Issues guarantee letter to LGU Hospitals and SUC Hospitals for indigent patients referred in accordance with the guidelines set in this Order. 

c. Prepare and facilitate processing of Disbursement Vouchers for the reimbursement of the cost of medical care incurred by the LGU Hospital and SUC managed Hospital in the implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program; 

d. Consolidate annual inventory reports of approved medical assistance submitted by the LGU Hospital and SUC Hospitals including far flung hospitals and other health facilities and submit said report to the PAU Central Ofﬁce with viable recommendations for the improvement of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program; 

e. Conduct periodic on site monitoring to ensure compliance to the program. 

f. Monitor the status of fund transferred from the DOH Central Ofﬁce using the WEBPAIS. 

3. Operation Cluster Heads 

a. Monitor the strict implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program as prescribed by this Administrative Order; 

b. Review monitoring report and recommendation for the efﬁcient implementation of the program to the Ofﬁce of the Secretary as need arises; 

4. DOH-Financial Service 

a. Prepare and facilitate processing of Disbursement Vouchers for fund transfer to DOH Specialty Hospitals and request for sub-allotment for DOH Special and Retained hospitals upon recommendation of PAU Central Ofﬁce; 

b. Provide technical assistance on ﬁnancial issues and concerns. 

c. Consolidate the monthly fund utilization of the Indigency Fund 

5. DOH-Information Management Service 

a. Provide technical assistance in the maintenance and upgrading of WEB PAIS; 

b. Conduct training related to Web-enabled Public Assistance Information System (WEB-PAIS); 

DOH Hospitals/Far Flung Hospitals/LGU Hospitals-rand SUC Hospitals 

a. Facilitate the provision of approved medical assistance; 

b. Prepare, through their accounting/ﬁnance service, the fund utilization report reﬂecting the charges made for the period against the Indigency Fund , signed by the Chief Accountant, approved by the Head of Agency and submit to the PAU Central Ofﬁce for repleniShment or DOH Retained Hospital for reimbursement; 

c. Implement the MHCAP program at the hospital level through the MSWD; 

d. Monitor the status of fund transferred from the DOH Central Ofﬁce using the WEB PAIS; 

e. The LGU Hospitals and SUC Hospitals shall take full responsibility over any and all transactions related to the medical assistance provided and accordingly are advised to secure COA post audit review. 

VIII. MONITORING AND EVALUATION 

1. A monitoring tool/template shall be prepared by PAU central ofﬁce. 

2. PAU Central Ofﬁce will provide a standard patient’s satisfaction survey form which will be distributed to all participating agencies. Patients will be encouraged to answer the patient’s satisfaction survey form. Result of the survey shall be included in the report submitted to PAU Central Ofﬁce. 

3. The Program Review shall be conducted to assess the impact of the program on DOH thrusts and to commend to the RO, Specialty, Special and Retained Hospitals and its units for their support and partnership in the implementation of the Medical Health Care Assistance Program. 

4. All participating ofﬁces in the MHCAP are required to participate in all the Monitoring and Evaluation activities. 

IX. REPEALING CLAUSE 

The provisions of existing administrative issuances, guidelines or regulations inconsistent with this order are hereby repealed, superseded, revised or modiﬁed.

X. EFFECTIVITY CLAUSE 

These implementing guidelines shall take effect immediately upon approval.
