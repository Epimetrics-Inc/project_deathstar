<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines on the Transfer of Funds Utilization intended for the capability upgrading of specific DOH SARS Referral Hospitals</subject>

<body>
I. Rationale and Objectives

In accordance with Executive Order (EO) 201 which defines the authority and responsibility of the Department of Health (DOH) to control the SARS contagion, this Order is issued in order to rationalize the utilization of funds to be transferred to specific DOH SARS Referral Hospitals as identified hereon by way of providing specific guidelines.

II. Scope

This Order shall be applicable to specific DOH SARS Referral Hospitals (see Annex A) using available funds as a consequence of EO 201.

III. Guidelines

A. The designated DOH-SARS Referral Hospitals as identified in Annex A of this Order shall be granted an amount of 1.5 million pesos each for the purpose of providing for the physical needs and/or improvement of their SARS Isolation and Isolation-Related Facilities.

B. The plan for the SARS isolation and Isolation-Related Facilities has been prepared by each hospital and evaluated/approved by the National Center for Health Facility Development as translated from the interim clinical guidelines for SARS (See Annex B).

C. The scope of works to be covered by the funds shall be for the Provision/Improvement of each of the hospital’s SARS isolation and isolation-related facilities specifically:

1. One (1) Holding Area/Room, preferably near the triage area, with toilet and lavatory

2. One (1) Admitting Room, adjacent to the isolation rooms, with:
a. Changing room/anteroom for the use of clinical staff with handwashing counter and sink, cabinets for storage of new PPE and space for biohazard bin for used PPE
b. Patient’s toilet and bath
c. Patients handwashing counter with sink and water heater

3. At least two (2) Isolation Rooms, each room with
a. Changing room/anteroom for the use of clinical staff with handwashing counter and sink, cabinets for storage of new PPE and space for biohazard bin for used PPE
b. Patient’s toilet and bath
c. Patients handwashing counter with sink and water heater

D. The admitting and isolation rooms must be installed with air condition units, exhaust fans, nurse call and intercom units, entrance doors with glass panels. Adequate water supply and fire protection features.

E. The detailed architecture and engineering plans, technical specification and other documents for procurement and construction purpose are to be prepared by each hospital with the technical assistance of the engineering unit of the Center for Health Development concerned, if necessary.

F. The implementation of the works shall either be born by the hospital through administration, or by a work contractor procured through negotiation per RA 9184 condition.( when times is of the essence)

G. A report of fund utilization shall be submitted to the DOH Finance Service upon completion of the work, certified and indorsed by the accountant and the Chief of Hospital respectively, and verified by their auditor.

H. Liquidation of Funds and Implementation of Work should be within 2 months.

IV. Separability Clause

If any provision in these guidelines or application of such provision to any circumstance, is held invalid, the remainder of these guidelines shall not be affected.

V. Repealing Clause

The provisions of previous issuances, which are inconsistent with those provided in this Order, are hereby rescinded and modified accordingly.

VI. Effectivity

This Order shall take effect immediately.
