I RATIONALE

In line with the thrust of the Department of Health to ensure quality health care as a means of improving the quality of life of all Filipinos especially the poor, several projects have been prepared and approved for funding by International Funding Institutions. Among these projects is the Project entitled Construction and Equipping of the Davao Medical Center which is funded by the Japan International Cooperation Agency. In order to fully achieve the objectives of this Project and to ensure maximum utilization of this important health resource, guidelines on Project implementation need to be set.


II OBJECTIVES

(1) To set the roles and responsibilities of the various Project key players;
(2) To ensure that the Project objectives are in line with the overall objectives of the Hospital Development Plan;
(3) To ensure that the objectives of the Project are accomplished on time, within budget and in conformity with sound technical performance standards.


III ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE KEY PLAYERS OF THE PROJECT

(!) Office of the Chief of the Medical Center

The Chief of the Medical Center shall serve as the Project Manager. The we responsibilities of the Office of the Project Manager shall include.

* Supervision of the day-to-day implementation of the Project;
* Monitoring progress of the works of the Contractor;
* Inspection and acceptance of all goods to be supplied by the Contractor under the Project prior to installation;
* Testing and acceptance of all goods after these have been installed by the Contractor.
* Endorsement of claims for payment of the Contractor to the Director of the Center for Health Development Southern Mindanao (CHD-SM);
* Submission of annual GOP counterpart fund requirement of the Project to the CHD-SM director for inclusion in the CHD-SM budget;
* Submission of Project implementation progress reports to the CHD-SM Director copy furnished the Unified Project Management Office-Bureau of International Health and Cooperation (UPMO-BIHC); 
* Approval of Change Orders/Variation Orders and implementation schedules that do not constitute increase in the Project cost and/or do not require approval of the JICA copy furnished CHD-SM Director and UPMO- BIHC;
* Submission to the Office of the CHD-SM Director, request for Change/Variation Orders that would increase the cost of the Project
and/or that would require approval of the JICA; and
* Submission to the CHD-SM Director request for amendments to the Contract.

The Chief of the Medical Center shall be assisted by the Engineer of the Medical Center on physical facilities (civil works and equipment) matters; Supply Officer on inspection and recording of goods delivered; Finance Officer on booking up of goods delivered and accepted; and Administrative Officer on the administrative needs of the Project

(2) Office of the Director Center for Health Development for Southern Mindanao - this Office shall be responsible for: 

* Monitoring and evaluation of the progress of Project implementation in coordination with the Project Manager; 
* Providing assistance to the Office of the Chief of the Medical Center in resolving implementation bottlenecks that can not be resolved at that level;
* Including and defending the annual GOP counterpart fund requirement of the Project in the CHD-SM budget;
* Reviewing/recommending to the UPMO—BIHC approval for Change/Variation Orders that would increase the cost of the Project and/or that would require approval of the JICA;
* Reviewing/recommending to the UPMO-BIHC approval of amendments to the contract
* Updating the BIHC on the progress of Project implementation on a quarterly basis using the prescribed monitoring tool for DOH Foreign Assisted Projects;
* Reviewing/recommending approval to the UPMO-BIHC claims for payment of the contractor.

(3) Office of the National Center for Health Facility Development—this office shall be responsible for;
* Reviewing requests for Change Orders, Variation Orders that would increase the cost of the project
* Periodic inspection of the works of the contractor in coordination with the Office of the Director CHD—SM.
* Assisting the CHD-SM in overseeing installation and testing of equipment.
* Assisting the CHD-SM in the final inspection and acceptance of the Project.

(4) Office of the Bureau of International Health Cluster/Unified Project Management Ofﬁce

The BIHC through its Uniﬁed Project Management Office (UPMO) shall be responsible for:

* Monitoring and evaluation of the progress of implementation of the Project in coordination with the Office of the CHD-SM;
* Providing assistance to the CHD—SM in resolving implementation bottlenecks that can not be resolved at the CHD level,
* Ensuring that the annual GOP counterpart fund requirement of the Project is included in the CHD SM budget; 
* Updating the Secretary/Undersecretary/Assistant Secretary on the progress of implementation of the Project from time to time;
* Reviewing/Endorsing to the JICA claims for payment of the Contractor, in the absence of the Assistant Secretary for Management Services; 
* Endorsing to JICA requests for approval of Change Orders/Variation Orders that would increase the cost of the Project;
* Reviewing/endorsing to JICA requests for contract amendment; and
* Periodic coordination With the JICA and oversight agencies at the Central Office level.

(5) Assistant Secretary for Management Services

The Assistant Secretary shall endorse to the JICA claims for payment by the Contractor.
