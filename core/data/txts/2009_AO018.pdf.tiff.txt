I. Rationale

As a policy the Department of Health (DOH) leads and coordinates support from the different stakeholders and development partners in formulating Philippine health sector policy reforms. The Sector Development Approach for Health (SDAH) as elaborated in A0 2007— 0038 “Adopting the Sector Development Approach for Health (SDAH) in the implementation of Fl for Health " aims to reduce fragmentation of partner suppOrt. increase the impact on health seCtOr. - development and sustain institutional development by Using government systems and procedures instead or parallel implementation structures. It incorporates the key principles of the Paris Declaration on Aid Effectiveness particularly on' harmonization and alignment which aims to reduce transaction costs in engagement with donors and other development partners particularly in the planning and review processes. 

Development partners become health Stakeholders when they suppert the health sector goals and priorities in general and health reforms through the Fourmula One for Health (Fl) in particular. Such support may be in the form at program assistance site specific assistance F1 pillar assistance or agency-Wide assistance

III. scope 

This Administrative Order covers all development partners support to health sector which nature may vary from program assistance, site—specific assistance, Fl pillar assistance or agency-wide assistance. 

IV. General Principles 

1. The Development Partner Scorecard (DPS) shall be implemented in a transparent manner. 

2. The DPS will cover all development partners providing assistance to the Philippine health sector 

3. The DPS shall be used as a tool for dialogue and shall not be used to rank develOpment partners. 

4. The DPS shall serve as a report to all health sector stakeholders for enhancing partnership toward attainment of health sector goals. 

V. Deﬁnition of Terms 

1. Paris Declaration on Aid Effectiveness — The Paris Declaration is an international agreement to which over one hundred country representatives and international organizations committed their countries and organisations to increase efforts in harmonizing aligning and managing aid for results with a set of monitorable actions and indicators. It was officially endorsed in March 2005. The Philippines adheres to this agreement 

2. Alignment — Development partner support is based on national development strategies. institutions. systems and procedures. 

3. Harmonization - Development partner processes are implemented using common arrangements and simplified procedures in terms of monitoring, evaluation and reporting. 

4. Development Partner — foreign or local organization/entity which provides support in the form of program assistance site specific assistance in any pillar of the retarm agenda. 

5. Monitoring and Evaluation far Equity and Effectiveness (MES) The MES is a system that aims to determine if reforms have equitably and effectively achieved health system geals. 

6. Scorecard — A scorecard is a tool for measuring and reporting inputs processes outputs and outcomes of stakeholders Which they are accountable in a manner that clients and other stakeholders can easily camprehend. The DPS is one of the five scarecards under the MES. It measures inputs structures processes, and major final outputs of development partner support in the context of SDAH and the Paris Declaration.

Introduction 

The Development Partner Scorecard (DPS) is a tool to measure outcomes or outputs valuable toclients in a manner that can be easily comprehended. In this case the client is theIDepartment of Health the lead agency in the Philippine _ Health Sector. The purpose of the DPS is to measure alignment and harmonization of development partner support In health. 

The DPS shall be used as a self—assessment tool by the development partners for demonstrating progress in the SDAH. It will serve as an instrUment far dialogue and will not be used to rank development partners. 

Indicators  

There are twelve indicators in the scorecard categorized under two of five principles of the Paris Declaration on Aid effectiveness -- Alignment and Harmonization. Indicators were patterned after Indicators of Progress of the Paris Declaration and then modified to fit the local situation. 

Alignment of development partner support is monitored according to support provided to the health planning and implementatizon 

alignment to health planning & programming— 3 indicators alignment to plan implementation— 6 indicators 

Harmonization is measured according to support provided to monitoring and evaluation (3 indicators) 

Color Coding Scheme 

The DPS will utilize a color codingscheme to present the status of an indiCator. Only three colors will be used to keep the scheme simple and easy to understand. A red color code reflects that there is substantial potentialfor improvement. A yellow . color code shows presence of some weakness in performance that should be. addressed to otimize performance. A green color code reflects performance that is up to par or exceeds standards. 

Accomplishment of the Tool and Validation of Results - Development partners will be provided with an electronic copy of the DPS by the BIHC secretariat and will accomplish the tool by coloring the circle across each indicator. Accomplished tools shall be submitted to the BIHC on a requested date. Results shall be validated and then consolidated for presentation in selected fora . and publications.
