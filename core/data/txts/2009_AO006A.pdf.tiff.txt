<subject>SUBJECT: Addendum to Administrative Order No. 2009- 0006 dated January 13, 2009 re: Guidelines on Antiretroviral Therapy among Adults and Adolescents with Human Immunodeﬁciency Virus (HIV) Infection</subject>

Provision of vaccines is important to prevent infectious diseases and its accompanying morbidity and mortality especially to immunocompromised individuals such as people living with HIV        (PLHIV). As such, provision of vaccines should be included to the existing administrative order on the guidelines on antiretroviral therapy in order to fully complement and complete the treatment, care and support package to PLHIV.  

The following shall be added to the Administrative Order No. 2009-0006:

a. Addendum to Subject which states that: 

SUBJECT: Guidelines on Antiretroviral Therapy among Adults and Adolescents with Human Imunodeficiencv Virus (HIV) Infection 

The foregoing subject is hereby amended and read as follows: 

SUBJECT: Guidelines on Antiretroviral Therapy and Provision of Vaccines among Adults and Adolescents with Human Immunodeﬁciency Virus (HIV) Infection 

b. Addendum to Implementing Guidelines 

The following provision shall be added to Section V Implementing Guidelines of the said Administrative Order: 

K. Provision of Vaccines to Adults and Adolescents with HIV Infection 

1. The following vaccines must be given to all PLHIV regardless of CD4 Cell Count: 
a. Hepatitis B 
b. Inﬂuenza (Inactivated type) 
c. Pneumococcal 

2. The following vaccines may be given safely to PLHIV if indicated, regardless of CD4 Cell 

Count: 
a. Hepatitis 
b. Cholera 
c. Haemophilus Inﬂuenza Type B 
d. Japanese B Encephalitis 
e. Meningococcal 
f. Rabies 
g. Td (Tetanus, Diphtheria)/ Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis)
h. Inactivated Polio Vaccine 
i. Typhoid vaccine (Vi capsular polysaccharide vaccine) 
j. Human Papilloma Virus 

3. The following vaccines may be given safely to PLHIV if indicated and if the CD4 Cell Count is more than 200 cu/mm3 : 
a. Mumps, Measles and Rubella 
b. Varicella 
c. Yellow fever 

4. The following vaccines should not be given to PLHIV regardless of the CD4 Cell 

Count: 
a. Oral Polio Vaccine 
b. BCG 
c. Typhoid Vaccine (Ty21a, live vaccine) 
d. Inﬂuenza (live attenuated inﬂuenza vaccine) 
e. Herpes Zoster 

All other provisions of Administrative Order No. 2009-0006 dated January 13, 2009, stand in effect. 

This order shall take effect immediately.
