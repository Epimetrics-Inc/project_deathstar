<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines and Procedures on Accreditation of Government Suppliers for Pharmaceutical Products</subject>

<body>

This shift of the procurement of drugs from the Central Office to the Regional Health Offices calls for the accreditation of qualified drug suppliers that are able to supply safe, efficacious, affordable and good quality drugs.

This order hereby sets in place the foregoing guidelines and procedures on accreditation.

1. Accreditation for drug suppliers shall be centralized, and lodged at the Office for Standards and Regulation. A unit shall be created to be composed of a Chairperson, Vice—Chairperson and four other members.

1.1 The Undersecretary for Standards and Regulations shall be the Chairperson; the Director of the Bureau of Licensing and Regulation, shall act as the Vice-Chairperson. The four other members shall be from the Bureau of Food and Drugs and the Philippine National Drug Policy Program; one each from Regulations Division I, Regulation Division II, Product Services Division and from the National Drug Policy Program.

The Unit shall be assisted by a Secretariat of at least two members from the BLR.

2. The following types of establishments are eligible for accreditation:

2.1. Drug Manufacturer means any establishment engaged in operations involved 1n the production of drug, including propagation, processing, compounding, finishing, filling, packing, re-packing, altering, ornamenting and labelling with the end in view of storage, distribution or sale of the product; provided that for the purpose of this regulation the compounding and filling of prescriptions in drugstores and hospital pharmacies shall not be considered as production operations.

2.2. Drug Distributor/wholesaler means any establishment that acts as exclusive distributor of any drug product of local and foreign accredited manufacturers and or of a drug importer.

3. Foreign Manufacturers of pharmaceutical products shall be subject to inspection by BFAD at company’s expense. Presently, however, WHO certification scheme shall be adopted for this purpose.

4. Drug suppliers shall be categorized based on financial, service and production  capability (Annex 1) and this shall be indicated in the accreditation certificate.

5. Only complete application containing all the specific required documents (Annex 2) shall be accepted by the Secretariat lodged at the BLR.

6. Only BFAD duly authenticated documents of the following are to be submitted:

6.1. Valid license to operate (LTO) 
6.2 Valid Certificate of Product Registration (CPR)

7. Validity of Accreditation 

Accreditation will be valid for three (3) years but subject to yearly review. Other mechanism for evaluation maybe applied such as conduction of visits to the manufacturing plant. Any violation will result to the withdrawal of said accreditation.

Accreditation shall start immediately upon approval of this Order.
