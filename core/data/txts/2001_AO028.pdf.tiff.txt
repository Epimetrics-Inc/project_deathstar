<subject>SUBJECT: Designation of the Bureau of Food and Drugs (BFAD) as the lead unit of the Department of Health (DOH) in the Implementation and Monitoring of the Food Fortification Program</subject>


<body>

Food fortification is a unique example of a DOH program where the food industry which work in a largely commercial environment plat a major role in health intervention endeavors. However, for the government to undertake effective food fortification activities, it is first necessary to ensure that the laws and regulations are fully implemented and enforced. In the Philippines, two (2) bills have been approved and signed into law namely: Republic Act (RA) 8172 or ASIN Law and RA 8976 or Food Fortification Act of 2000.

In the exigency of service and to streamline the functions of the DOH whereby regulatory functions are delegated to regulatory agencies of the DOH, the BFAD shall hereon be the lead unit in the implementation of the Food Fortification Program. Pursuant to this, BFAD shall be responsible in the formulation of policies, guidelines and standards relative to food fortification, provide technical assistance on technology and quality assurance to the industry and monitor program implementation. In addition, BFAD shall be responsible in the full implementation and enforcement of RA 8172 and RA 8976. As lead unit, BFAD shall also carry out the following functions: convene and chair the National Food Fortification Steering Committee (NFFSC) and the Sub-Committee on Salt. Furthermore, organize and act as a secretariat to all the existing sub-committees on Flour, Sugar, Oil and Rice.

The Center for Family and Environmental Health (CFEH) as well as the National Center for Health Promotion (NCHP) shall primarily assist in the implementation of the Food Fortification Program through the conduct of intensive information and education campaigns and be a member of the NFFSC and all the sub-committees.

This Order shall be take effect August 1, 2001.
