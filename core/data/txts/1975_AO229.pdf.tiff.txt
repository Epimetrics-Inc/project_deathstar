<subject>SUBJECT: Renewal of designation as Deputy Dangerous Drugs Inspector of certain law enforcement officers whose deputation as such expired on December 31, 1974.</subject>

<body>
Pursuant to the authority vested in me as Secretary of Health, concurrently Chairman of the Dangerous Drugs Board, to enforce the provisions of Section 27 of Republic Act No. 3720 and Section 12 of Republic Act No. 953 in relation to pertinent provisions of Republic Act 6425, the designation of the following persons as Deputy Dangerous Drugs Inepectors much was effected thru Administrative Order Nos. 207 and 217 dated March 5, 1974 and June 19, 1974, respectively, and which expired on December 31 , 1974 is, in the interest of the public service, hereby renewed effective January 1, 1975, and to continue only up to December 31, 1975, unless sooner revoked:

<image>table_1.png</image>

<image>table_2.png</image>

It is understood that the nature of the authority and the guidelines defined in the previous Administrative Orders (Nos. 207 and 217 dated March 5, l974 and June 19,1974, respectively), shall continue to prevail under the renewed designation, to wit:

A. Nature of Authority

1) to enter, at reasonable hours, any madical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore, factory, warehouse, or other drug establishment in which drugs, are manufactured, processed, packed, or held, for introduction into commerce or are held after such introduction, or to enter any vehicle being used to transport or hold such drugs, in commerce; and

2) to inspect, in a reasonable manner, such medical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore, factory, warehouse, estahlishment, or vehicle and all pertinent equipment, finished and unfinished materials, containers, and labeling-therein.

B. Guidelines:

1) Their area of jurisdiction shall be limited within the city/municipality where they are regularly assigned as police officers.

2) They shall, in respect of their duties as deputy dangerous drugs inspectors, be under the direct supervision and control of the Secretary of Health.
 
   
3) If any of the above-named persons making such inspection of medical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore, factory, warehouse or other establishment has obtained any sample in the course of the inspection, upon completion of the inspection and prior to leaving the premises, he shall give to the owner, operator, or agent in charge a receipt describing the sample obtained.

4) They shall submit an after-operation report of every activity and accomplishment to the Secretary of Health, copy furnished the Food and Drug Administration, provided that if there are more than one activity or accomplishment performed in one day, a single integrated report of all such activities or accomplishments in one day will suffice.
