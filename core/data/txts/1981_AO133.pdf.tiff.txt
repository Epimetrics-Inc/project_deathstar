<subject>AMENDMENT TO SECTION 14a and 14c(3)(d)3 OF ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER NO. 156, SERIES OF 1971, DATED SEPTEMBER 23, 1971, CONCERNING THE RULES AND REGULATIONS GOVERNING THE COLLECTION, PROCESSING, AND SALE OF HUMAN BLOOD AND THE ESTABLISHMENT AND OPERATION OF BLOOD BANKS AND BLOOD PROCESSING LABORATORIES, AS AMENDED BY ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER NO. 144-32, SERIES OF 1973.</subject>

<body>
SECTION 1. The provisions of Section 14 sub-paragraph a and c(3)(d)3 of Administrative Order No. 156, series of 1971, are hereby amended to read as follows:

"Sec. 14 - Minimum requirements for the collection, processing and disposal of citrated whole blood (human)

a. Donor requirement - Professional blood donors to be qualified to give blood must first obtain a medical clearance from the Bureau of Research and Laboratories, except blood donors of a hospital-based blood bank in which case the determination of the suitability of the donor shall be the responsibility of the physician-in-charge of the blood bank concerned. However, donors from hospital maybe referred to the Bureau for proper clearance. Provided however, that the medical clearance requirement for provincial blood donors shall be enforced by stages at a time as the Bureau of Research and Laboratories may deem proper and appropriate.

A medical clearance, in both instances, shall mean that the donor is free of diseases transmissible by blood transfusion particularly syphilis and hepatitis B. Hemoglobin determination shall be done on all blood donors.

c. Collection of the blood -
(3) Collection and storage of blood —
(d) Labelling — the label of the blood container must contain the following —
(3) Donor's name and control number."

SECTION 2. These amendments shall take effect 60 days upon approval and publication in the Official Gazette.
