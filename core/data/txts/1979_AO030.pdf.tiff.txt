<subject>SUBJECT: Criteria for Accreditation of Teaching and Training Hospitals under the Ministry of Health</subject>

<body>
The following guidelines are the criteria for the accreditation of Ministry of Health Teaching and Training Hospitals by the Committee on Accreditation of Teaching and Training Hospitals and Certification of Medical Specialists:  
  
1. The minimum requirement for accreditation is the license issued by the Bureau of Medical Services and/or Regional Directors Special Licensing Teams. The standards for categorization of hospitals are: (a) Manpower or Staffing Pattern; (b) Diagnostic Services Capabilities and Skills; (c) Technical Equipment and Instruments; and (d) Physical Facilities pursuant to Administrative Order No. 13, s. 1978 dated December 2, 1978.

2. Teaching and Training Hospitals should be licensed and categorized to deliver tertiary health care.

3. Only those participating in the program of manpower dispersal shall be accredited.

4. All previously designated Teaching and Training Hospitals are given one year grace period or up to August 1, 1980 to be accredited so that their graduate training residents could take the Certifying Board Examination.
