GUIDELINES FOR ALLOCATION OF IUD KITS


I. Rationale

In line with the DOH thrust to improve access and expand availability of FP methods in the government service outlets, the Family Planning Service with support from the Women's Health and Safe Motherhood Project will distribute IUD Kits to health facilities nationwide. These kits are also intended to augment the existing equipment in your facility.


II. Eligibility Criteria for Service Facilities Recipient:

1. The recipient facility must be an accredited FP service outlet by the Regional Health Office. 
2. Service providers of the facility had undergone training on Basic Comprehensive/CBT Family Planning Course conducted by DOH/RHO/Provincial trainers.
3. The facility conforms with the quality of care standards FP service provision set by DOH—FPS.


III. Allocation Guidelines:

1. The IUD kits are intended exclusively for the use of health providers trained in IUD insertion.

2. Regional FP coordinators are designated to do the allocation to the facility within the region/province/city/municipality, particularly the preceptor areas with preceptor trained on CBT.

3. The Family Planning Service should be provided a copy of the list where such IUD Kits were distributed.

4. A memorandum receipt should be issued for all recipients, a xerox copy of which be sent to Family Planning Service - Central Office. Standard operating procedures in issuance and retrieval of IUD kits should be observed.

5. The Regional FP coordinator should check the condition of IUD kits every 3 years and provide FPS a duplicate copy of the report as to the status of the instruments.

6. Attached is a copy of allocation per Region.
