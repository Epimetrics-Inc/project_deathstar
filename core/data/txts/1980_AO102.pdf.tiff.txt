TO: The Regional Health Directors, Chiefs of Hospitals and Provincial Health Officers

<subject>SUBJECT: Designation of Officers in Charge in Provincial Health Offices and Hospitals under the Ministry of Health</subject>

<body>
In order to provide a uniform policy in the designation of officers in charge of provincial health offices and hospitals under the Ministry and for the information, guidance and reference of the officials concerned, the following guidelines are hereby issued on the matter:

l. Whenever the Provincial Health Officer or Chief of Hospital in a regional health office has to transact official business outside of his official station or go on leave of absence, the next ranking official shall be recommended for designation as Officer in Charge to the Regional Health Director.

2. When the duration of the absence, whether official or personal, of the Provincial Health Officer or Chief of Hospital is less than fifteen (15) days, the next ranking official shall be designated automatically as Officer in Charge of the Provincial Health Office or Hospital, to perform only routinary duties as provided under Administrative Order No. 96-B dated August 2, 1980. When the period covered by the absence is beyond the above-mentioned period, adequate authority to act on all technical and administrative matters shall be delegated to whoever shall be designated as Officer in Charge of the Provincial Health Office or Hospital by the Regional Health Director.

3. Whenever the next ranking official in the Office is not recommended as Officer in Charge, the Provincial Health Officer or Chief of Hospital shall submit his reasons in writing for the decision of the Regional Health Director.

4. In the event that the Regional Health Director, for some valid reasons cannot act on the recommendation of the Provincial Health Officer or Chief of Hospital, he shall submit the matter immediately, together with his comments, for the decision of the Minister of Health.

5. In the case of retirement of the Provincial Health Officer or Chief of Hospital, or whenever the interest of the service so requires, the Regional Health Director may designate the Officer from among ranking medical officials in other organizational units within the Region.

6. In the case of special hospitals directly under the Office of the Minister, the Minister of Health shall exercise the discretionary authority, as the interest of the service may warrant, of designating the Officer in Charge thereof in the event of extended leave of absence, official travel abroad or retirement of the incumbent.

This Order takes effect upon approval.
