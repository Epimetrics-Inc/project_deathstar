<subject>SUBJECT: Revocation of Bureau Circulars Nos. 01 and 02, series of 1990 and 2004, respectively</subject>

<body>
WHEREAS, Section 11 of Administrative Order No. 85-A, series of 1990, dated February 22, 1990, otherwise known as the "Revised Rules and Regulations Governing Accreditation of Medical Clinics and the conduct of Medical Examination of Overseas Workers and Seafarers", provides that the schedule of fees for pre-employment medical examination shall be prescribed by the Bureau of Licensing and Regulation (currently assigned to the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services [BHFS]).

WHEREAS, the Department of Health (DOH), in the exercise of its supervision and control over the BHFS, has the power and authority to amend, revise, modify, repeal or revoke the latter's prescribed schedule of fees.

NOW THEREFORE, for and in consideration of the foregoing, the Department hereby withdraws, repeals and/or revokes Bureau Circulars Nos. 01 and 02, series of 1990 and 2004, respectively, issued by the BHFS for being contrary to the functions of government regulatory agencies of fixing rates and for not ensuring the quality of pre-employment medical examination of Overseas Filipino Workers.

Hence, all other administrative issuances, bureau circulars, orders, and memoranda related to the aforementioned Bureau Circulars, are hereby withdrawn, repealed and/ revoked accordingly.

This Order shall take effect upon publication in two (2) newspapers of general circulation.
</body>

BUREAU CIRCULAR
No. 1, s. 1990

To : All Medical Clinics and Hospitals accredited to conduct medical examination of overseas contract workers (landbased and seafarers), recruitement agencies/entities, manning agencies, shipping companies and other agencies concerned.

SUBJECT: Revised Standard Medical Examination Fees

As provided for under Section 11 of Administrative Order No. 85-A series 199O which is the "Revised Rules and Regulations Governing accreditation of Medical Clinics and the Conduct of Medical Examination for Overseas Workers and Seafarers" all medical clinics and hospitals are enjoined to follow strictly the herein schedule of fees prescribed by the Bureau of Licensing and Regulation, Department of Health. These fees should be followed in charging the overseas workers and seafarers.

To ensure quality of medical examination rates should not go lower than this schedule. Giving of rebates or charging lower than these prescribed rates shall be considered a violation under Section 25.1 of the roles and regulations which shall be dealt with severely.

The cost of the basic pre-employment medical examination shall be P250.00. It will include the following examinations:

1. Complete physical examination
2. Chest X-ray using plates not smaller than ll" x 14".
3. Complete blood count (CBC) including hemoglobin determination.
4. Blood typing (RED)
5. Stool examination
7. Psychometric evaluation

For the seafarers, in addition to all of the above examination, Ishihara tests for color perception and audiometry, among others are required for specific personnel. These tests have separate rates which should be charged in addition to the basic examination rates.

BUREAU CIRCULAR
No. 02 s. 2004

To: All Medical Clinics and Hospitals accredited to conduct Pre-employment Medical Examinations of Overseas Filipino Workers (Landbased and Seafarers), recruitment agencies, manning agencies, shipping companies and other agencies concerned.

Subject: Reiteration of Bureau Circular No. 1, s. 1990

As provided for under Section 11 of Administrative Order No. 85-A s, 1990, otherwise known as the "Revised Rules and Regulations Governing Accreditation of Medical Clinics and the conduct of Medical Examination of Overseas Workers and Seafarers", all OFW Medical Clinics and Hospitals are enjoined to follow strictly the Schedule of fees prescribed by the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services as stipulated in Bureau Circular No. 1. s. 1990.

To ensure quality of medical examinations, rates should not go lower than the schedule of fees stated in the said Bureau Circular. Giving of REBATES or charging lower than these prescribed rates shall be considered a violation under Section 25.1 of the rules and regulations which shall be dealt with severely.

This shall take effect immediately.
