<subject>SUBJECT: RULES AND REGULATIONS ON GENERIC PRESCRIBING BY GOVERNMENT PHYSICIANS</subject>


<body>     

Pursuant to the provisions of Section 6 (a) and (b) and in relation to Sec. 9 of RA 6675, otherwise known as â€˜he Generics Act of 1988, the following rules and regulations are hereby promulgated for the information and guidance of government physicians in prescribing generic drugs.


SEC. 1 - USE OF GENERIC TERMINOLOGY

All government physicians shall in all cases, use generic terminology or generic names in prescribing drugs and medicines. Same rule shall apply when ordering it in hospital charts, in lieu of prescriptions.


SEC. 2 - ROLE OF BFAD INSPECTORS/FDROs/NDP COMPLIANCE OFFICERS AND OTHER DEPUTIZED OFFICERS

To effectively implement the above-cited provisions, BFAD inspectors, Center for Health Development Food and Drug Regulation Officers, Planning, Policy and Advocacy Division Officers and such other officials as may be deputized by the Secretary of Health are hereby deputized as duly authorized representatives of the Department of Health. For this purpose, these officials are directed to monitor/check compliance with the request set forth by the Generics Act of 1988, and its Implementing Rules and Regulations (IRR) including this Order and that of A.0. 62 s. 1989 among others. Further, the deputized Officers may conduct Investigations of violations of the Generics Act and this Order, and after due notice and hearing recommend the imposition of administrative sanctions to the Secretary of Health.


SEC. 3 - ADMINISTRATIVE SANCTIONS

The Secretary of Health shall have the exclusive authority to impose administrative
sanctions as follows:

(a) for the first conviction, he shall suffer the penalty of reprimand which shall be officially recorded in the appropriate books of the Professional Regulation Commission.

(b) for the Second conviction the penalty of line in the amount of not less than two thousand pesos(P2,000.00) but not exceeding five thousand pesos(P5,000.00) at the discretion of the Court.

(c) for the third conviction the penalty of fine in the amount of not less than five
thousand pesos (P5,000.00) but not exceeding ten thousand pesos (P10,000.00) and suspension of his license to practice his profession for thirty (30) days at the discretion of the Court.  

(d) for the fourth and subsequent convictions, the penalty of fine of not less than ten thousand pesos (P10,000.00) and suspension of his license to practice his profession for one year or longer at the discretion of the Court.


SECTION 4 - REPEALING CLAUSE

All Orders, rules and regulations, administrative issuances or parts thereof which are inconsistent with this Order are hereby repealed or amended accordingly.


SECTION 5- EFFECTIVITY

This Order shall take effect fifteen (15) days after publication in two (2) newspapers of general circulation.
