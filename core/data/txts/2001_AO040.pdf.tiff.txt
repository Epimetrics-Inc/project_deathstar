<subject>SUBJECT: Allowing Drug Manufacturers and Suppliers Without CPR's to Participate In the Bidding of the DOH Central Office.</subject>


<body>

In line with the policy of the government to encourage more bidders to participate and promote a healthy competition among drug suppliers which will eventually result in the lowering of the prices of drugs and medicines, suppliers or manufacturers of drugs and medicines who are duly accredited by the DOH or those who were issued provisional accreditation under A.O. 34, but without the corresponding Certificate of Product Registrations (CPR's) at the time of the bidding may be allowed to participate provided that no Notice of Award shall be given unless the corresponding CPR shall be issued by BFAD to the bidder with the lowest calculated bid.

All participating bidders without the corresponding CPR's shall be required to submit, within Five (5) days after notice that their bid was found to be the lowest bid, all the necessary documentation for the processing of their application for CPR. Otherwise their bid shall be considered non-complying.

After notification by COBAC, BFAD shall be given a non-extendible period of 20 working days within which to process the CPR of the winning bidder declared with the lowest calculated bid.

Any provision of any Order or other regulation of the Department contrary to the foregoing shall be deemed repealed.

This Order shall take effect immediately.

August 20, 2001.

By Authority of the Secretary of Health:
