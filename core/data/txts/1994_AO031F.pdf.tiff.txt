<subject>SUBJECT: Creation of are CHILD AND YOUTH UNIT (CYU) under
the Ofﬁce of the Chief of Staff, Department of Health.</subject>


<body>
       Children and Youth aged ﬁve (5) to twenty-ﬁve (25) have long been the forgotten lot when it comes to health issues and concerns. The Department of Health (DOH), in its effort to establish a people-focused rather than a program-based health agenda, ‘realizes the need to create a coordinating unit that shall be directly responsible for the organization, mobilization and implementation of a wide range of health service delivery package biased towards the children and youth in situation of armed-conﬂict (CSAC), labor force (CL); victims of natural disaster(CVND), and children and youth in especially difﬁcult circumstances (CEDC), irrespective of sex, culture, creed and economic status. For this purpose, the Child and Youth Unit (CYU) is hereby created.

       The CYU shall perform the following roles and functions:

1. Responsible for the organization, mobilization and networking not only of the children and youth as beneﬁciaries, customers and consumers but also of their agencies and organizations and all other agencies and organization relative to children and youth including their facilities and ofﬁces (e. g. hospitals and clinics, etc.) from the national, regional up to the local level

2. Develop and coordinate in consultation with the children and youth representatives, package of health and health-related services that will redound to the development, survival and protection of this sector,

3. Facilitate the development and conduct of research agenda;

4. Document signiﬁcant activities, process and ﬁndings; and

5. Monitor and evaluate the progress of the program and make the necessary reduction and modiﬁcation responsive to the need of children and youth.

       Under this order, all expenses incurred in the conduct of CYU activities shall be charged against the Primary Health Care fund subject to the usual accounting and auditing rules and
regulation.
