<subject>SUBJECT: MAXIMUM ALLOWABLE SERVICE FEES FOR THE PROVISION OF WHOLE BLOOD AND BLOOD COMPONENTS</subject>

<body>

Section 1. Legal Basis

Pursuant to Section 35 of the Implementing Rules and Regulations (Administrative Order No. 9 s. 1995) of (RA 7719 otherwise known as the National Blood Services Act of 1994,) the Department of Health is authorized to set a reasonable service fee for every blood or blood product issued by a licensed or authorized Blood Service Facility. The Blood Service Facility may collect such fees which shall not be greater than the maximum prescribed by the Department of Health.


Section 2. Purpose / Coverage

The maximum allowable service fees for the provision of whole blood and blood products shall be applicable to all Blood Service Facilities in the Philippines.


Section 3. Maximum Allowable Service Fees

The Department of Health hereby sets the following maximum service fees to be collected by Blood Service Facilities, to wit:

Whole Blood (500ml unit) 	P800.00
Packed Red Blood Cells 	P750.00
Other Components		P700. 00
	Platelet Concentrates 
	Fresh frozen Plasma 
	Cryopreciptate
	Cryosupernate


Section 4. General Guidelines

4.1 The above service fees shall be based on the following basic expenses such as:

4.1.1 Donor Recruitment
4.1.2 Blood Collection from voluntary Blood Donors 
4.1.3 Testing for Infectious Agents (Hepatitis B Virus, Malaria Parasite, Treponema Pallidum, HIV, and Hepatitis C Virus 
4.1.4 Component Preparation Costs; and
4.1.5 General/ Administrative Expenses for the preparation of the Blood Products

4.2 No Honorarium Fee shall be given to Blood Donors

4.3 Expenses for Transfusion-Related Procedures like Compatibility Testing, other Blood Banking Procedures and Handling Fee which shall be determined by the Transfusion Facility, are separate from the foregoing basic service fees for the provision of whole blood and blood components.

4.4 Government and PNRC-Blood Service Facilities may set lower rates provided they duly notify the Bureau of Research and Laboratories, Department of Health.


Section 5. Effectivity Clause 

This Order shall take effect immediately and shall supersede other previous related issuances.
