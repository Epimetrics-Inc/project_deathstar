<subject>SUBJECT: General Regu1ations Governing the Prohibition of the Use of Cyclamic Acid and its Salts (B-6,3 Food Additives and Preservatives).</subject>

<body>
B - 6.3.1 Drugs containing cyclamates for non—therapeutic use should be withdrawn by January 1, 1971. For the purpose of this order alone, dietary supplements such as vitamin and mineral combinations should be considered in the category of drugs.

6.3.2 Beverages (carbonated or non—carbonated fruit—flavored drinks), containing cyolamates, including packaged mixes and artificially sweetened foods containing lower levels of cyclamates like dietetic jams, jellies, desserts and ice cream should likewise be withdrawn from the market by September 1, 1970.

6.3.3. Canned fruits and vegetables containing cyclametes shall be phased out of use by September 1, 1970. For the purposes of this order, the terms canned fruits and vegetables shall be taken to mean fruit and vegetable juices, concentrates for lemonade and non—carbonated fruit drinks normally consumed with meals.

These regulations shall take effect immediately after publication in the Official Gazette.
