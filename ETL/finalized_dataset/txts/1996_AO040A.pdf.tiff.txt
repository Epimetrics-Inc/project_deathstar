<subject>SUBJECT: Revising Administrative Order Nos. 88-A s. 1990, 17 s. 1993 and 12 s. 1995 re: Delegation of Authority to certain officials of DOH Central Office and at the different levels of organizational segments of the Department of Health</subject>

<body>

In order to streamline and systematise administrative, procurement and financial operations within the department in view of policy reforms in the health sector as well as the Modification of the Procurement System, authority for certain officials to approve transactions is hereby delegated.

Delegation of authority to certain officials is consistent with the provisions of the following:

1. P.D. 1445 which places on the heads of the government agencies the responsibility to ensure efficiency, economy and effectiveness of agency operations under their supervision and control;

2. E.O. 119 s. 1987 reorganizing the Department of Health which authorizes the Secretary of Health to issue such orders and regulations for the proper implementation of policies;

3. Executive Order 292 otherwise known as the Administrative Code of 1987, which authorizes the department Secretary to delegate authority to officers and employees under the secretary’s direction in accordance with said code;

4. E.O. 301 s. 1987 which decentralized among other matters, action on government negotiated contracts and lease contracts;

5. E.O. 380 s. 1989 which revised the levels of authority on approval of government contracts. 
 
In keep with the authority and responsibility of the Secretary of Health over the Department, the following administrative and financial matters shall be approved by the Secretary of Health:

NATURE OF TRANSACTION

A. ADMINISTRATIVE:

1. All transactions dealing on personnel matters for the following employees and officials:

a. Assistant Secretaries, Undersecretaries and CESOs (Secretary recommends appointment to the President):
b. Officials and employees of the OSEC Proper
c. Medical Pool
d. Doctors to the Barrios
e. Regional Directors/ Assistant Regional Directors
f. Chief of retained Hospitals (Medical Center/ Regional Hospital)
g. Division Chief or Equivalent Chief of Division of Hospital/ Region

These transactions include: 

1.1 Original and Promotions Appointments and reinstatements to positions
1.2 Casual/ Contractual Appointments 
1.3 Notice of Entrance to Duty and Oath Office
1.4  Decisions on Protested Appointments for (a) to (g) including all other personnel
1.5 Notices of Salary Adjustments/ Salary Increase (a, b, c, d) 
1.6 Decisions on Administrative Cases involving penalty of suspension, removal or fine of thirty (30) days or more for (a) to (g) including all other personnel
1.7 Claims for benefits under Section 699 of the Revised Administrative Code
1.8 Permission to teach, exercise profession or engage in business outside office hours
1.9 Application for Sick, Vacation, Maternity, Terminal and Special Privilege Leave as provided by law including commutation of money value of (a), (b) and (e)
1.10 Designation of Officer—in-charge in the absence of (a) and (e)
1.11 Designation of Officer—in-charge in the absence of (f) for a period exceeding fifteen (15) days
1.12 Temporary Detail for a period not exceeding ninety (90) days to other executive agencies
1.13 Reassignment from one major office to another for (a) to (g) including all other personnel 
1.14 Approval of Formal Investigation of Findings and Recommendations on Administrative Complaints for (a) to (g) including all other personnel
1.15 Approval of Official Local Travel to conferences, seminars, and non-degree training programs for more than (7) days but less than one (1) month for (a) to (g) including all other personnel.
1.16 Approval of Official Foreign Travel to conferences, seminars, and non-degree training programs for (a) to (g) including all other personnel.
1.15 Secondment for one (1) year
1.16 Acceptance of Resignation of Personnel
1.17 Dropping from the Roll of Service
1.18 Application for Retirement
1.19 Extension of Services for six (6) months after compulsory retirement.

2. Communications to Regional Health Offices, Retained Hospitals, other executive departments, legislative offices, foreign government or organizations which are policy determining, primarily confidential or have significant implications on national health policies

3. Approval of Department Administrative Issuances per DOH AO No. 32 on Administrative Issuance System

4. Request for Organizational/ Staffing Modification

5. Individual Consultancy Contracts

B. PROCUREMENT 

1. Notice of Award thru Public/ Simplified Bidding in the Central Office involving amounts of over P10 M

2. Notice of Award thru Negotiated and Emergency Purchase involving amounts of P2 M and above

C. FINANCIAL TRANSACTIONS

1. Purchase Orders, Contracts, Letters and Job Orders as a result of Public Bidding in the Central Office involving amounts over P10M

2. Negotiated Contracts of P2M and above to be signed also by two (2) Undersecretaries

3. Emergency Purchase in the Central Office in the amount of P2M and above to be signed by two (2) Undersecretaries

4. Box C of the Disbursement Vouchers in the Central Office which have ceilings corresponding to 1,2 and 3

5. Authority to Render Overtime Services and payment of additional compensation for all personnel in the Central Office and for Retained Hospitals and Regional personnel for more than thirty (30) calendar days

6. Request for release of Congressional Initiatives, Agency Initiatives and Funds needing clearance of authorities concerned.

7. Purely consultancy contracts relating to infrastructure projects, regardless of amount

8. Agency Budget Estimate endorsed by OMS Undersecretary 

For greater efficiency/ effectiveness of economy of transaction/procurement levels, the delegation of authority hereby granted to the respective heads of offices to act on administrative and financial matters related to the their “missing text” functions.

RESPONSIBILITIES:

1. In the exercise of this delegated authority in administrative and financial functions, responsible officials are enjoin to observe with diligent care and always uphold the standards of protecting the integrity of the Department and the primacy of promoting and preserving the health of the community. Adherence to the Department’s priority programs shall be demonstrated in undertaking the responsibility.

2. The persons/officials to whom this authority has been delegated are primarily responsible and accountable for any violation of existing budget, accounting auditing and other applicable rules and regulations.

3. The exercise of powers herein provided shall in all cases be subjected to such existing department policies as may have been or may be promulgated.

* All personnel actions and transactions under the delegated authority shall be furnished a copy to the Personnel Division of the Administrative Service

This supersedes all other Orders on Delegation of Authority and all other Orders inconsistent herewith.
