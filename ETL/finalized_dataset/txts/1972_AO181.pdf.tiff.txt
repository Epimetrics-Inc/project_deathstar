1. The proper directions of the medical care of patients in government hospitals is the responsibility of the medical staff of that institution. For this reason, only members of the medical staff and those authorized be use the hospital facilities. In accordance with the Medical staff by-laws and hospital rules and regulations should be allowed to direct the course of management of a patient with the assistance of physicians on training. <missingtext> </missingtext> rule should apply to medicare patients.

2. The privilage given to salaried physicians employee by the Department of Health to attend to medicare cases and to collect professional fees authorized under the provisions of Republic Act No. 6111 is subject to the conditions set forth <missingtext> </missingtext> and the requirements prescribed under existing laws, rules and regulations.

a. The physician has been specifically chosen as the attending physician by the medicare patient who is admitted in a government hospital.

b. The physician has been granted the privilege to use the facilities of the government hospital concerned in accordance with the Medical staff by-laws and subject to the hospital rules and regulations. such privilege being based mainly on accreditation to practice specific medical and/or surgical procedures.

c. For full-time salaried physicians, the professional fees that could be charged medicare patients should be in accordance with the schedule of professional fees prescribed by the GSIS and SSS and approved by the Philippine Medical CAre Commission pursuant to the provisions of Republic Act No. 6111;

d. The performance of the regular duties for which the physician has been employed by the Department of Health should not be adversely affected, and that in case of abuse of the privilage or when the interest of public service so demands, upon recommendation of the Regional Health Director concerned, the privilage authorized in this Order may be suspended or revoked;
