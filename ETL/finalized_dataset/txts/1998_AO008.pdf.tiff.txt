1. BACKGROUND and COVERAGE

Pursuant to Sec 22 (1) of Executive Order No. 119 dated January 30, 1987, the Administrative Service of the Department of Health was reorganized and mandated to establish a Division of Medical Examination and Department Infirmary (MEDI). MEDI was  subsequently created a Division under the said Service and was tasked to provide medical services, especially laboratory examinations and diagnostic procedures to the following: 

a. DOH Central Office officials, employees and their dependents;

b. Candidates, awardees or grantees (of fellowships, scholarships, study or research grants to be undertaken in foreign host countries) who are referred by the National Economic Development Authority ( NEDA;

c. Local employment applicants or workers referrred by the Department of Labor and Employment ( DOLE ) and Bureau of Apprenticeship;

d. Employees of Government agencies for purposes of employment, annual check-up, reinstatement or retirement;

e. Government Service Insurance Corporation ( GSIS ) for insurance and re- insurance requirements;

f. Applicants for professional regulation examinations referred by the Civil Service Commission ( CSC );

g. Foreigners or applicants referred by the Bureau of Immigration and Deportation (BID) for naturalization and immigration purposes; 

h. Applicant for foster parent adoption, tenement occupancy, or resettlement referred by the Department of Social Worker and Development ( DSWD ) for  fitness evaluation; and

i. Persons referred by the Office of the Secretary (OSEC).


II. PURPOSE and RATIONALE

This administrative order is issued to set fees and charges for laboratory examinations and diagnostic procedures performed by the Division of Medical Examination and Department Infirmary (MEDI). This revenue enhancement measure aims to improve the diagnostic services and facilities of the Division.

Based on quality control consideration and the current costs of reagents, supplies and materials, fees set by this order are within the range of prevailing rates charged by the public health laboratories and are therefore considered reasonable.
