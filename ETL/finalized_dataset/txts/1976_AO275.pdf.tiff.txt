TO: The Regional Health Directors, Chiefs of Agencies, and Training and Teaching Hospitals

<subject>SUBJECT: Rules and Regulations Governing Affiliation of Midwifery Schools in Government Hospitals under the Department of Health.</subject>

<body>
The Department of Health believes in adequately prepared midwives for service. Aware of the importance of maintaining the safety and welfare of patients committed to their care, the designated training and teaching hospitals offer their facilities for the desired clinical experience of students provided the following rules and regulations are strictly observed and complied with:

I. Responsibilities of the Department of Health Hospitals

Training and teaching hospitals designated by the Secretary of Health where midwifery students may affiliate to obtain the desired clinical experience must meet the following requirements:

1. Be a general or special hospital with not less than 100 bed capacity and an average bed occupancy of not less than 80%.

2. Follow the ratio of one (1) midwifery student affiliate to at least every:

a. Five (5) obstetrical cases
b. Five (5) nursery newborm

3. a. Midwifery affiliation shall be allowed only in the morning and afternoon shifts.

b. Night shift affiliation shall be allowed only in the delivery room, nursery and labor room. Midwifery students assigned in these areas shall be accompanied by their corresponding clinical instructors. The number of midwifery student affiliates assigned to the above-mentioned areas will be declared on by the Director of the hospital in order to avoid unnecessary congestion in these critical areas.

4. Midwifery student affiliates allowed in the pre-natal out-patient unit and in the Family Planning Clinic shall be limited to only five (5) students in each shift.

5. Require affiliating schools of midwifery to assign one (1) clinical instructor coming from their school to proctor every twelve (12) midwifery student affiliates per shift. Clinical instructors must be in complete prescribed uniform.

6. a. Formulate the general plan for the training of the midwifery student affiliates in the hospital before the actual start of the school year.

b. Meet with the principals of affiliating midwifery schools sufficiently ahead of the start of the school year and shall schedule meetings with the principal as often as deemed necessary by the hospital, in order to ensure the effective implementation of the affiliation rules of the Department of Health for the upgrading of the standard of midwifery education.

7. a. Have a well organized and administered nursing service with qualified and adequate nursing personnel.

b. Orient the clinical instructors coming from the midwifery schools for not less than two (2) weeks and not more than four (4) weeks. Orientation must be given before the actual period of affiliation starts.

8. Have an adequate, well-maintained physical plant with the provisioln of necessary equipment and supplies including:

a. A well-equipped central supply service
b. A well-equipped main conference room
c. Library
d. Nursing training office
e. Suitable area for small group conferences in the wards

9. Have a nursing training staff responsible for the training educational program on the nursing personnel of the hospital including the orientation of new clinical instructors of the affiliating midwifery schools.

10. Have a suitable dining space for the faculty and students while on clinical experience in the hospital upon proper arrangement between the school and the hospital authorities.

Hospital authorities reserve the right and have the duty to suspend or cancel affiliation privileges of any school when the school authorities and/or their respective students have not complied with the requirements of the contract of affiliation; and/or have violated the rules and regulations set; and/or the affiliating students have repeatedly committed errors detrimental to the health, safety and welfare of the patients. The final decision in case any appeal is made by affiliating schools will be made, however, by the Secretary of Health, through the Affiliation Committee.

Department of Health hospitals should enforce the rules and regulations set by this Administrative Order in accepting new midwifery schools for affiliation and in renewing existing contracts with affiliating schools for the next school year.

Hospital authorities reserve the right to assign the midwifery student affiliates to specific areas of the hospitals where they could be trained effectively.

Hospital authorities shall not allow midwifery student affiliates to enter the training areas in the hospital without their accompanying clinical instructors from the midwifery schools. Absence of clinical instructors or a lack in the approved number of clinical instructors must be reported by the hospital to the school concerned and to the Secretary of Health through the Accreditation Committee on Training and Student Affiliation. These reports shall be compiled by the Department of Health and submitted to the Department of Education and Culture for appropriate action on the midwifery school concerned.

Any hospital that operates its own school of midwifery should not allow any affiliation from private schools except when its school of midwifery does not have enough or sufficient number of students to adequately fulfill the ratio of one (1) midwifery student per five (5) obstetrical patients.

Quarterly reports on the affiliation fees received showing the percentage of each category of personnel and the budgetary outlay showing the proposed amount for each personnel participating in the affiliation program must be submitted to the Secretary of Health thru the Accreditation Committee together with the narrative report on the status and/or problems of the Affiliation Program.

II. Responsibilities of the Affiliating School of Midwifery

All schools of midwifery must have met the following requirements before applying for the use of clinical facilities of a general training/teaching hospital of the Department of Health:

a. Approval from the Department of Education and Culture
b. Compliance wutht the provisions of the Midwifery Law R.A. 2644

These schools shall:

1. Provide qualified clinical instructors to guide, teach, supervise and evaluate students' clinical experience in the training/teaching hospitals in accordance with the ratio of one (1) clinical instructor per twelve (12) students. No student shall be sent to the clinical area without the clinical instructor from the affiliating school. This includes midwifery students affiliating during the night shift in the delivery room and nursery room.

2. Pay or replace damages, breakages or losses by the faculty and students of hospital property, equipment and supplies.

3. Enforce that the clinical practice of each student be set at a minimum of 20 hours per week.

4. See to it that all midwifery students before beginning their clinical practive in a particular clinical area must have satisfactorily completed the theoretical aspect of that clinical area.

5. Pay the hospital ten pesos (₱10.00) for each student per month. Payment is due within the first five days of each month. An increase in the affiliation fee to twenty pesos (₱20.00) per student per month will be incorporated with the budget for the next fiscal year.

6. Make a request for new affiliation to the Chief of Hospital accomplishing the appropriate standard form of the Contract of Affiliation (6 copies) and coursed through proper channels to the Secretary of Health through the Chairman, Accreditation Committee on Training and Student Affiliation. Contracts shall be subject for renewal every school year by the Chief of Hospital.

7. See to it that the instructors and students observe and abide with the policies, rules and regulations of the hospital and that professional discipline is maintained at all times.

8. See to it that their instructors are in their proper uniform when supervising midwifery student affiliates in the hospital.

III. Joint responsibilities of Affiliating School of Midwifery and the Training and Teaching Hospitals

1. The clinical program of midwifery students shall be the joint responsibility of the nursing training staff of the hospital and the clinical instructors of each respective school. Assignments and proper rotation of students for each clinical area shall be prepared in accordance with the aforementioned rules. When there are two or more schools affiliating in a hospital, the assignment of students in a clinical area may be on a two-shift basis.

2. Holding of joint conferences with the authorities of the hospital and the school for the proper implementation, evaluation and improvement of the program.

3. Promotion of cooperation and harmonious working relationships among the teaching/training personnel and school personnel. Periodic dialogue between the Nursing Service, Training Staff and the Midwifery School should be held in order to entertain suggestions and thresh out problems in relation with the clinical experience program for the benefit of the patients and the students.

IV. Special Provision

1. All schools of midwifery under the Department of Health affiliating with other teaching and training hospitals of the Department of Health shall be governed by the above rules and regulations except those that pertain to the payment of affiliation fees and the signing of the appropriate Contract of Affiliation.

This Administrative Order supersedes all issuances on this matter and shall take effect immediately.
