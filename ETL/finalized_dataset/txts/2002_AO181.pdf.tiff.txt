I. LEGAL BASIS

Pursuant to Section 35 of the Implementing Rules and Regulation (Administrative Order No. 9 s. 1995) of RA 7719 otherwise known as the "National Blood Services Act of 1994", the Department of Health is authorized to set reasonable service fee for every blood or blood products issued by a licensed or authorized Blood Service Facility. The Blood Service Facility may collect such fees which shall not be greater than the maximum prescribed by the Department of Health.

After a comprehensive study on the actual costs incurred in the collection and testing of blood units specifically considering the prevailing cost of the reagents used by the Blood Service Facilities (BSF), the National Voluntary Blood Services Program (NVBSP) Technical Committee has come up with the new maximum allowable blood service fees.

II. PURPOSE/COVERAGE

The revised maximum allowable service fees for the provision of whole blood and blood products shall be applicable to all Blood Service Facilities in the Philippines.

IV. GENERAL GUIDELINES

4.1. The above revised service fees shall be based on the following basic expenses, such as:

4.1.1. Donor recruitment
4.1.2. Blood Collection from Voluntary Blood Donors
4.1.3. Testing for Infectious Agents:
Hepatitis B Virus (by EIA, Chemiluminescence, or Particle Agglutination);
Hepatitis C Virus (by EIA, Chemiluminescence, or Particle Agglutination);
HIV (by EIA, Chemiluminescence, or Particle Agglutination);
Malaria Parasite (by thick and thin blood smear or Quantitative Buffy Coat Method)
Treponema Pallidum (by Rapid Plasma Reagent (RPR) or Treponema Pallidum Hemagglutination Test).
4.1.4. Component preparation costs; and
4.1.5. General/Administrative expenses for the preparation of the blood products.

4.2. No honorarium fee shall be given to blood donors.
4.3. Expenses for Transfusion- related procedUres, and handling fee which shall be determined by the Transfusion Facility, are separate from the foregoing basic service fees for the provision of whole blood and blood components. These expenses shall be charged or indicated on separate receipts.
4.4. Government and PNRC-Blood Service Facilities may set lower rates provided they duly notify the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services, Department of Health.
4.5. Non-replacement fee or Blood Bond for blood should not be collected from the patient/relatives of the patient.

V. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS

Violations according to RA 7719 (National Blood Services Act of 1994) Section 12. Penalties; and under the Implementing Rules and Regulations (Chapter X. Section 47. Penalties) states that, upon complaint of any person and after due notice and hearing, any blood bank/center which shall collect charges and fees greater than the maximum prescribed by the Department shall have its license suspended or revoked by the Secretary.

VI. REPEALING CLAUSE

This Order supersedes other previous related orders and Issuances inconsistent with this Order.

VII. EFFECTIVITY CLAUSE

This order shall take effect immediately.
