<doctype>ADMINISTRATIVE ORDER</doctype>
<docnum>No. 301 s. 1976</docnum>

TO: Regional Health Directors, Acting Regional Health Directors and Officers-in-Charge of Regional Health Offices, duly designated by the Secretary of Health.

<subject>SUBJECT: Delegation of authority to create a Regional Committee on Evaluation and Protests.</subject>

<body>
For purposes of expediting the resolution of protest cases against appointments to first level positions up to R-50, whether original, promotional or by reinstatement in nature, issued by the Regional/Acting Regional Health Directors and Officers—in—Charge of regional health offices, pursuant to the delegation of authority granted them over these appointments in accordance with Letter of Instructions No. 448 dated August 18, 1976, the said regional officials are hereby delegated the authority to create a Regional Committee on Evaluation and Protests in their respective regions to receive, process and decide such protests. This Committee shall be composed of:

(l) The Director/Acting Director/Officer—in—Charge of the regional health office, or his duly designated representative;
(2) The Administrative Officer III or the Personnel Officer II; and
(3) The Legal Officer III.

The Regional Committee on Evaluation and Protests shall meet whenever necessary, and shall keep and maintain an orderly record and files of its deliberations on all evaluation and protest cases. Actions on protest cases shall be in accordance with the pertinent regulations on contested appointments as embodied in Civil Service MC No. 13, s. 1963, and with Sec. 10 of Rule V (Promotion), Rules on Personnel Actions and Policies. The following guidelines shall likewise be observed in taking actions thereon:

(l) A fair and just comparative evaluation of the parties' (the contenders', or the protestant's and the protestee's) qualifications (educational and academic preparation; civil service eligibility; in—service training, seminars, special studies, grants, fellowships, awards; training and experience; service record) and performance as borne by facts and creditable records shall be made by the Committee and presented in tabulated form for easy reference.

(2) Other relevant factors like potential for higher level of duties, physical fitness for the work involved, attitude and behavior of the parties concerned, shall also be duly considered in the evaluation.

(3) A definite stand shall be taken by the Committee in deciding the case, and only those protests which cannot be resolved at the regional level shall be submitted to the Department for adjudication.

(4) Upon resolution of the case, the parties concerned shall be served immediately with the notice of decision, receipt of which to be properly acknowledged in writing by the said parties.

Nothing in this Order shall be construed as limiting the authority of the Secretary of Health to reverse any decision that may be found erroneous upon review when necessary.

This Order shall take effect immediately.
