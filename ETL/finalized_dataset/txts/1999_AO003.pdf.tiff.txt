<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines and Criteria for Selection of Appointees for the positions, Chief of Hospital and ChiefS of major divisions in DOH Hospitals.</subject>

I. Rationale

The Health Care delivery system like any other system is made up of interdependent an interrelated components. To be able to effectively and efficiently function as a whole, it would require each and every component to function to the optimum. In the DOH Hospital System the hospital as vital component of the whole structure are mandated to provide an equally major output in health care delivery. Crucial to an effective health care services are competent and skilled managers to implement the plans and programs of the agency.

The Chief of Hospital, Administrative Officer, Chief Nurse and Chief of Professional Medical Service are positions of management level and as such are positions of public trust. Appointees for these positions should surpass the qualifications set by the Civil Service Commission (CSC) and meet the criteria set by the Department of Health (DOH). This set of criteria shall be the basis for the selection of appointees for the mentioned positions in order to have quality
leaders and managers in DOH hospitals.

A set of qualification standard comprising 100% as the total rate has been formulated by the Department of Health in the selection of qualified appointees for the positions. This serves as a set of qualification standards that is of a higher level than that of the Civil Service Commission’s basic qualification standard.


II. Criteria for the Selection of Appointees for the Positions.

1. The Civil Service Requirements: 30 points

1. Educational Requirements 	- 10 pts. 
2. Experience Requirements 	- 10 pts.
3. Civil Service Eligibility 	- 5 pts.
4. Training Requirements 	- 5 pts.

Please refer to Annex - A

2. Vital Standards and Qualities: 70 points 

1. Leadership Qualities: 20 pts.

A. Visionary Leadership: (10 pts)

This refers to the ability to set ambitious but achievable directions for the unit/division/office consistent with the Department of Health and the hospital’s commitment to the delivery of quality health services. 

It refers to the ability to truly inspire others, specially the staff to apply all their talents and energies towards achieving the DOH and institutional vision and goal with enthusiasm, creativity and hospital persistence.

B. Ability for Resource generation: (10 pts.)

This is a cluster of related criteria which indicate potential traits to support the hospital organization in achieving the goal.

a) Having a wide network of connections (individuals, organizations, institution, funding agencies) that can be tapped for technical and final resources.

b) An effective public relations skills to promote DOH and hospital programs and projects.

c) Good links with local government units, other government line agencies, national and international organizations.

2) Personal Integrity: 15 pts.

An individual who is highly credible as a “person because he/ she is honest. morally upright, apolitical, a person with deep faith (make-Diyos) and maka-tao. 

3) Recognized Technical Expertise: 15 pts.

With technical expertise in the management planning and leadership in the medical/nursing/administrative services of a tertiary hospital. 

4) “Malasakit”: 10 pts. 

a) With an abiding sense of commitment to the DOH expressed in a willingness to devote full-time attention to work.

b) to entail personal sacrifice and stretch oneself for the good of the DOH and the hospital.

c) Genuine concern for the welfare of the staff expressed in persistent efforts to improve working conditions and performance rewards, upholding fairness at all levels, and a willingness to be accessible to the rank and file.

5) Familiarity with the Department of Health’s Organization: 10 pts.

Who has been connected in some substantive way to the DOH (either as a staff, head or consultant). He/She will be in a better position to appreciate and promote the DOH and the hospital’s distinctive mandate, approach and organizational culture: appreciate the constraints unique to the hospital and how these constraints need to/can be changed. 


III. This Order shall take effect on January 21, 1999.

All other issuances inconsistent with this Administrative Order are hereby rescinded.
