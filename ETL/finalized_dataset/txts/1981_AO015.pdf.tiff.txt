<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines and policies regarding patients admission in hospitals.</subject>

<body>
For the proper and uniform implementation of Admission of patients in hospitals, the following procedures shall be observed by all concerned:

I. Categorization of Patients:

Class A - Private Pay - All charges. This class refers to those affluent group of patients who occupy the suites, private and semi-private rooms.

Class B - Pay and Medicare Pay - those patients who can pay on the ward level (4 beds and above) and those who can pay more than the Medicare benefits that they are entitled, that is, they can pay the excess based on the Medicare fees.

Class C - Service Medicare, Partial Donation - those patients who depend solely on the Medicare benefits they are entitled to, they cannot afford to pay excess, but can afford to give some donations.

Class D - Complete indigent - patients who are totally incapable of paying, so everything is free.

II. 1. A patient upon admission maybe accommodated in any of the above categories and later maybe moved to any of the other category after being classified within 72 hours.

2. No hospital may hold the discharge of any patient if they cannot pay (unauthorized detention).

3. The patient can execute promissory notes relative to their hospital bills. However, if they do not pay as promised, they must be notified in writing for at least three (3) times. If no favorable response is received within the above-stipulated time, the Chief of Hospital should institute the necessary legal proceedings with the court of proper jurisdiction for recovery of money in order not to be held liable for such account.

This Order shall take effect immediately.
