<subject>SUBJECT: ADDENDUM TO MANUAL OF OPERATION OF RHEUMATIC FEVER/RHEUMATIC HEART DISEASE (RF/RHD); GUIDELINES ON THE REFERRAL, CONFIRMATION, DIAGNOSIS, REGISTRATION AND MANAGEMENT OF RF-RHD CASES.</subject>

<body>


1. All RF-RHD suspects from the RHUs/District Hospitals and schools shall be referred as soon as possible for confirmation at the Referral Centers (City or Provincial Hospitals/Regional Hospitals/Medical Centers/participating Private Hospitals).


2. Confirmation shall be done as soon as possible by:

a. Private Medical Center Cardiologist or Pediatrician

b. City or Provincial Hospital/Regional Hospital or Medical Center: by a DOH consultant (cardiologist or pediatrician)

In the absence of a DOH consultant, volunteer cardiologists (from the Philippine Heart Association) or pediatricians (from the Philippine Pediatric Society) within the geographic areas shall be available every last Thursday of the month to do the confirmation of cases in the Referral Centers.


3. Diagnostic tests shall be done at the Referral Centers

- Regional Field Health Office shall purchase, allocate/distribute ASO titer to City/provincial and regional hospitals/medical centers

- For confirmation of cases, a thorough physical examination with or without ASO titer testing shall be done


4. Registration of cases shall be done at the Referral Centers (also known as Registry Centers)

- Form A (initial registration form) shall be properly filled up in triplicate
- Form B (patient record card) shall be issued to the patient 
- Form C (patient data form) shall be kept at the Referral/Registry Centers
- Form D (annual follow-up form) shall be kept at the Referral/Registry Centers


5. Management of cases shall likewise be done at the Referral/Registry Centers

a. Benzathine Penicillin shall be administered every 21-28 days preferably at the Regional or Provincial Hospitals. (Refer to Manual of Operations for specific dosages, pg.19)

b. Benzathine Penicillin shall be given free to indigent patients (government or private) with confirmed RF/RHD.

c. Drug allergy shall ruled out by a thorough personal history; in doubtful cases, skin testing may be done.

d. Skin testing for penicillin shall be done only before the initial dose is given.

e. Erythromycin shall be prescribed for patients with allergy to Benzathine Penicillin. (Refer to Manual of Operations for specific dosages)

f. In case a patientâ€™s residence is a far flung area, buffer stock good tor six months may be sent to the Referring Unit (District Hospital or RHU).

g. Compliance of Benzathine Penicillin injection shall be monitored by RHU personnel and/or barangay health worker with a quarterly report to the registry center.
