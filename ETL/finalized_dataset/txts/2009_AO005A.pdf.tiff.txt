<body>
The Department of Health issued Administrative Order (A0) No. 2009-0005 dated January 12, 2009 to regulate toys sold or given free in the Philippines. However, to ensure that the toys used by Filipino children are safe, A0 2009- 0005 is hereby amended to include the following guidelines on the regulation of phthalates: 

A. Prohibition on the sale of certain products containing phthalates“ 

It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture for sale, ofﬂer for sale, distribute in commerce, or import into the country any children’s toy that contains concentrations of more than 0.1 percent of di-(2 -ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP), dibutylphthalate (DBP) or benzyl butylphthalate (BBP) 

B. Prohibition on the sale of additional products containing-certain phthalates* 

It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture for sale, offer for sale, distribute in commerce or import into the country any children’ s toy that can be placed In a child s mouth that contains concentrations of more than 0.1 percent of diisononyl phthalate (DINP), diisodecyl phthalate (DIDP) or di-n-octyl phthalate (DnOP).

This Order shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication 1n an ofﬁcial gazette or - in a newspaper of general circulation.
