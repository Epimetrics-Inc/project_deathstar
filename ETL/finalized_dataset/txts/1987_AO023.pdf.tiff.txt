<subject>Subject: Guidelines in the utilization of the P74 million fund/ donation from the Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR) to be availed of in the construction, equipment and facilities of DOH agencies.</subject>

<body>
1. A total of P74 million special funding from PAGOR was recently approved by its Board of Directors for the construction, purchase of equipment and supplies and improvement of facilities of the following DOH agencies.
<image>table_1.png</image>
 

2. Separate checks issued by PAGCOR to the beneficiary agency shall be treated as a Trust Liability of the agency concerned and deposited in an authorized government depository bank, pursuant to Section 4, General Provisions, Executive Order No. 87.

3. The Medical Center chiefs/Chiefs of Hospital concerned in consultation with their staff shall determine their priorities and activities and translate these into budgetary estimates, together with their justifications, made into a proposal for projects utilizing funding. Proposed priorities and activities should be geared towards enhancing the referral network within their catchment area and improving efficiency of services.
4. This proposal shall be endorsed by the respective Regional Health Office for submission to the Office of the Secretary through the Chief of Staff for review and approval. Formal approval shall be issued and whatever modification necessary shall be indicated.
Construction/procurement can only be made on the basis of the approval by the Secretary of Health or the Chief of Staff.

5. Competitive public bidding shall be the only mode of acquisition. All rules and regulations governing procurement and contracts will apply.

6. Withdrawals from the depository bank .in payment of claims will only be upon the joint signature of the authorized representative of the agency and the representative of the Commission on Audit, without the need for a special budget or the issuance of a Cash Disbursement Ceiling.

7. If the amount is deposited in a savings account, the interest thereon shall accrue to the General Fund and shall be remitted to the National Treasury at the end of each quarter.

8. Upon completion of proposed projects utilizing this funding, whatever balance outstanding may be used to fund additional proposed projects provided these are also approved by the Secretary through the Chief of Staff.

9. Status of implementation of approved projects shall be reported every quarter until the funding is exhausted.
