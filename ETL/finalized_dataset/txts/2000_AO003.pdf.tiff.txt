<subject>SUBJECT: Hiring, Distribution and Utilization of Medical Pool Items of the Department of Health</subject>

<body>

In line with the thrust of the Department of Health to strengthen the regional and devolved health facilities, coupled with its drive towards quality health care delivery, Medical Pool items shall be programmed in response to the changing needs and priorities of the country.


I. OBJECTIVES

A. General Objective:

To provide the country with adequate and competent Medical Human Resource who will render quality medical care to patients.

B. Specific Objectives:

1. To provide replacements for Provincial and District Hospitals who are sending their service residents for training.

2. To augment the medical human resource needed in hospitals.



II. GENERAL GUIDELINES


A. Qualification Standards

1. General Requirements

a. Must be a Filipino Citizen
b. R.A. 1080 Board Certiﬁed
c. Justification/s for the item requested coming from the requesting hospital

2. Additional requirements for

2.1 Medical Specialist Pool Item

a. Fellow/Diplomate of the relevant accredited specialty society.

b. A project proposal along the line of specialty, in accordance with the hospital development plan, in which it will be implemented.

c. Indorsement by the Selection and Promotions Board upon the recommendations of the Hospital Medical Credentials Committee.

d. Recommending approval by the Chief of Hospital to the Secretary of Health.

2.2 Medical Officer Pool item

a. Certificate of QUERT eligibility.


B. Hiring and Selection Process

1. Medical Specialist II

a. Applicants shall apply directly to the hospital concerned.

h. Applicants in DOH hospitals in coordination with the Chief of Hospital should be assigned in a provincial/district hospital for development where the applicant shall render his/.her services on a regular basis.

c. The Hospital Selection and Promotions Board shall conduct an initial evaluation of all applicants to the position based on the Qualification Standards of the position, specialty needs and overall hospital development plan of the health facility which the applicant is to serve.

d. Selected applicant shall be recommended by the Chief of Hospital to the Chairman, Medical Pool Committee for final evaluation.

e. The Medical Pool Committee composed of the Health Manpower Development and Training Service (HMDTS), Hospital Operations and Management Service (HOMS) and the Personnel Division, Administrative Service shall conduct the final evaluation based on the existing criteria and will recommend to the Secretary of Health for appointment. 

2. Medical Officer III

a. Applicants shall submit their application to the Secretariat of the Medical Pool Committee.

b. The Medical Pool Committee shall conduct an evaluation based on Qualification Standards of the Position and on the needs of provincial and district hospitals.

c. Selected applicant shall be recommended to the Secretary of Health by the Chairman of the Medical Pool Committee.

d. Upon approval of the appointment, a Department Order shall be issued by the Personnel Division, Administrative Service relevant to their place of assignment. 


C. Nature of the Appointment

Appointments to the positions of Medical Specialist II and Medical Officer III under the Office of the Secretary shall be TEMPORARY in nature with the following period limits:

1. Medical Specialist II

a. Renewable yearly for a maximum of three (3) years.
b. Renewal shall be based on a satisfactory performance.
c. Measures have to be made to provide them regular hospital items within three (3) years from date of initial appointment to a pool item. 

2. Medical Officer III for Service

a. Renewable yearly corresponding to the length of the residency training program of the doctor being replaced.
b. Renewal shall be based on a satisfactory performance.
c. Can be terminated upon the recommendation of the Chief of Hospital.

IV. BASIS FDR DISTRIBUTING/ALLOCATING AND RE-ALLOCATING MEDICAL POOL ITEMS

1. On geographical location: Far flung or hard to reach areas in the catchment of the DDH hospitals as determined by Regional Directors and approved by the EXECOM.

2. On Hospital Development Plan: Hospital Development Plan of the health facility concerned in consonance with the National Hospital Development Plan.

3. On Percent Distribution:

a. Medical Specialist II

i. Seventy percent (70%) of the total Medical Specialist II pool items shall be allotted to DOH
hospitals who have far flung or hard to reach provinces/districts in their catchment areas needing specialists as determined by the Regional Directors and approved by the EXECOM

ii. Thirty percent (30%) shall be allotted for District and Provincial Hospital for development.

b. Medical Officer III

i. All Medical Officer III items shall be allotted to  District I Provincial Hospitals to replace service residents who are on residency training. Replacement shall not cover sub—specialty program.



V. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

A. Administrative Service: All original/reemployment appointments shall be processed at the Personnel Division, Administrative Service.

B. HMDTS: Shall be in charge of allocating/re-allocating the Medical Pool items according to the changing needs and priorities of the country.

C. Regional Office: Renewal of Medical Pool Appointments shall be done at the Regional level subject to the provision of C.1.a and C.2.a.

D. Medical Pool Committee: Members of the committee shall have the following functions

1. Validate and evaluate applications and other pertinent documents of applicants doe original appointments to the medical pool positions based on the national distribution scheme of medical pool items.

2. Evaluate the need of the hospitals specified at the applications for medical pool items.

3. Make recommendations for original appointments of qualified and needed applicants and submit the same to the Office for Hospitals and Facilities Services for indorsement to the Secretary of Health.

VI. EFFECTIVITY

This Order shall take effect upon approval.

Existing issuance related to the subject and all other Orders inconsistent with this Administrative Order are hereby rescinded.
