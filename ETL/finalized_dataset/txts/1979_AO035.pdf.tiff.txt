Section 1. SCOPE - This Merit Promotion Plan shall cover only positions in the career and non-career service.

Section 2. DEFINITION OF TERMS

As used in this Order, the following terms shall mean:

2.1 Agency - region, bureau, office, service, project, hospital, board, council directly under or attached to the Ministry of Health.

2.2 Appropriate Civil Service Eligibility - the eligibility resulting from a civil service examination given by the Commission, or with its assistance or collaboration, or which by specific provisions of law/decree or by declaration of the Commission is deemed suitable for the position to be filled.

2.3 Career Service - category of service in the Philippine Civil Service characterized by entrance to positions based on merit and fitness to be determined as far as practicable by competitive examinations, or based on highly technical qualification, opportunity for advancement to higher career positions, and security.

2.4 Commission - refers to the Civil Service Commission.

2.5 First Level - includes clerical, trades, crafts and custodial service positions which involve non-professional or sub-professional work in non-supervisory or supervisory capacity <missingtext> </missingtext>

2.6 Second Level - includes professionals, technical and scientific positions which involve professional technical and scientific work in a non-supervisory or supervisory capacity requiring at least four years of college work.

2.7 Next-in-Rank Position - refers to a position which by reason of the hierarchical arrangement of positions in the Ministry or Agency, is the nearest degree of relationship to a higher position, taking into account the following: (1) organization structure/s as reflected in the approved organization chart/s: (2) classification and/or functional relationship; (3) salary and/or range allocation and (4) geographical location.

2.8 Occupational Group - a series of classes or positions in the same occupation or occupational areas and arranged by level of difficulty and responsibility.

2.9 Merit Promotion - a systematic method of selecting personnel for advancement or promotion on the basis of their qualifications, fitness and ability to perform the duties and assume the responsibilities of the position being filled.

2.10 Promotion - the advancement of an employee from one position to another with an increase in duties and responsibilities as authorized by law and usually accompanied by an increase in salary.

Promotion may be from one ministry or agency to another, or from one organizational unit to another in the same ministry or agency.

2.11 Promotional Line-Up - a listing of incumbents of positions next-in-rank containing all necessary information about such incumbents.

2.12 Qualification Standards - express the minimum requirements for a class of positions in terms of education, training and experience, civil service eligibility, physical charac- <missingtext> </missingtext>

<missingtext> </missingtext> successful performance of the duties and responsibilities of a position.

Section 3. POLICIES

3.1 On Promotion

3.1.1 In the selection of personnel for the ministry or agency, the appointing authority shall be given a wide latitude of discretion, in the exercise of which, however, he shall be guided by the Civil Service Law and rules, and the policy guidelines on promotion embodied in this Administrative Order. Provided, he shall not be precluded from appointing employees who possess special qualifications not found among those within the organization.

3.1.2 Whenever a position in the first level or second level becomes vacant or is newly created, it shall be filled by promotion, except:

- the lowest position in an occupational group which, in the exercise of sound discretion of the appointing authority, may be filled by an appointment of a person not in the government service who is competent, qualified and who possesses an appropriate civil service eligibility; and

- positions which cannot be filled by promotion due to the absence of qualified employees in the Ministry or Agency.

3.1.3 Whenever a position in the first level or second level becomes vacant, the employees in the Ministry or Agency who occupy positions deemed to be next-in-rank to the vacancy and who are competent, qualified and with the appropriate civil service eligibility shall be considered for promotion.

3.1.4 Preference shall be given to the next-in-rank employee who is the most competent and qualified and who has the appropriate civil service eligibility. In cases where all the next-in-rank employees meet the requisites for the positions, preference shall be given to the employee in the organizational unit where the vacancy occurs, or should there be none, in other agencies or offices in the MOH.

3.1.5 Whenever a position in the second level becomes vacant and incumbents of next-in-rank positions are deemed not qualified for appointment to the vacancy, incumbents of first or second level positions who are competent, qualified, and who possess the appropriate civil service eligibility may be considered for promotion to the second level position although their positions are not next-in-rank.

3.1.6 Positions which cannot be filled by promotion due to the absence of qualified employees in the Ministry or agency, shall be filled by transfer of present employees in the government service, by reinstatement, by reemployment of persons separated through reduction in force or by appointment of persons who are qualified and possess the civil service eligibility for the position.

3.1.7 In determining the degree of competence or qualifications of an employee, performance shall be given more weight to make merit more meaningful. No employee shall be considered for promotion unless his performance is at least satisfactory.

3.1.8 Lack of confidence and the mere filing of an administrative charge against an employee shall not constitute disqualifications of an employee for promotion.

3.1.8.1 The special reason must be real and substantial to be considered for disqualification.

3.1.8.2 When an employee who is legitimately entitled to promotion is administratively charged, the position to which he should have beem promoted shall not be filled until after the administrative case have been finally decided, except when the exigencies <missingtext> </missingtext>

3.1.8.3 If the position is filled in accordance with the foregoing, the appointment should be temporary, and shall end as soon as the employee legitimately entitled to it is exonerated. Should the employee be exonerated of the charges against him, he shall be promoted. If he is found guilty, he shall be disqualified for promotion, and the duration of such disqualification shall be determined by the Civil Service Commission.

3.1.9 An employee who holds a next-in-rank position who is competent and qualified, possesses and appropriate civil service eligibility and meets the other conditions for promotion shall be promoted to the higher position when it becomes vacant.

3.1.10 When the existing vacant position cannot be filled in accordance with the above guidelines, the employee who is occupying the next lower position which is in the same occupational group or, where none is available, in other functionally related occupational group in the same organizational unit should first be considered for promotion: Provided, however, that the said employee meets all the qualification requirements and the appropriate civil service eligibility necessary for promotion thereto.

3.1.11 In the case of new positions, except those in the lowest level, the same shall preferably be filled by competent and qualified employees occupying existing next-in-rank positions in the same organizational unit where the new positions belong. Should there be none, from the other agencies or offices in the Ministry of Health.

3.1.12 In case of positions in newly created offices, the filling thereof shall either be by promotion or recruitment, but preferably by promotion of qualified and competent personnel possessing appropriate civil service eligibilities.

3.1.13 The incumbent of a position that has been reclassified or upgraded due to increase in duties and responsibilities shall be appointed thereto if he is qualified and he possesses the appropriate civil service eligibility for the position.

3.1.14 The qualification standards of a vacant position to be filled by promotion shall not be modified nor revised as to adversely affect the chances of promotion of incumbents who are holding next-in-rank positions except when the exigencies of the service so require.

3.2 For the Promotion Boards

3.2.1 With regards to area of selection for purposes of promotion in the 1st and 2nd level positions:

(a) Preference for qualified and competent next-in-rank employees within the organizational unit where the vacancy exists.
(b) If there is none in the organizational unit, the selection is made from the Office/Service/Bureau/Hospital/Regional Health Office concerned.
(c) If still there is none in the above-mentioned Offices under letter (b), selection will be Ministry wide.
(d) If still there is none in the Ministry concerned, selection will be government or service wide (any government agency other than the Ministry of Health).

3.2.2 That the Promotion Board is only advisory in character and that the Head of Office or agency concerned shall be the recommending official to the Minister of Health.

3.2.3 That the recommendation of the Screening/Credential Committee of the Hospital is to be considered as a guiding principle in both selection and promotion purposes.

3.2.4 For screening procedure, the rule of five is to be followed.

3.2.5 No employee/officer with a performance rating of below satisfactory for the last grading period involved is to be considered for promotion to any position.

<missingtext> </missingtext> as Chief of Hospital, Provincial/City Health Officer, or Assistant Provincial/Assistant City Health Officer may be Ministry-wide or region-wide depending upon the circumstances. However, preferential consideration will be given to the candidate-physician who has been for three (3) or more years in a hardship post or place of assignment, provided he is qualified and meets the requirements for the position involved.

3.2.7 For promotional appointment to Chief of an emergency hospital, preferential consideration may be given to the candidate-physician with surgical capability, provided he is qualified and meets the other requirements for the said position.

3.2.8 For promotional appointment to Chief of Provincial/Regional Hospital, preferential consideration may be given to the candidate-physician with the degree of Master in Hospital Administration (MHA), provided he is qualified and meets the other requirements for the said position.

Section 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 The Ministry - It shall establish a system of ranking positions in accordance with its merit promotion plan for the effective implementation of the Civil Service Law and Rules and the policy guidelines on promotion embodied in this Plan.

4.1.1 System of Ranking Positions - This shall set the hierarchical ranking of positions and the degree of relationships of one position to another.

In determining the positions that are next-in-rank, the following shall be taken into consideration:

- Classification and/or functional relationship;
- Organization structure/s as reflected in the approved organization chart;
- Salary grade and/or range allocation; and
- Geographical location of the position.

4.2 The Ministry and the Agency - Both the ministry and the agencies under or attached to it, shall, for the same purpose as stated above, establish employees' qualification index, Merit Promotion Board, and Complaints Committee.

4.2.1 Qualification Index - Individual personnel folders for employees directly under the Office of the Minister shall be kept in the Personnel Division of the Ministry, while for those agencies under or attached to the Ministry shall be kept in the Personnel Office of said agencies. These personnel folders shall contain, among others, the following data:

4.2.1.1 Performance Record - the employee performance rating for each rating period.

4.2.1.2 Occupational History - the employee record of service indicating office of employment, positions held, inclusive dates of service, salary received, status of appointment, etc.

4.2.1.3 Educational qualification and training - these shall include the employee's educational attainment, special studies and training, the name of the school attended, date of attendance, degree earned or in-service training courses successfully finished, special studies undertaken and the like.

4.2.1.4 Civil Service Eligibility - the title, date and place of every civil Service examination taken by the employee and his ratings shall be indicated. Also included are those eligibilities converted which have been under R.A. 186, R.A. 1080, R.A. 1844, P.D. 907, P.D. 993, P.D. 997, P.D. 1125, or P.D. 1408, indicating respective dates of effectivity.

4.2.1.5 Other Relevant Data -  these shall include all other matters which bear on the qualification of the employee such as commendations and awards for outstanding accomplishments.

4.3 The Promotion Board

4.3.1 The Promotion Board of the Ministry or Agency shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

4.3.1.1 Formulate criteria for evaluating qualifications of employees considered for promotion in accordance with the guidelines on promotion;

4.3.1.2 Adopt a formal screening procedure;

4.3.1.3 Make systematic assessment of the qualifications of candidates for promotion;

4.3.1.4 Conduct tests and interviews;

4.3.1.5 Recommends to the appointing authority the employee who may be considered for promotion; and

4.3.1.6 Recommend appropriate actions to be taken for employees not considered for promotion.

4.3.1.7 Inform the employees who are candidates for promotion of the criteria for and the procedure on promotion.

4.3.2 Composition of the Promotion Board

4.3.2.1 For first level position -

On the Ministry or agency level:

a) An official of the ministry or agency directly responsible for personnel Management.
b) A representative of the organizational unit, which may be an Office, Department or Division, where the vacancy is;
c) A representative of management; and
d) Two representatives of rank-and-file employees who shall be chosen by their peers from the organization unit where vacancy is.

On the Regional Level:

a) The Chief of the Administrative Division;
b) A representative of rank-and-file employees; and
c) A representative of the Division where the vacancy is.

4.3.2.2 For second level positions -

On the Ministry or agency level:
a) Two members to be designated by the Head of the Ministry or agency concerned, who must have a background in public personnel administration or psychology;
b) Two senior members to come from the organizational unit, which may be an Office, Department or Division, where the vacancy is; and
c) One member to be designated by the Civil Service Commission either from the Commission itself or from any other agency of the government when the promotion involves supervisory positions as Chief of Hospitals, Chief of Division, and others of equivalent rank.

On the Regional Level:
a) Assistant Director or Second ranking official;
b) Member to be designated by the Director, who must have a background in public personnel administrative or psychology;
c) <missingtext> </missingtext> nization unit where the vacancy is; and
d) One member to be designated by the Chairman of the Civil Service Commission either from the Commission itself or from any other agency of the government when the promotion involves supervisory positions as Chief of Hospitals, Chiefs of Divisions, and others of equivalent rank.

4.4 The Personnel Officer - The Personnel Officer of the Ministry Personnel Division and those of the agency Personnel Section/Unit shall have the following functions and responsibilities:

4.4.1 Prepare promotional line-up to be submitted to the Promotion Board;

4.4.2 Counsel employees not considered for promotion in accordance with the recommendations made by the Promotion Board;

4.4.3 Maintain and up-date the qualification index and promotional qualification standards; and

4.4.4 Keep and maintain official records pertaining to the Ministry or Agency promotion plan.

4.5.2 Function

The MOH/Regional Complaints Committee shall hear and process protests concerning promotion in the Ministry/Agency or in the Regional Health Offices, as the case may be, and submit its findings and recommendations to the appointing authority within thirty (30) days from the receipt of the protest.

The appointing authority shall evaluate the recommendation of the Complaints Committee and shall decide who shall be promoted to the vacant position involved. In the event the party adversely affected by the decision of the appointing official is not satisfied with the said decision, he may file a written request for reconsideration (3 copies) within five (5) days with the Office of the Minister or the Regional Health Director, as the case may be, from receipt of the decision on the protest. Receipt thereof by the Committee will suspend the period of appeal. If still not satisfied, he may elevate his written appeal (3 copies) within ten (10) days to the Merit Systems Board from receipt of the decision on his request for reconsideration. The appellant shall simultaneously furnish the appointing authority with a copy of his appeal. Any request for reconsideration and/or appeal filed after the expiration of this period shall no longer be entertained.

The decision of the MSB may be appealed to the CSC within ten (10) days from receipt of the copy of the decision by the aggrieved party. The decision of the Commission shall be subject to review only by the Court.

4.5.3 Effectivity of a Contested Promotional Appointment

A contested promotional appointment shall take effect immediately upon its issuance if the appointee assumed his duties immediately. However, the appointment, together with the decision of the Ministry Head, shall be submitted to the Commission for appropriate action within sixty (60) days from its issuance.

Pending the final outcome of the appeal, the appointee should be allowed to assume the duties of the position and paid his salary immediately without waiting for the approval of his appointment.

Section 5. PROTESTS

5.1 Who may file protest

Officers or employees occupying positions which, according to the approved System of Ranking Positions, are next-in-rank to the vacant position, who are competent and qualified and who possess the appropriate civil service eligibility may protest. Protest shall be filed in writing with either the Evaluation and Protest Committee of the Ministry of Health or the Regional Health Office, as the case may be, within fifteen (15) days from notice of the issuance of an appointment.

Any change or amendment to this Plan and System of Ranking Positions shall not take effect earlier than six (6) months after the approval by the Civil Service Commission.

Section 6. GENERAL PROCEDURES ON PROMOTION

Whenever a vacancy occurs in the career or non-career service in this Ministry, the Director of the Bureau/Project or the Chief of Office/Service/Hospital where the vacancy exists, shall submit to the Minister his recommendation: Provided, however, that in the Regional Health Office, whenever such a vacancy occurs, the Chief of Division, the Chief of Hospital, or the Provincial/City Health Officer of the Provincial/City Health Office, where said vacancy exists, shall submit to the Regional Health Director his recommendation.

6.1 Upon the approval of the authority to fill vacant positions, the Personnel Officer of the Ministry/Agency or Regional Health Office, as the case may be, shall determine the incumbents of positions deemed next-in-rank to the vacant position.

6.2 The Personnel Officer shall post on the bulletin board in a conspicuous place within the Ministry/Agency/Regional Health Office, an announcement of the vacant position/s, indicating therein, among other things, the title of the position/s, the duties and responsibilities appertaining thereto, the qualification requirements, and the positions deemed next-in-rank.

6.3 The Personnel Officer shall submit to the Promotion Board the promotional line-up together with the recommendations made by the Chiefs of Divisions/Offices and such other pertinent supporting documents.

6.4 The Promotion Board shall evaluate the qualifications of those employees included in the promotional line-up. In determining the degree of competence and qualifications of an employee, the following factors shall be taken into account:

Performance - this is based on the employee's performance rating for the last period of evaluation.

Education and Training - these shall include educational background: academic studies, scholarships, training grants, the successful completion of approved in-service training courses, and the like. Only those who meet the educational and training requirements for the position and the degree of relevance of such education and training to the duties of the position under consideration shall be taken into account.

Experience and Outstanding Accomplishments - these shall include occupational history, work experience which are relevant to the position to be filled, and accomplishments <missingtext> </missingtext> commendations.

Physical Characteristics and Personality Traits - <missingtext> </missingtext> to physical fitness, attitudes, personality traits of the employee which bear on the nature of the position to be filled. Personal characteristics may include test, imagination, discretion, impartiality, and the like.

Potential - this is an estimate or prediction of an employee's capacity and ability to perform the duties of the position to be filled, and those of higher, more responsible positions in the line of promotion. The prediction may be based on factors such as past performance, training, past work experience or occupational history and outstanding accomplishments, physical condition, attitudes and aptitudes and all other factors that may help in predicting the employee's ability to efficiently perform the duties of the position to which he will be promoted and of other more responsible position higher in the line of promotion.

6.5 The PB shall submit to the appointing authority a list of recommended employee/s for promotion to the vacant position/s, taking into consideration the following criteria:

6.5.1 If there is only one employee who is next-in-rank to the vacant position and he meets all the requirements for promotion thereto, he shall be given the promotion.

6.5.2 Whenever there are two or more employees who are next-in-rank, preference shall be given to the employee who is the most competent and qualified and who has the appropriate civil service eligibility.

6.5.3 In cases where all meet the requisites for the position, preference shall be given to the employee in the organizational unit where the vacancy occurs.

6.5.4 In cases where incumbents of first or second level positions not next-in-rank to the vacant positions are considered for promotion, preference shall be given to the employee who is the most competent, qualified and who has the appropriate civil service eligibility.

Section 8. This Plan and System of Ranking Positions shall take effect upon approval by the Civil Service Commission.

Section 9. All department (now Ministry) orders, circulars, memoranda and regulations inconsistent herewith are hereby rescinded.
