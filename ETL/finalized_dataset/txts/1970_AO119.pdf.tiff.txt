<subject>SUBJECT: Placing the Quirino Memorial General Hospital temporarily under the direct control and supervision of the Secretary of Health.</subject>

<body>
In view of the commitment or working arrangement made by this Department with the NEC—ICA, WHO, UNICEF and other agencies, both public and private, with respect to assistance provided to training and teaching hospitals of this Department, the Quirino Memoria1 General Hospital, which is a training and teaching institution, is hereby temporarily placed under the direct control and supervision of the Secretary of Health. Such arrangement is imperative in order to provide central authority in the administration of on-going projects , expedite action and facilitate the ﬂow of foreign aid and other donated resources for the proper development of the said hospital and of the six designated training and teaching hospitals already placed under the Secretary of Health, namely: the National Orthopedic Hospital, the National Mental Hospital, the National Children's Hospital, the Dr. Jose R. Reyes Memorial Hospital, (North General Hospital), the San Lazaro Hospital and the Dr. Jose Fabella Memorial Hospital (Maternity and Children's Hospital).

This Order which supplements Administrative Order No. 162 dated Iuly 20, 1965, shall take effect July 1, 1970 and shall remain in force until further order.
</body>

<sign>AMADEO H. CRUZ, M.D., C.P.H.</sign>
<signtitle>Secretary of Health</signtitle>

All previous orders, circulars, memoranda, or parts thereof, inconsistent with the provisions of this Order are hereby rescinded.
