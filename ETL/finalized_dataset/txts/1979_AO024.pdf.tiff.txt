<subject>SUBJECT: ISSUANCE OF CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE WITH TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS (CTR) BY THE FOOD AND DRUG ADMINISTRATION (FDA)</subject>

<body>
Pursuant to the provisions of Section 4 (e) in relation to Section 26(a) of Republic Act No. 3720, otherwise known as the "Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act", the following regulations are hereby promulgated for the information, guidance and compliance of all concerned.

SECTION 1. Any person, whether natural or juridical, desiring to engage in the business of manufacturing and/or processing of food, drugs and cosmetics shall secure the required Certificate of Compliance with Technical Requirements (CTR) from the Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

SECTION 2. The CTR issued by the Food and Drug Administrator shall serve as a basis for the issuance of a national License to Operate by the Food and Drug Administration. This may likewise be relied upon by the local government in the exercise of its licensing power.

SECTION 3. For every CTR issued by FDA, a fee shall be charged by it in accordance with the following schedule of payment:

SECTION 4. No License to Operate shall be issued or revalidated by FDA to any Food, Drug and Cosmetic establishment unless a current CTR is first presented to it.

SECTION 5. The CTR once issued shall remain valid and in full force and effect from the date of issue to the end of the calendar year, unless sooner revoked, after a summary investigation made by FDA. The same shall be renewed within the first three (3) months of each calendar year.

SECTION 6. All existing Food, Drug and Cosmetic establishment shall comply with the requirements of these regulations effective January, 1980.

SECTION 7. These regulations shall take effect on January 1, 1980.

Manila, Philippines, May 22, 1979.
