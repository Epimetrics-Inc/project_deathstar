<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines for the Selection of Delegates for the First Children and Tobacco Congress on May 31, 2000</subject>

<body>

On May 31, 2000, the world celebrates the World No Tobacco Day with the theme “Children and Tobacco." In the Philippines, the celebration will be highlighted by the selected children delegates simulating a plenary session in Congress. The passage at a resolution on the ill-effects of tobacco to our children and society shall culminate the session. The following shall serve as the guidelines for the selection of the delegates:

1) One child representative per congressional district shall be chosen nationwide;
2) The regional health offices shall select and send the children delegates to Metro Manila, with the following qualifications:
a) with grand oral and communication skills;
b) from 10-15 years of age;
c) supports initiatives and activities that will help achieve a tobacco-free society

3) The Regional Health Offices shall submit the names of the delegates, the names if their guardians, the consent from parents/guardians, and their tobacco control resolutions on or before May 2, 2000 to Dr. Joseph T. Aricheta, Head of the Tobacco Control Secretariat, Office of the Chief of Staff, Department of Health, San Lazare Compound, Sta.
Cruz, Manila at fax numbers 711-6061, 711-6670 or 743-1829.

Funding sources:

The Regional Health Office shall shoulder the transportation expenses to and from Manila of the delegates and their respective guardian (only one for each delegate]. The Family Health Cluster shall shoulder the
following expenses:
a) The board and lodging expenses from May 30 to June 1, 2000 (for the out—of-town delegates and their guardians);
b) The rental for seven air-con buses that will transport the provincial delegates and their guardians to and from the Batasan Complex and in other areas of Metro Manila. However, the NCR Regional Health Office shall shoulder the transportation and boarding needs of the NCR delegates.
