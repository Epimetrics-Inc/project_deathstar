<docnum>NO. 207, S. 1974</docnum>

<subject>SUBJECT: DESIGNATING CERTAIN MEMBERS OF LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCIES WHO HAVE COMPLETED THE NARCOTICS COMPLIANCE INCESTIGATIVE COURSE CONDUCTED BY THE DRUG ENFORCEMENT ADMINISTRATION OF THE UNITED STATES IN COOPERATION WITH THE UNITED STATES AGENCY FOR INTERNATIONAL AID AS DEPUTY DRUGS INSPECTORS.</subject>

<body>
In the interest of the public service, and pursuant to the authority vested in me as Secretary of Health, concurrently ex-officio member and Chairman of the Dangerous Drugs Board, to enforce the provisions of Section 27 of Republic Act No. 3720 and Section 12 of Republic Act No. 953 in relation to the effective implementation of the pertinent provision of Republic Act 6425 for the purpose of securing more effective control of illegal possession, sale, manufacture of and traffic in dangerous drugs, the following persons who have finished the Narcotics Compliance Investigative Course are hereby designated as Deputy Dangerous Drugs Inspectors:

<image>figure_1.png</image>

<image>figure_2.png</image>

Under the designation, the above-named persons shall have the authority.

1) to enter, at reasonable hours, any medical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore, factory, warehouse, or other drug establishment in which drugs, are manufactured, processed, packed, or held, for introduction into commerce or are held after such introduction, or to enter any vehicle being used to transport or hold such drugs, in commerce; and

2) to inspect, in a reasonable manner, such medical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore. factory, warehouse, establishment, or vehicle and all pertinent equipment, finished and unfinished materials, containers, and labeling therein.

While performing the above functions, the following guidelines shall be strictly followed:

1) Their area of jurisdiction shall be limited within the city/municipality where they are regularly assigned as police officers.

2) They shall, in respect of their duties as deputy dangerous drugs inspectors, be under the direct supervision and control of the Secretary of Health.

3) If any of the above-named persons making such inspection of a medical, dental and veterinary clinic, drugstore, factory, warehouse or other establishment has obtained any sample in the course of the inspection, upon completion of the inspection and prior to leaving the premises, he shall give to the owner, operator, or agent in charge a receipt describing the samples obtained.

4) They shall submit an after-operation report of every activity and accomplishment to the Secretary of Health, copy furnished the Food and Drug Administration, provided that if there are more than one activity or accomplishment performed in one day, a single integrated report of all such activities or accomplishments in one day will suffice.

The authority herein granted shall take effect immediately and shall continue only up to December 31, 1974, and may be extended after assessments made, unless sooner revoked.
