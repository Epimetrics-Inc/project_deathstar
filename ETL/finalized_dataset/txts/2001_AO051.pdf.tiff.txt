<subject>SUBJECT: Decentralization of Dental Prosthetic Laboratory licensing functions of the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services to the Center for Health Development—NCR.</subject>


<body>

I. RATIONALE:

E.O. 102 signed May 24, 1999 by President Joseph E. Estrada, redirected the functions and operations of the Department of Health. To effectively fulﬁll its refocused mandate, the DOH is required to undergo changes in roles, organizational processes, corporate values, skills, technology and structures. Regulatory reforms shall be initiated in accordance with the new roles and functions of the DOH Central Ofﬁce by decentralization of the Dental Prosthetic Laboratories (DPL) licensing functions of the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services to the Centers of Health Development — NCR.

II. SCOPE:

Effective January 2002,- NCR Dental prosthetic laboratory renewals of license to operate except Tertiary Service category will be decentralized to the Centers for Health - Development — NCR. However, issuance of initial license to Operate of all categories will remain a function of the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services (BHFS) Central Office.

III. SPECIFIC GUIDELINES AND IMPLEMENTING MECHANISM:

The new roles and functions of the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services Central Office and the Centers for Health Development - NCR will be as follows.

Bureau of Health Facilities & Services (Central Office):

1. Policy formulation and setting of standards.
2. Supervisory and periodic monitoring of implementation of policy and standard for regulations of DPLs
3. Receive, process and Issue initial license to operate for all categories and renewal of license to operate a tertiary service category DPL.
4. Investigation on charges of complaints for failure to comply and or/ violations to DPL rules and regulations and other oversight functions.
5. Collation & publication of submitted list of licensed dental facility.
6. Provide technical assistance to CHD and other concerned agencies and clientele,
7. Reconstitution of license certiﬁcates.

Center for Health Development — NCR

1. Implement the rules and regulation on DPL licensing.
2. Receive, process and issue renewal of License to Operate (LTO) for primary and secondary service category.
3. Conduct ocular inspection of DPLs applying for renewal of LTO (except tertiary Service category).
4. Monitor compliance to rules and regulation on DPL licensing.
5. Monitor and report DPLs operating without LTO.
6. Submit complete list of licensed DPLs.
7. Submit quarterly/ annual report of licensed DPLs.
8. Accept screen and forward to BHFS applications for initial LTO.
9. Provide technical assistance to LGU’s and clients on DPL licensure & regulation.

REPEALING CLAUSE:

Any Administrative Orders, Rules and Regulations, and other department issuances inconsistent herewith are hereby repealed, modiﬁed or amended accordingly.

V. EFFECTIVITY:

This order shall take effect upon approval of the SECRETARY OF HEALTH.
