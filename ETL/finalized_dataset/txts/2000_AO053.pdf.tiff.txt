<subject>SUBJECT: Amendment to Administrative Order No. 26 s. 1999 Re: Admission Procedure for Residency Training in all DOH Teaching and Training Hospitals</subject>

<body>

Administrative Order No. 26 s. 1999 is hereby amended to include the following provisions:

Admission procedure for residency training in all DOH Teaching and Training Hospitals has been developed for the following purpose:

a. To standardize selection into residency training programs with a centralized qualifying examination under the Department of Health.
b. To match qualified applicants to specialties and to training hospitals.
c. To select applicants with suitable entry competencies for residency training.


1. Item 2.2 shall be revised to include the following:

HMDTS will take charge of the residency matching. The bases for residency matching are as follows: 

a. Centralized Qualifying Examination for Residency Training (QUERT) to be conducted by HMDTS
b. Government service
c. Specialty needs of the endorsing region/province/municipality/district
d. Hospital and specialty preference of eligible resident trainee


2. Item. 3.1 shall as modiﬁed as follows:

Eligible resident trainees are required to secure proper endorsements from a member of the local health board or a well known government or private medical professional in the community for their heeded area of practice as specialist, and submit the same to the teaching/training hospital.


3. Item 3.3 shall likewise be modiﬁed to include the following:

As part of the recruitment/selection of residents, the teaching/training hospitals may conduct attitude and skills assessment through any of the following:

a. One (1) month minimum pre-residency training in the specialty being applied for.
b. Interview
c. Psychometric examination
d. Document review

Written entry examination of individual hospitals shall be discontinued in favor of QUERT. 


4. Item 4.1 shall be reduced to exclude Local Health Board and Regional Director. It shall be read as follows; 

Once the eligible trainee is accepted into a residency training program, a TRAINING AGREEMENT is forged with the teaching/training hospital specifying therein the area of practice after completing the residency training
program. 

As thus amended, all other provisions of administrative Order No. 26 s. 1999 still stand in effect.
