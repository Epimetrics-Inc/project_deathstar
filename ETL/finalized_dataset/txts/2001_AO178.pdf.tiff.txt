<subject>SUBJECT: PRESCRIBING GUIDELINES IN THE GRANTING OF LONGEVITY PAY TO DESERVING PUBLIC HEALTH WORKERS PURSUANT TO SECTION 6.3 OF THE REVISED IMPLEMENTING RULES AND REGULATIONS OF R.A. 7305, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE MAGNA CARTA OF PUBLIC HEALTH WORKERS.</subject>


<body>

1.0 RATIONALE

Public Health Workers shall be encouraged to join and remain in the government service. It is but ﬁtting and proper that those who, because of appropriate qualiﬁcation and excellent abilities, have rendered efﬁcient and meritorious services for the improvement of government operations, be recognized and given incentive awards. The following guidelines in granting longevity pay to deserving Public Health Workers are hereby prescribed.

2.0 ELIGIBILITY FOR LONGEVITY PAY

All Public Health Workers who have rendered ﬁve (5) years of continuous, efﬁcient and meritorious services rendered as certiﬁed by the Head of Agency/Local Chief Executive shall be entitled to monthly longevity pay equivalent to ﬁve percent (5%) of the present monthly basic pay starting April 17, 1992 and for every succeeding 5-year service thereafter.

3.0 CRITERIA

To be entitled to Longevity Pay, a Public Health Worker shall meet the following requirements:

3.1 Have served for ﬁve (5) continuous years as Public Health Worker. Continuous service would include employment on a permanent, temporary, casual or contractual basis.

3.2 Have been rated at least satisfactory for the last ﬁve (5) years or ten (10) appraisal periods.

3.3 Have not been found guilty of any administrative or criminal case within the rating period as certiﬁed by the Legal Ofﬁce concerned.

4.0 EXEMPTIONS

Consultants and/or experts who are hired for speciﬁc purposes and services are not covered by this Order.

5.0 FUNDING SOURCE

Funds for this purpose shall be chargeable against any available savings of the
authorized appropriations of their respective present agency/ofﬁce/service subject to
pertinent budgeting, accounting and auditing rules and regulations.

6.0 INITIAL IMPLEMENTATION

For initial implementation, computation of this beneﬁt shall be based on the employee's salary as of December of every year starting 1997 to 2000. Illustrative examples of computations are attached for guidance and ready reference.

7.0 EFFECTIVITY

The length of service required for longevity pay under this Order shall commence on April 17, 1992.
