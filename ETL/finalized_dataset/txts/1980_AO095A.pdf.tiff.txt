"SUBJECT: The Ministry of Health Policy on Selective Merit Increase for Outstanding/Deserving Employees.

In line with the spirit of LOI No. 562 and for purposes of uniformity in the implementation of the rules/regulations/guidelines for selective merit increase, as provided for in the joint Budget Circular No. 286 and Civil Service Memorandum Circular No. 3, s. 1978, there is hereunder prescribed policy and procedure in evaluating and granting such merit increases to outstanding/deserving officers and employees in the Ministry of Health.

SECTION 1 - Scope - All MOH deserving officers and employees who are covered by the position-classification and compensation plans.

SECTION 2 - Criteria for Merit Increase - The health personnel referred to in Section 1 shall be entitled to merit increase provided they meet any of the following:

2.1 rendered outstanding performance in assigned functions including a creditable accomplishment of special assignment that brought benefits to the government;

2.2 introduced a significant cost-saving or efficiency-enhancing innovation;

2.3 rendered outstanding rural service in community projects; and

SECTION 4 - Appeals - In case an employee/officer does not agree with the adverse findings and recommendation of either of the Committee (MIRC/ESIAC), or with those of the Minister of Health/Regional Health Director, and believes he rightfully deserves the merit increase award, he may appeal in writing, first, to the Agency Head/Regional Health Director if it is the Committee's recommendation he is contesting, of the ESIAC if it is that of the Agency Head/Regional Health Director, within (3) days upon receipt of such adverse action. He should cite his reasons and furnish said bodies with pertinent papers, data, information and other evidences in support of his protest.

4.1 Protests shall be decided within five (5) days upon submission.

4.2 The decision of the ESIAC shall be final.
