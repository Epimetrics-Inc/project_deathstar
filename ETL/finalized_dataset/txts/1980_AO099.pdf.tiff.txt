<subject>SUBJECT: Priority of placement to positions in lower echelons to those qualified and competent coming from the same congressional district or province</subject>

<body>
For reasons of equity, fairness and justice and in order to provide positive assistance in matters of government employment to those residing in the rural areas, the following guidelines in the recruitment of health and hospital personnel dare hereby issued:

1. Whenever there are vacant lower echelon positions in a clinic, rural health unit, hospital or sanitaria, priority of placement shall be given to persons residing within the same congressional district who are competent and qualified for appointment, subject to existing regulations of the Ministry of Health and Civil Service Laws and rules. The term lower echelon positions shall include nurse, medical technologist, pharmacist, midwife, and other similar professions of comparable rank and those below in terms of salary and occupational groupings.

2. Whenever there are vacant medical positions in a clinic, rural health unit, hospital or sanitaria, preference shall be given to residents of the same congressional district or province who are qualified and competent, subject to existing regulations of the Ministry of Health and pertinent Civil Service Laws and rules, except where the interest of the service shall require their immediate filling up, in which case qualified and competent persons from other areas may be appointed.

3. These guidelines shall refer only to those positions which may be filled through original appointment, reinstatement or re-employment which fall under the entrance category. In all other cases, the pertinent provisions of Administrative Order No. 78 dated February 28, 1980, shall prevail.

4. Other appointments issued as exceptions to this Order in the interest of the service require the confirmation of the Minister of Health or his authorized representative.

This Order takes effect immediately.
</body>
