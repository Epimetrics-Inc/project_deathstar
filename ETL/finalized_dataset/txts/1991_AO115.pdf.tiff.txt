<subject>SUBJECT: Providing for the Classification of Household Remedies</subject>

<body>

1. Section 20 paragraph b(3} of R.A. 3720 as amended, authorizes the Secretary of Health to remove by regulation, drugs subject to Sections 21 and 21-B from the requirements of dispensing only by prescription, when such requirements are not necessary for the protection of public health. Consistent therewith and pursuant to Section 26(a) of the same Act, this Order is hereby issued re-establishing a classification of Household Remedy and providing the guidelines for registration as well as dispensing requirements.

2. Household remedy shall refer to any preparation containing pharmaceutical substances of common or ordinary use to relieve common physical ailments which may be dispensed without a medical prescription ' in original packages, bottles or containers, the nomenclature of which has, been duly approved by BFAD in the process of registration.

The household remedy preparation should have no history of or recognized adverse reaction when used according to its indication-

3. The procedure of registration for drugs shall apply to household remedies whenever practicable, provided that the Bureau, by circular, may waive any registration requirement that in its judgment is not applicable to a particular or class of household remedy.

3.1 The labeling regulations on drugs shall likewise govern household remedies- For the reason, however, that household remedies may be dispensed by establishments other than licensed drug' outlets, generic labeling shall be required only when attainable‘ or compatible with the nature of the household remedy applied for.

 

 

' establishment-

    
 
3.2 Upon the effectivity of this Order, the interested drug establishment may apply for the re—classification of its currently registered products which may qualify as household remedies. Its application shall be accompanied by a position paper showing that the products have common or ordinary use and recognized to be safe from long experience.

For products without current registration, the applicant shall accomplish the requirements for drug registration together with a position paper to show that the product has a common or ordinary use and recognized to be safe from long experience.

3.3 The initial list of products classified as Household Remedies is hereto attached as annex A. Inclusion of other household remedies may be done by BFAD. in an appropriate Memorandum Circular after such household remedies are duly registered by HEAD, For this purpose, an annual update of this list shall be made by BFAD

4. Household remedies may be dispensed by licensed drug outlets and other establishments other than drug outlets, PROVIDED that household remedies shall be displayed in a section for its class segregated from any other merchandise or goods carried for sale by such

5. All administrative orders, rules and regulations and other administrative issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Regulation are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

6. This regulation shall take effect fifteen (15) days after, its publication in a newspaper of general circulation.
