<subject>SUBJECT: Coverage and Validity of Medical Examination of Overseas Contract Workers and Seafarers as Specified on the Footnote of the Standard Medical Examination Report Form</subject>

<body>

I. Rationale

Pursuant to Administrative Order No. 85-A s. 1990 on “The Revised Rules and Regulations Governing the Accreditation of Medical Clinics and Hospitals and the Conduct of Medical Examination for Overseas Workers and Seafarers", this guideline is hereby issued to lay down the coverage and validity of medical examination report form.


II. Coverage

This administrative order covers all medicaL clinics/hospitals and all others concerned who are accredited to conduct medical examination of applicants for overseas employment and seafarers.


III. Implementing Guidelines

The following changes shall be incorporated in the footnote of the medical examination report form (Annex 1).

1. validity of Medical Examination Reports

"Consistent with Sec. 9 of AO No. 85-A, s. 1990, this examination is valid at the end of the contract unless not deployed within 90 days from the date of examination. As such a repeat examination will be conducted.”

2. coverage of Examination

“The medical examination does not cover diseases that would require special procedures and examination for their detection such as bronchiectasis, diseases which need chole-GI series; certain kidney problems which need IVP etc., and those which are asymptomatic at the time of examination including pregnancy test.”

This order shall take effect upon approval.
