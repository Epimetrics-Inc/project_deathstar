<subject> S U B J E C T: Guidelines to be observed in granting 3—week year-end bonus to Ministry official and employees for the calendar year 1983.</subject>

<body>

       For the guidance and information of all concerned, the following shall be observed by all agencies under the Ministry officials and employees per Ministry Circular No. 35, s. 1984 and Office of Budget and Management Memorandum dated March 7, 1984.


l. Officials and employees who are entitled to receive the 3~week year—end bonus are those recipients of the 1-week bonus as of December 31, 1983. '

2. Manner of computation shall be one month basic salary less amount of bonus received in December, 1983.

3. Agenc1es with available savings as of December 31, 1983 may effect payment immediately.

4. Agenc1es (bureaus/offices/spec1al hospitals under 0M and hospitals/san1tar1a operating under BC 246) without available savings may avail of the available savings pooled in the Office of the Minister thru realignment of funds prov1ded:
       -the amount necessary shall have been taken up as an obligation in excess of allotment as of December 31, 1983.
       -Such obl1gation shall be cleared with FMS for the determination of the source of savings in the realignment of the funds.

5. Agencies under No. 4 but with available CDC may effect payment immediately.
6. Agencies without available CDC may prevail upon source agencies with excess CDC to accommodate payment of their bonus which should be reimbursed to source agencies upon receipt of the approved realigned advice of allotment
7. the Regional Health Office shall be responsible for the payment of bonus of hospitals/sanitaria/operating units under their jurisdiction.


  
8. Hospitals with excess allotment after payment of bonus shall close their books and revert to the Regional Health Office the excess allotment with the corresponding cash

9. Regional Health Offices with insufficient funds for the purpose shall reflect in their books as of December 31, 1983 as an obligation in excess of allotment the amount necessary to cover the insufficiency.
10. Regional Health Offices shall consolidate resources and the amount of bonus yet to be paid to determine if funds available is sufficient to cover the requirements within their jurisdictions.
ll. If the pooled resources are sufficient to cover the requirements, realignment may be proposed on regional level in order to augment insufficient funds of any one unit within their jurisdiction.
12. Revised financial work plan shall be submitted immediately to proper authorities.

 

       Any problem that may arise with the implementation hereof should be cleared with the Financial and Management Service, this Ministry.  
	This Order shall take effect immediately.
