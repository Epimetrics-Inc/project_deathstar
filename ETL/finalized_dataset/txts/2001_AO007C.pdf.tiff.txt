<subject>SUBJECT: Decentralization of some regulatory functions of the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services on Clinical Laboratories and HIV Testing Laboratories to the Centers for Health Development in the Transition Phase.</subject>

<body>

Stipulated reforms in the Health Sector Reform Agenda deﬁne the regulatory reforms to be instituted in accordance with the new roles and functions of the DOH Central Ofﬁce and the Centers for Health Development.

To effect efﬁciency and effectiveness in the regulation of health facilities and services, the functions of the Bureau of Licensing and Regulation, renamed the Bureau of Health Facilities and Services, have been increased to include the regulatory function of the former Bureau of Research and Laboratories.

The Bureau of Health Facilities and Services is the main policy, supervisory and monitoring body on health facility regulation and the Center for Health Development is the implementing arm'at the regional level.

During the transition phase, some regulatory functions particularly renewal of license to operate clinical laboratories and accreditation of HIV testing laboratories shall be decentralized to the Centers for Health Development.
