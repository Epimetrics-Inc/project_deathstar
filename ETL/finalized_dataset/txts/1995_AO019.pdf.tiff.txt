<subject>SUBJECT: GUIDELINES FOR THE ISSUANCE, UTILIZATION, MAINTENANCE AND LOSS OF COMMUNICATION UNITS (CELLULAR PHONES, BEEPERS, PAGERS AND HAND-HELD RADIOS) OF THE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH</subject>

<body>
I. ISSUANCE OF COMMUNICATION UNITS

1. The following are entitled to issuance of cellular phones, pagers/beepers and radios:

1.1 Secretary and his Staff
1.2 Undersecretaries
1.3 Assistant Secretaries
1.4 Regional Directors
1.5 Assistant Regional Directors
1.6 Bureau/Service Directors 
1.7 Chiefs of retained Hospitals
1.8 Other employees whose duties require the use of aforesaid communication units for effective dispatch of duties.

2. Issuance of any communication unit to an employee below the rank of a Service Director shall be justified by the Director. There should also be a certification by the Accountant concerned as to the availability of funds for monthly rental of the unit.

3. Authority to use the communication unit shall be given by the immediate supervisor upon compliance with all the necessary requirements.

4. All previous issuances of communication units shall be subject to re—registration, inventory and validation at the Disaster Management Unit (DMU). All necessary documents which correspond to previous issuances of the equipment need to be revalidated for proper identification.

5. Memorandum Receipts and other documents which correspond to the transfer of the communication unit to the end—user must be properly signed by the requisitioning officer and end—user for record purposes and documentation.



II. UTILIZATION AND MAINTENANCE OF THE UNITS

1. Communication units are non—transferable. Only personnel with corresponding Memorandum Receipts are allowed to use the unit.

2. All monthly rental fees arising from the use of cellular phone units, beepers or pagers will be charged from the rental expense or communication (03) of each Receiving Office.

3. Preparation of vouchers for rental expense of the aforesaid equipment shall be borne by the Receiving Office or end—user concerned.

4. The Secretary, Undersecretaries, and Assistant Secretaries shall have no ceiling for monthly rate of payment.

5. Regional Directors, Assistant Regional Directors, Bureau/Service Directors and Chiefs of Retained Hospitals shall have monthly ceiling rates of payment of P2,500.00 for cellular phones. However, in the event of a disaster, the ceiling may be increased to P5,000.00. Any excess over the allowed ceiling will be considered personal calls and shall be charged to their personal accounts.

6. Personnel below the level of a Bureau/Service Director who have been issued cellular phones shall have a fixed ceiling rate of P2,500.00 except for those personnel in charged of Disaster Management Operations who are authorized a ceiling rate up to P5,000.00 during disasters. Any excess from the allowed amount shall be charged to their personal accounts.



III. LOSS OF THE UNIT

1. In case of loss of the unit, the following requirements shall be accomplished by the user within 30 days from the date of actual loss: 

1.1 Request for change of unit
1.2 Service Agreement
1.3 Notarized Affidavit of loss
1.4 Notarized Affidavit of Undertaking
1.5 Payment of Outstanding Accounts
1.6 Payment of the Replacement Fee amounting to P 1,450.00

2. Forms shall be made available at the DMU and shall be returned to the same for submission to the Pilipino Telephone Corporation (PILTEL).

3. Failure to submit all the requirements within the allowed number of days (30 days) shall make the user accountable for the replacement of the unit.

This order shall take effect on January 1, 1996.
