<subject>SUBJECT: Pre-Audit of Financial Transactions by Members of the Internal Control Unit and Sub-Units.</subject>

<body>
I. Authority

A. Following the provisions of COA Circular No. 76-26, s. 1976 and pursuant to Administrative Order No. 284 s. 1976, the Management and Audit Chief, Senior Management and Audit Analyst, Management and Audit Analyst II of the Central Internal Control Unit and all designated Internal Control Officers are authorized to:

1. Examine and/or review all financial transactions not subject to pre-audit by COA representatives per COA Circular No. 76-26 dated April 6, 1976;

2. Sign vouchers and other related financial transactions in the following manner:

<image>figure_1.png</image>

The amount in words shall be written above the signature. Rubber stamp shall be used for this purpose;

3. Have access to all records pertinent to transactions under review.

B. In the pre-audit of financial transactions, the Heads of all units shall be primarily responsible to their respective heads, for the proper examination of all transactions in compliance with auditing rules and regulations including existing policies prescribed by the Secretary of Health.

II. Special Provisions

A. The Chief of Financial and Management Service shall initiate any or all necessary arrangements to prevent disruption of pre-audit services in the various agencies under the Central Office.

In case a Sub-Unit head is absent, a representative from the Central Internal Control Unit could be assigned to temporarily head the sub-unit, or establish arrangements to have the financial transactions of the agency concerned to be acted upon by the Central Internal Control Unit staff.

B. Financial transactions shall be routed as follows:

1. From units or officials initiating the preparation of vouchers and/or payrolls.
2. Budget unit or officials maintaining obligation control.
3. Officials authorized to approve disbursements.
4. Accounting Division or Unit.
5. Central Internal Control Unit or Internal Control Sub-Unit.
6. Cashier's Office or COA representative as the case may be.

C. The Internal Control Unit or Sub-Unit shall be responsible in coursing financial transaction which need to be pre-audited by COA representatives. Accordingly, all financial transactions shall be coursed through the Internal Control Unit or Sub-Unit after the Accounting Division has certified the availability of funds.

D. All queries coming from COA representatives relevant to transactions under examination shall be handled accordingly by the Central Internal Control Unit.

III. Effectivity

This Order shall take effect immediately upon receipt of the Office concerned.
