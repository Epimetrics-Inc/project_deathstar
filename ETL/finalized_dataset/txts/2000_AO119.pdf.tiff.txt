<subject>SUBJECT: ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES ON THE PROMOTION OF OVER-THE-COUNTER DRUGS (OTC) TO THE PUBLIC.</subject>

<body>

RA. 7394, otherwise known as the Consumer Act of the Philippines entrusted to the Department of Health to enforce the provisions of Chapter VI thereof,â€™ with respect to Advertising and Sales Promotion insofar as food, drugs, cosmetic and household hazardous substances are concerned. Furthermore, Section 2 of GA 6675 or the Generics Act of 1988 provides that it is the policy of the state to promote, encourage and require the use of generic terminology in the importation, manufacture, distribution, marketing, advertising and promotion, prescription and dispensing of drugs.

For this purpose, the following rules and regulations are hereby prescribed as supplementary guidelines to Administrative Order. No. 23-0 3. 2000 re: The Promotion of Over-TheeCounter (OTC) drugs to the public.

1. Consistent with the rational drug use policy the promotion of OTC drug to the public will only be allowed if its known adverse effects are also cited. Moreover. it is the intention of RA6675 or the Generics Act of 1988," to promote drug safety by minimizing duplication medications and/or use of drugs with potentially adverse drug interactions. 

2. Any sales promotion campaign using medical prescriptions or any part thereof or attachments thereto for travels (particularly travels abroad) or a promise or reward shall not be allowed, nor a permit be issued thereof.

3. Permit to conduct promotion, sponsored and promoted by manufacturing enterprises must in all cases secure the necessary approval from the BFAD, pursuant to the existing law, rules and regulations on the matter.

This Order shall take effect within 15 days from the date of publication in two (2) newspapers of general circulation.
