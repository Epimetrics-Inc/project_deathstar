<subject>SUBJECT: BANNING AND WITHDRAWAL OF PENICILLIN FOR TOPICAL APPLICATION</subject>

<body>
It has been established that penicillin when topically applied can be absorbed through the skin to cause hypersensitivity reaction to the user. However, the proof of evidences that penicillin for topical use is adequately effective is lacking.

In the interest of consumer protection and as a guidance to pharmaceutical manufacturer, distributor and retail outlets, the following orders are hereby promulgated:

O R D E R S

1. Effective immediately, Penicillin Ointment and other penicillin-containing products for topical application are no longer certifiable.

2. Henceforth, the registration of the hereunder are hereby recalled and cancelled:

a. all penicillin ointment and other penicillin-containing products for topical application.

3. For the orderly phasing out of stock which have been distributed to retail outlet prior to the issuance of this order, all stocks should have been recalled and withdrawn by the manufacturer or distributor before June 30, 1976.
</body>
