Sec. 1. Title - These Policies, Procedures and Guidelines shall be known as "Revised Policies, Procedures and Guidelines Governing Affiliation and Training of Students in the Department of Health Hospitals and other Health Facilities".

Sec. 2 Rationale - The production and development of Health Manpower is necessary for the promotion, maintenance and delivery of health services. The Department of Health is making available its hospitals and community health resources for the learning and training of students from health professional schools. To maintain the quality of this relationship, guidelines were originally promulgated under Administrative Order Nos. 26 - C, series of 1982 and 13, s. 1986 governing affiliation and training of students.

Due to the changing needs of society, in science and technology, and changes in the Department of Health's policies, the procedures, and the current implementation of the Local Government Code, regulations governing affiliation and training of students in the Department of Health as embodied in Administrative Order No. 70-A, s. 1989 is hereby revised.

This revised guidelines shall only be applicable to retained <missingtext> </missingtext> hospitals and health facilities.

Sec. 3 National Committee on Affiliation and Training

3.1 The National Committee on Affiliation and Training of Students (NCATS) shall be created, composed of representatives from various health professional groups and administrative support services in the Department of Health.

3.2 Functions:

The National Committee shall:

3.2.1 Be responsible for the administration and supervision of all affiliation and training activities.

3.2.2 <missingtext> </missingtext> regulations on affiliation and training of students.

3.2.3 Plan programs and activities on affiliation and training.

3.2.4 Monitor and evaluate programs and activities of affiliation and training of students.

3.2.5 Supervise Regional Committees on the implementation of policies and standards on affiliation and training.

3.2.6 Recommend for approval to the Secretary of Health Contracts of Affiliation from Special/Specialty Hospitals and other agencies/institutions directly under the Office of the Secretary of Health.

3.2.7 Maintain a list of Department of Health devolved and retained Hospitals, Laboratories and Rural Health Units capable of providing the necessary resources for the students' learning.

3.2.8 Keep records and reports.

Sec. 4 Regional Committee on Affiliation and Training

4.1 A Regional Committee on Affiliation and Training of Students (RCATS) shall be created, composed of representatives from the technical and training staff of the regional field office, the devolved and retained hospitals, the laboratories, provincial health offices and financial services to be appointed by the Regional Health Director.

4.2 Functions:

The Regional Committee (RCATS) shall:

4.2.1 Supervise activities of affiliation and training of students at the regional level.

4.2.2 Monitor and evaluate all affiliation programs and activities.

4.2.3 Recommend for approval to the Regional Health Director/Mayor or Governor contracts of affiliation from hospitals and rural health <missingtext> </missingtext>

4.2.4 Maintain a list of DOH devolved and retained hospitals, laboratories and rural health units capable of providing the necessary resources for students learning. The list shall be approved by the National Committee on Affiliation and Training of Students.

4.2.5 Keep records and reports.

4.2.6 Submit quarterly reports to the National Committee on the following:

4.2.6.1 Name of college, number and category of students

4.2.6.2 Name of hospital, area/department utilized for training

Sec. 5 The Regional Health Director is authorized to approve Contracts of Affiliation within the region except those of Special/Specialty Hospitals which fall under the jurisdiction of NCATS.

Sec. 6 Procedures in Applying for Affiliation with the Department of Health hospitals and other health facilities.

6.1 Hospitals shall make available copies of affiliation contracts to schools, colleges and universities.

6.2 All contracts of affiliation shall be duly accomplished and signed by both parties (Chief of hospital and President of the College/University).

6.3 For newly established schools, colleges and universities with Medical, Nursing and Midwifery courses, application for affiliation to any DOH hospital should be approved by the Step Ladder Curriculum/Education Committee prior to recommendation for approval of NCATS/RCATS to the Secretary of Health or his duly representative. Contract form attached as Annex N.

6.4 All duly accomplished affiliation contracts for hospitals under the Department of Health shall be forwarded to the National Committee for evaluation, then for the recommendation of the Chairman, and later for the approval by the Secretary of Health or his duly representative.

6.5 For hospitals in the region, all duly accomplished contracts shall be submitted to the Regional Committee (RCATS) for evaluation, and for the recommendation of the Chairman and later for approval by the Regional Health Director or his duly representative.

6.6 Application for affiliation shall be filed two (2) months before the start of the actual affiliation.

6.7 Affiliation Contracts shall be renewed yearly for the first two (2) years and every two (2) years subsequently.

Sec. 7 Standard Rates of Affiliation Fees

7.1 The following shall be the standard rate of fees to be collected by all agencies of the Department of Health from affiliating students from various disciplines:

Sec. 8 Distribution of Affiliation Fees:

8.1.1. All staff members in the hospital shall have a share of honoraria from the total collection of affiliation fees per batch provided that all leaves and absences shall proportionally deducted from the amount due him.

8.1.2. Honoraria from resource person shall be taken from the Training Service/Department concerned and shall be based on the number of hours he/she has lectured; and

8.1.3. In the event of conflicts regarding affiliation and training of students, the Chief of Hospital has the authority to resolve the issues within his level in accordance with the Administrative Order.

8.2 The collected affiliation fees shall be divided on the following <missingtext> </missingtext>

8.2.1 Five per cent (5%) of the total collection shall be remitted by Metro Manila hospitals and agencies to the National Committee on Affiliation and Training of Students, Department of Health, Manila

The regional and provincial offices shall remit 5% of their collection to the Regional Committee.

8.2.2 Forty per cent (40%) shall be used to procure supplies and equipment based on training needs as well as for research related to training, as determined by a Committee composed of training officers from the different services. The Accounting Office shall furnish a copy of the updated report of affiliation fees collection and disbursement to the Director and Chief Training Officer.

8.2.3 Fifty five per cent (55%) shall be intended for honoraria or incentives for personnel:

8.3.1 Sixty per cent (60%) for the Training Service/Department concerned (pro-rated)

Forty percent (40%) for the other hospital staff (pro-rated)

8.3.2 Guidelines for Pro-rata Allocation

8.3.2.1 For Service / Department providing training:

An ad-hoc Committee shall be formed in the agency which is composed of one representative from each position group to formulate percentage distribution for each category of staff based on the degree of their participation in the training of students.

8.3.2.2 For Hospital Staff Members not directly involved in the training:

For the other hospital staff members who are not directly involved in affiliation, another Ad-Hoc Committee which is composed of the Heads of the different services shall be formed to work on the distribution of fees.

ANNEX A

GUIDELINES GOVERNING AFFILIATION AND TRAINING OF STUDENTS ON DENTISTRY

I. Policy Statement:

The mutual agreement between the affiliating colleges of Dentistry and Training Hospital selected should be based on reciprocity mutually beneficial to the contracting parties.

The Colleges/Universities utilize the Department of Health hospital dental clinics/rural health units so that their dental students may gain the necessary exposure to clinical and hospital routine procedures familiarizing the participants with total patient care under team management concept and in wider sphere the relation of the hospital to the primary health care program of the administration. They may also utilize the technical staff of the Department of Health to the realistic aspect of dental public Health.

The Department of Health may in turn avail of the services of the students to augment and strengthen the existing dental manpower in the Regional, District and Provincial Hospital/Rural Health Units in the delivery of dental services.

II. General Requirements:

1. There should be an affiliation Dental Health Training Program prepared by the Dental Health Services in coordination with the Health Manpower Development and Training Service, Department of Health. It should contain the following:

1.1 General Objectives
1.2 Specific Learning Objectives
1.3 Course Contents
1.4 Syllabi
1.5 Methodology
1.6 Training Schedule
1.7 Monitoring And Evaluation
1.7.1 Periodic Evaluation Instrument
1.7.2 Grading Scheme
1.8 Rules and Regulations
1.9 Formal Contract of Agreement

2. Profile of Hospital that can accept affiliation of dental students:

2.1 The hospital must be a teaching/training hospital duly recognized by the Department of Health as listed by the NCATS.

2.2 The admission of affiliate for dental students will depend upon the number of available dental staff in the hospital. However, all efforts must be made to establish a linkage mutually acceptable between the school and the Department of Health.

2.3 The dental service of the teaching/training hospitals must be organized with at least a Chief of Dental Clinics and an adequate staff of dentists covering no less than twenty four (24) services call.

2.4 The dental service of the teaching/training hospitals must be involved in the evaluation and planning of suitable education and training program in coordination with the Dental Health Service, Department of Health and the Training Coordinator/Officer of the hospital for professional staff development.

2.5 The Chief of Hospital must make available modern and adequate facilities including multiple function dental units, hydraulic chairs (motorized or pump chair), high speed turbine drill, ultrasonic scaling/polishing set among others in addition to at least a complete set of dental instrument for every dental operatory.

Hospital should provide necessary supplies and materials for the use of undergraduate dental students.

2.6 Hospital administration must provide adequate floor space for each of a dental operatory with good ventilation water system and electrical supply following standard ratio for teaching dental clinics.

2.7 Standard Operating Procedures for dental services and its hospital related function must be in print and upgraded periodically subject to review by the Dental Health Services and must be in accordance with the standard requirements set forth by the Dental Health Service.

2.8 Library and Record/Filing System

An adequate compilation of dental literature, journals and books updated on time should be made available for students use. A record keeping, filing and recall system is supportive of the program or purposes of orientation and reference.

2.9 Records of training for each participant must be prepared by the Chief of Dental Clinics concerned approve by the Chief of Hospitals following the termination of each training group. An evaluation of the dental students affiliation must be a part of the training report.

2.10 Training hospitals should submit a quarterly report to the National Committee on Affiliation and Training of Students of the Department of Health.

3. Affiliation rules and regulations for Schools/Colleges/Students

Only colleges and universities duly licensed by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports upon the recommendation of the Dental Health Services, Department of Health should be allowed to affiliate with the recognized teaching/training hospitals.

3.1 The undergraduate dental students should comply strictly with the rules and regulations of the hospital.

3.2 Registered application forms and payment of fees must be made before the participants could be accepted in the program.

3.3 After approval of the application form submitted, there should be a formal contract of agreement between the hospital and the participant. A fee of ₱50.00/month or ₱300.00/semester must be paid at the Cashier's Office before admission.

3.4 Punctuality shall be observed and "Daily Time Records" should be accomplished upon arrival and departure from the hospital.

3.5 Participants may apply for a leave of absence but not to exceed ten percent (10%) of the total number of hours of the program. In case of five unexcused absences or twenty (20) within the entire program participants will be automatically dropped from the program. Excused absences not to exceed more than ten (10) working days may be made up. Beyond the twenty days maybe decided on a case to case basis depending the merits of the case.

3.6 Code of ethics for dentists must be observed at all times.

3.7 Participants should wear clinical gowns with nameplates at all times for the purpose of identification.

3.8 The participants must be aware of his responsibilities to take care of the supplies, materials, equipments in the hospital. Every end of the month, the dentist shall assist in the preparation of reports on these items. Undergraduate dental students of affiliating dental colleges who cause damages and/or losses of hospital property, equipment, supplies, etc. are required to replace or to pay for the same.

3.9 Dental colleges affiliating with the hospitals under the Department of Health shall be required to send a preceptor to teach and guide dental students in hospital dentistry practice. The ratio of dental students to a preceptor shall depend upon mutual agreement to be established by the college and hospital authorities concerned.

Annex B

GUIDELINES GOVERNING AFFILIATION AND TRAINING OF STUDENTS IN HOSPITAL DIETETICS

I. Policy Statement:

With the mutual agreement between the affiliating colleges and schools which are licensed by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports to offer Nutrition practicum in hospital dietetics should be based on reciprocity mutually beneficial to the contracting parties. The colleges or schools utilize the DOH hospital dietary services to provide their students with the basic practical experiences in the administrative and clinical phase of hospital dietetics. The hospital in return can avail of the professional expertise, research and library facilities and continuing Nutrition Education Program of affiliating colleges and schools.

II. General Requirements:

1. There should be a formal organized training program of affiliation duly agreed upon by the training hospital and colleges or schools containing among other things but not limited to:

1.1 General Objectives
1.2 Specific Learning Objectives
1.3 Course Contents
1.4 Syllabi
1.5 Methodology
1.6 Training Schedule
1.7 Monitoring And Evaluation
1.7.1 Periodic evaluation instrument
1.7.2 Grading scheme
1.8 Rules and Regulations

2. Qualification Standards of Hospitals for Hospital Dietetics Affiliation:

2.1. Minimum Requirements:

A registered Nutritionist-Dietitian must be in charge in the dietary service of the hospital to guide the students during the training course.

2.2. The dietary service of the hospital must have an organized training program in which it shall not exceed 5 undergraduate nutrition students to every registered Nutritionist-Dietitians actually employed by the hospitals.

2.3. The Dietary service of the hospital must have well planned facilities in order that it can be accredited for training. The accreditation of the hospital dietary service shall be in accordance with the provision of R.A. 4226. The NCATS shall recommend the hospital for accreditation as Teaching and Training Agencies.

3. Hospital Dietetics General Affiliation Training Programs:

3.1. The minimum requirements for the length of affiliation period shall be 192 hours. 128 hours of which shall cover the clinical phase and 64 hours of which shall cover the administrative phase.

3.2. Require affiliating schools and colleges to assign one (1) Nutritionist-Dietitian preceptor per twenty five (25) students. These preceptors shall help in the evaluation and supervision of students as well as the training program.

3.3. Coordinator of the training program must have at least three (3) years of experience in the field of nutrition and dietetics and only a Nutritionist-Dietitian who have had at least a year of experience may participate in the training of students.

4. Responsibilities of Affiliating Agencies:

Only colleges and schools which are licensed by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports will be allowed to affiliate with the Department of Health Agencies:

Affiliating Agencies shall:

4.1. Obtain affiliation contract forms from the Department of Health Hospital Agencies recognized as Teaching and Training for Hospital Dietetics to be forwarded to NCATS for approval.

4.2. Permit an affiliation fee of ₱250.00 per undergraduate nutrition student for the whole 192 hours in the period of affiliation.

4.3. See to it that all students shall have first satisfactorily completed the theoretical aspect requirement in Nutrition education, methods of nutritional assessment, normal and therapeutic nutrition, infant feeding, nutritional food service, etc.

4.4. Guarantee that their affiliates shall be responsible for any damages, breakages and losses of the Department of Health and hospital property, equipment and supplies set by the hospital Agencies.

4.5. Enforce that hospital dietary service training be set at 24 days duration.

4.6. See to it that instructors and students observe and abide with the policies, rules and regulations set by the Department of Health Training Agencies and that Professional Discipline be maintained at all times.

ANNEX C

GUIDELINES GOVERNING AFFILIATION AND TRAINING OF STUDENTS IN PUBLIC HEALTH NUTRITION

I. Policy Statement

The mutual agreement between the affiliating schools/colleges/universities which are licensed by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports and the Department of Health to offer practicum in Public Health Nutrition should be based on reciprocity beneficial to both contracting parties.

The schools/colleges/universities utilizes the technical staff of the Department of Health and facilities of the Regional/District/Provincial necessary exposure to realistic aspects of public health nutrition and nutrition related health services/programs in the community.

The Department of Health in return can avail of the services of the students to augment or strengthen the existing health manpower in the delivery of nutrition and nutrition related health services at the grassroot level with community participation.

II. General Requirements:

There should be an affiliation training program prepared in coordination with the Nutrition Service and the Health Manpower Development and Training Services (HMDTS).

1. It should contain the following:

1.1 General Objectives
1.2 Specific Learning Objectives
1.3 Course Contents
1.4 Syllabi
1.5 Methodology
1.6 Training Schedule
1.7 Monitoring And Evaluation
1.7.1 Periodic Evaluation Instrument
1.7.2 Grading Scheme
1.8 Rules and Regulations
1.9 Formal Contract of Agreement

2. Qualification Standards of DOH Field Health Units for Public Health Nutrition Affiliation.

2.1 Minimum Requirements:

The Department of Health through Nutrition Service, the Regional Health Offices and Integrated Provincial Health Offices where undergraduate nutrition students may affiliate to obtain the necessary skills and knowledge in community nutrition must meet the following requirements:

2.1.1 A Senior Dietary Nutritionist (Supervising Nutritionist as per Executive Order No. 119) at the Regional Health Office or a Dietary Nutritionist II at the Integrated Provincial Health Office must be present in the region or provinces, respectively, in order to orient, supervise and guide the nutrition affiliates in their field practice;

2.1.2 The Regions or Provinces concerned must have ongoing Nutrition programs such as center-based feeding and other organized health and nutrition education classes in which at least 10 students can participate and are exposed to other nutrition activities in the community;

2.1.3 Senior Dietary Nutritionist, Dietary Nutritionist, Provincial or Municipal Nutritionist or a licensed nutrition graduate may act as preceptor provided they have undergone orientation briefing on the DOH affiliation program and are entitled to an allowance of specific percentage of the affiliation fee as stipend;

2.1.4 Hold conference/meetings with the Heads of Affiliating schools/colleges/universities during and after affiliation as often as deemed necessary to assure the effective implementation of the Department of Health's affiliating rules and regulations;

2.1.5 Require affiliating schools and colleges to assign one (1) Nutritionist-Dietitian as preceptor per twenty (20) students. These preceptors shall help in the evaluation and supervision of students as well as the training program;

2.1.6 Must have a registered Nutritionist-Dietitian training staff responsible for the orientation-training program of affiliating students and preceptors;

2.1.7 The Regional Health Manpower Development Training Service and the Senior Dietary Nutritionist shall act as the coordinator for the training in public health nutrition.

2.1.8 Other requirements that the affiliating students will comply during the affiliation period are the following:

2.1.8.1 Passed qualifying examination before acceptance to the affiliation training program.

2.1.8.2 Case study

2.1.8.3 Practicum Report

2.1.8.4 Pre and Post Test in the Theoretical Phase

2.1.8.5 Complete attendance

2.1.8.6 One Nutrition education material

The place of the affiliation will be based on the choice of affiliating school. However, it will be subject to the approval of the Regional Health Office or Integrated Provincial Health Office concerned.

The Department of Health through NCATS reserved the right and has duty to suspend or cancel affiliation privileges of any schools when the school authorities and/or their respective students have not complied with the requirements of the Contract of Affiliation and/or affiliating students have repeatedly committed errors detrimental to the health, safety and welfare of their subjects. The final decision in case of any appeal is made by the affiliating schools and universities shall be made, however, by the DOH through the NCATS.

The rules and regulations set by the administrative organizer shall be enforces by the Regional/Provincial Chief in accepting affiliation nutrition students and in renewing existing contracts.

III. General Affiliation Training Program in Public Health Nutrition Guideline:

1. The minimum requirements for the length of affiliation shall be 288 hours or 7 weeks for undergraduate nutrition students.

2. A didactic training at the regional/provincial level shall be given for five (5) days (40 hours) before the affiliates are fielded.

3. Field practice shall be twenty-nine (29) days (232 hours).

4. Feedback and post-evaluation shall be two (2) days (16 hours).

IV. Responsibilities of Affiliating Agencies:

Only colleges and universities licensed by the Department of Education, Culture and Sports will be allowed to affiliate with the Department of Health.

Affiliating agencies shall be:

1. Obtain affiliation contract forms from RHO/IPHO and accomplish them properly before submission to the NCATS or RCATS.

2. Provide a field preceptor to guide, teach, supervise and evaluate students' field work in accordance to the ratio of the one (1) preceptor per 20 students. The field preceptor must have at least one (1) year experience in public health nutrition and must have undergone a one (1) week orientation at the RHO/IPHO.

3. Guarantee that the affiliates shall be responsible for any damages, breakages and losses of the DOH property, equipment and supplies.

4. Enforce that public health nutrition students be set at 36 days duration or 7 weeks or a total of 288 hours at 3 hours/day at 5 days a week.

5. See to it that all students shall have satisfactorily completed the requirements for affiliation.

6. Remit an affiliation fee of ₱250.00 per student for 288 hours.

7. See to it that instructors and students observe and abide with the policies, rules and regulations set by the NCATS and that professional discipline be maintained at all times.

V. Joint Responsibilities of Affiliating Schools/Colleges/Universities and Department of Health Through NCATS:

1. The training program shall be a joint responsibility of the Department of Health and schools/colleges/universities. Assignment of students shall be in accordance with the aforementioned rules.

2. Joint conference of schools/colleges/universities with the Department of Health through NCATS shall be in accordance to needs to ensure the proper implementation, evaluation and improvement of the program.

3. Promotion of cooperation or a harmonious working relationship among teaching/training and field health personnel.

ANNEX D

GUIDELINES GOVERNING AFFILIATION AND TRAINING OF STUDENTS IN MEDICAL TECHNOLOGY

I. Department of Health Laboratories designated for training of Medical Technology interns in order to obtain the necessary skills and knowledge. In clinical laboratory, practice must meet the following requirements:

1. The laboratory should be duly licensed and accredited as specified by the law on clinical laboratories R.A. 4688.

2. The position of medical technologist should be provided for each of the sections of clinical chemistry, clinical microscopy (urinalysis and parasitology), microbiology, serology and histopathology.

3. There should be an adequate laboratory space to accommodate both the staff and the medical technology interns.

4. There should be sufficient equipment and supplies necessary depending on the volume and type of examinations as well as the number of interns to be trained by the laboratory.

5. Each section of the laboratory can be utilized for training only if it can accommodate at least two (2) interns at any one time.

6. The laboratory should have a training program for medical technology interns.

6.1. The person responsible for this program should be a member of the specialty in laboratory medicine.

6.2. The training program should be prepared in coordination with the training officer of the hospital or health region and chief medical technologist and submitted to the Committee on Affiliation with the contract of affiliation.

6.3. The program should provide for an objective measure or evaluation of the baseline skills and knowledge prior to allowing and utilizing interns to handle actual specimens from patients.

6.4. The program should include the (1) objective of training, (2) method or procedures to be taught, (3) principles that go with the methods, (4) right work attitudes and ethical values, (5) precision, (6) objective evaluation of performance in each section, (7) time schedule and (8) lectures, seminars and conferences and (9) methodology of training.

7. The minimum number of examination of samples of each section to accommodate 1 intern are as follows:

7.1. Clinical Chemistry - 3,000 exams/annum

7.2. Clinical Microscopy

1) Urinalysis (complete analysis) - 3,000 samples/annum
2) Parasitology (direct fecal smear and concentration test of Kato thick smear) - 1,000 samples/annum

7.3. Microbiology

1) Gram-staining, acid fast staining - 1,000 samples/annum
2) Isolation and identification of organism - 1,000 samples/annum

7.4. Hematology

1) Peripheral blood study to include hemoglobin, white cell count, Schilling hematocrit and red cell morphology, or CBC - 3,000 samples/annum
2) Sedimentation rate, platelet count, bleeding and clotting time, malarial smears and other test - 1,200 samples/annum

7.5. Blood Banking and Serology

1) Typing - 1,200 tests/annum
2) Complete cross-matching - 600 tests/annum
3) Serology
VDRL - 300 tests/annum
OTHERS - 600 tests/annum

7.6. Histopathology - 600 specimens/annum
Cytology - 600 specimens/annum

For each additional intern, the above number of examinations shall be required.

8. At least one registered medical technologist shall be assigned to every group of interns not exceeding five (5) in each section.

9. The staff and personnel of the laboratory will be assisted by a laboratory instructor experienced in actual laboratory work from sending University or College to assist especially to monitor individual attendance, behavior, performance of the students including guidance in readings and application of the theoretical knowledge taken up in school to actual practice.

10. No more than 20% of the number of medical technology interns shall be allowed to train during night shift, such interns should have a medical technologist to work with them.

11. The allotment derived from the intern's affiliation fee for the laboratory must be spent for the laboratory needs utilized exclusively for training and not for payment of furnitures or appliances of the laboratory.

12. The Director of the Hospital is responsible for the admission of the number of interns approved by the Committee. He should not enter into any contract of affiliation with any college, university or school for medical technology if any of the conditions set forth in this Administrative Order are not complied with.

13. Priority should be given to interns residing within the area or regions of the hospital.

II. All universities, schools, colleges of Medical Technology must have met the following requirements before applying for affiliation to any clinical laboratory of the Department of Health:

1. Approval from the Department of Education, Culture and Sports.
2. Compliance with the provisions of R.A. 498 and Presidential Decree No. 534.

These universities, schools and colleges shall:

3. Provide laboratory instructor experience in actual laboratory work to monitor individual attendance, behavior and performance of the students including guidance in readings and application of theoretical knowledge obtained from school to actual practice and to assist the pathologist and professional staff in implementing the approved training program.

4. See to it that all medical technology interns before beginning the internship in any section must have completed the theoretical aspect of that particular section.

5. Pay to hospital sixty pesos (₱60.00) for each intern each month. Payment is done within the first fifteen days of the month or period stipulated.

6. See to it that the laboratory instructor and students observe and abide with the policies, rules and regulations of the hospital and that discipline is maintained at all times.

7. See to it that the students and instructor are in their proper uniforms and provided with a laboratory gown.

III. Joint responsibilities of affiliating universities, schools and colleges of Medical Technology and Laboratories.

1. The training program shall be prepared by the pathologist-in-charge of the laboratory in coordination with the training officer of the hospital. Its implementation shall be the joint responsibilities of the pathologist, staff and personnel of the laboratory and the laboratory instructor of each university, school or colleges assigned to the laboratory for the duration of <missingtext> </missingtext>

2. The internship schedule shall be properly apportioned to the different section of the laboratory services as follows:

2.1. Clinical Chemistry - 3 months
2.2. Clinical Microscopy (urinalysis and parasitology) - 2 months
2.3. Microbiology (bacteriology/) - 3 months
2.4. Hematology - 2 months
2.5. Blood Banking and Serology - 1 month
2.6. Histopathology - 1 month

Any change of this schedule shall be by mutual agreement in writing of the authorized representative of the school and the training laboratory and submitted to the committee.

3. A monthly conference should be held to evaluate the implementation of the program and institution of changes by the pathologist, laboratory instructor and staff of the laboratory and for problems that might arise during the implementation.

4. Maintenance of cooperation and harmonious working relations at all times among students and the staff of the hospital.

ANNEX E

GUIDELINES GOVERNING AFFILIATION AND TRAINING OF STUDENTS IN MEDICINE

I. Policy Statement:
