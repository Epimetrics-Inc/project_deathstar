<subject>SUBJECT: GUIDELINES ON THE PAYMENT OF SEPARATION PAY TO GRATUITY WORKERS IN SANITARIA.</subject>

<body>

INTRODUCTION

Gratuity workers are patient-assistants who are assigned to do specific tasks or jobs within the health facility (e.g. sanitaria/leprosaria) for purposes of rehabilitation therapy. For the services they render, they are given a monthly allowance known as Gratuity Pay amounting to four hundred and fifty pesos (P450.00) as authorized by the Department of Budget and Management. In recognition of their contribution in the provision of necessary health services, gratuity workers shall receive separation payment as part of their benefit should they opt to retire.

This set of guidelines is hereby prescribed to ensure appropriate fund allocation and utilisation for the purpose.


II. IMPLEMENTING GUIDELINES

1. All Sanitaria are hereby authorized to grant separation benefits for gratuity workers opting to retire.

2. Separation payment for each gratuity worker shall be equivalent to one (1) month gratuity pay for every year of service.

<image>figure_1.png</image>

3. Gratuity Workers who have already received the Ten Thousand Pesos (P 10,000.00) incentives are disqualified. 

4. The separation benefits are exclusive to workers who have been patient in the sanitarium.

5. The sanitary shall allocate the necessary budget for the granting of the separation benefits subject to the availability of funds.
