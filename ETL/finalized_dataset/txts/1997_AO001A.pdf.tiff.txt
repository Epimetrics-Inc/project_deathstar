TO: All Medical Clinics and Hospitals Accredited to Conduct Medical Examination of Overseas Contract Workers and Seafarers, the Philippine Overseas Employment Administration, they Maritime Industry Authority, and All Others Concerned.

<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines in the Prescription and Use of Drugs-Against Intestinal Parasites.</subject>

<body> 

1. In order to protect the health and well-being of overseas contract workers and seafarers, all accredited medical clinics and hospitals are hereby advised to take caution prescribing anti-helminthic and amebecidal drugs. 

2. These drugs should be given only when medically indicated, that is, when stool examination shows the presence of ova or parasites; specific drugs should be prescribed following the dosage schedule indicated in the literature and the PIMS.

3. Routine prophylactic and shotâ€”gun therapy are without scientific basis and therefore are strictly prohibited.

4. The accredited medical clinics/hospitals must educate all their reliant OCWs and seafarers regarding the dangers of self-medication. They should be advised to strictly follow the prescriptions given to them by the clinic/hospital.

5. The POEA and the MARINA are hereby requested to circularize this issuance to recruitment, manning and shipping agencies its help inform and educate their recruits regarding this matter.

6. A repeat stool examination may be performed one week prior to departure of the DUN and seafarer and if medically indicated, anti-intestinal parasitic drugs should be prescribed.

7. Drugs prescribed and their dosages must be properly recorded in the official logbooks of the medical clinics which shall be made available upon request of the BLR and other interested parties.

These guidelines shall take effect immediately.
