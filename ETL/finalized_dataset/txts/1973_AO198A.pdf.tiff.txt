<subject>SUBJECT: Rules and Regulations Governing the Hospital Pharmacy Internship Program in Hospitals under the Department of Health, in accordance with Article III R.A.5921 and the Special Provision of the General Appropriation Act.</subject>

<body>
The Pharmacy, as one of the Services of the hospital devoted to the professional care of patients, is extensively used for therapeutic services, cognizant of its importance to the delivery of good patient care and subject to the following rules and regulations, the Pharmacy services and facilities of Teaching and Training Hospitals under the Department of Health may be offered for the internship of Pharmacy students.

Hospitals accredited by the Council of Pharmaceutical Education where pharmacy students may affiliate must meet the following requirements:

1. That the Teaching and Training Hospital concerned is duly licensed as such in accordance with Bureau Administrative Order No. 3, series of 1971, of the Bureau of Medical Services;

2. That the Pharmacy must meet the requirements prescribed in the Drug and Cosmetic Act and the provisions of Administrative Order No. 60, series 1968, of this Department; and

3. That an agreement between the Affiliated College of Pharmacy and the Training Hospitals should be promulgated in accordance with the attached contract of affiliation form;

4. That the working relationship of the hospital, college and the students shall be in accordance with the following requirements.

Responsibilities of the Hospital.

a. Training hospitals shall submit a report to the Bureau of Medical Services as soon as their affiliation training has ended.

b. Records of practice of each student shall be signed by the Pharmacist incharge and approved by the Chief of Hospital at the end of internship.

c. An appropriate certification indicative of successful completion of the prescribed internship shall be awarded to the student by the Hospital.

Responsibilities of the affiliating College - 

a. The Dean of the affiliated school shall issue certification to the student of their respective colleges.

b. They must send only students in good health.

Responsibilities of the Student Affiliates - 

a. Keep an up-to date record of their hours of practice.

b. Comply strictly with the rules and regulations of the hospital pharmacy service.

c. Each student responsible thereto shall pay or replace, damages or loses to hospital property, equipment and supplies.
