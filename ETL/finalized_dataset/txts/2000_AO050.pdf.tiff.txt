<subject>SUBJECT: Procurement Benchmarks</subject>

<body> 

The Department of Health (DOH) recognizes absolute dependence of all its programs and projects including foreign—assisted projects (FAPs) for the accomplishment of their respective objectives in the procurement networks. However, utmost prudence
has to be exercised in order to be able to obtain an expedient and economical procurement process. It is essential therefore that all relevant procedures and activities be standardized. 

As it is, all procurement process are in particularity designed to achieve the following objectives:

1. That the procured goods, works and services must be of satisfactory quality and are compatible with the programmed activities:
2. That procured items are delivered, completed and implemented in a timely fashion; and
3. That procured items are priced so as not to adversely affect the economic and financial viability of the programs and projects.

While implementation of procurement process are mandatorily in accord with the provision s of Executive Order No. 302, actual conduct thereof is the primary responsibility of the Office for Management Services of this Department. As such,
establishing lead times and loci of responsibilities therefor as herein enumerated are damned rational and regular. Thus,
