<subject>SUBJECT: Guidelines for the implementation of Item 5.5.4 of the General Appropriations Act of 1981 Batasang Pambansa Blg. 80.</subject>

<body>
"5.5.4 Subsidy for indigent patients in the use of specialized equipment not available in government hospitals, subject to the rules and regulations approved by the President (Prime Minister) upon recommendation of the Minister of Health ----- ₱2,000,000"

The following guidelines regulating the use of this fund are hereby recommended:

a. Only indigent patients can avail of this subsidy.
b. Only equipments not found in any government hospital in the area, i.e., scanner, cobalt, laser beam, laboratory, computers, x-ray will be included.
c. Only extremely necessary examinations shall be undertaken by this subsidy.
d. A committee will be created to regulate the use of this fund. It shall prescribe fees and promulgate rules and regulations prescribing the utilization of this fund.

Rules and Regulations for the implementation of the above guidelines:

1. The Chief of Hospital concerned shall determine the necessity of the requested examination.
2. The Medical Social Worker will certify for the indigency of the patient.
3. In the Rural area X-ray shall be included in the subsidy.
4. Patients admitted in the subsidized private hospitals shall also be entitled to this subsidy.

Mechanism of Reimbursement for hospitalization:

Private hospitals shall send the bill to the Office of the Minister for reimbursement. Where advance arrangement has been made with particular private hospitals, a portion or the total fund may be obligated at once in favor of such hospitals to assure them of the availability of funds and expeditious payment. If necessary, a portion or the total fund may be deposited with recognized government depository banks, such as the Philippine National Bank, to allow settlement of bills by commercial checks.

Accordingly, all the above guidelines as formulated for the information, guidance, reference and for strict observance and implementation in all hospitals under the Ministry of Health.

The Order takes effect upon approval.
