<subject>SUBJECT: Providing for the Classification of Household remedies</subject>

<body>

1. Section 20 paragraph b(3) of R.A. 3720 as amended, authorizes the Secretary of Health to remove by regulation, drugs subject to Sections 21 and 21.3 from the requirements of dispensing only.by prescription, when such requirements are not necessary for the protection of public health. Consistent therewith and pursuant to Section 26(a) of the same Act, this Order is hereby issued re—establishing' a classification of Household Remedies and providing for the guidelines of registration as well as dispensing requirements. 

2. Household remedy shall refer to any preparation containing pharmaceutical substances of common or ordinary use to relieve common physical ailments which may be dispensed' without a [medical prescription in original packages, bottles or containers, the nomenclature of which has been duly approved by BFAD in the process of registration.

The household remedy preparation should have no history of or recognized reaction after being marketed and used for at least 20 years according to its indication.

3. The procedure-for registration of drugs shall apply to household remedies whenever practicable, provided that the Bureau, by circular, may waive any registration requirement that in its judgment, is not applicable to a specific class of household remedy.

3.1 The labeling regulations on drugs shall likewise govern household remedies.
     

3.2, Upon the effectivity of this Order, the interested drug establishment may apply for the re—classification of its currently registered products which may qualify as household remedy subject to the rules and regulation of BFAD

3.3. The initial list of products classified as Household Remedies is hereto attached as Annex ”A". Inclusion of other household remedies may be done by BFAD in an appropriate Memorandum Circular after such household remedies are duly registered by BFAD. For this purpose, an annual update of this list shall be made by BFAD.

4. Household remedies may be dispensed by licensed drug outlets and other establishments other than drug out1ets, PROVIDED that household remedies shall be in a section for its class segregated from any other merchandise or goods carried for sale by such establishment.

5. All administrative orders, rules and regulations and other administrative issuances or parts thereof inconsistent with the provisions of this Regulation are hereby repealed or modified accordingly

6. This regulation shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in a newspaper of general circulation.
