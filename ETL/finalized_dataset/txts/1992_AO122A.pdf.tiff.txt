<subject>SUBJECT: Subject: GUIDELINES ON THE PROCESSING AND APPROVAL OF CLEARANCE FOR DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH-CENTRAL OFFICE</subject>


<body>
       To expedite the —processing and approval .of clearance certificate relative to the retirement, resignation and transfer of DOH officials and employees, the following guidelines are hereby~ prescribed for the guidance and information of all concerned:

1. A Clearance Certificate (Annex "A” — revised June 1, 1992) shall be properly accomplished in four (4) copies as Supporting document to the following applications:
1.1. Retirement

1.2 Resignation
1.3. Transfer
1.4 Maternity Leave;

l.5 Vacation/Sick leave for a period of thirty (30) days or more.

i.6. Travel abroad irrespective of the number of days whether official or personal

1.7. Fellowship/Study grant

2. The Chief of Bureau/Office/Hospital with whom the concerned -employee' is assigned shall be the recommending official for the clearance certificate.

3. To facilitate the approval of clearance, the following shall be observed:
3.1. Items 1 to 7 of the Clearance Certificate Form (revised June 1, 1992) must bear the signature of the clearing officials concerned. 

3.2 In the absence of the duly authorized clearing officials, the officials, next-in-rank or officers-in-charge shall sign for and in their behalf.

4. Except for those securing clearance for purposes falling under 1.4. and 1.5, officials and employees are required to execute affidavits to the effect that they are committed to settle any suspension/disallowance that may arise beyond the valid date of the clearance issued to them.

5. With respect to Item 5 Completion of service contract for training/fellowship/study grant local and abroad of the revised clearance certificate, the requirements of Sec. 12 of Executive Order No. 367 shall be fully enforced. 

6. This Order shall apply to all officials occupying third (3rd) level positions in the Regional Health Offices as well as the Medical Center Chiefs of the eleven (11) Special Hospitals under this Department, in case of retirement/resignation/transfer in addition to the prescribed clearance from their respective offices.

7. The Clearance Certificate is valid up to six (6) months from the date of its approval.

8. This Order takes effect on June 1,1992.
