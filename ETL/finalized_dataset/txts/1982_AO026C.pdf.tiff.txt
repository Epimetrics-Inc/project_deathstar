Provide qualified, clinical instructors to guide, teach, supervise and evaluate students’ clinical experience in the training/teaching hospital in accordance with the ratio of one (1) clinical instructor per eight (8) students. No student shall be sent to the clinical area without the clinical instructor from the affiliating school or college.
b. Pay or replace damages, breakages or losses by the faculty and students of hospital property, equipment and supplies.
c. See to it that all radiologic technology affiliates before beginning of their clinical practice in a particular clinical area must have satisfactorily complete the theoretical aspect of radiation techniques.	
d. Pay the hospital forty pesos (Php 40.00) for each student per month. Payment is due within the first five days of each month.	
e. Make a request for new affiliation to the Chief of Hospital accomplishing the appropriate standard form of the Contract of Affiliation (4 copies) and coursed through the Executive Secretary NCATS.
f. See to it that the instructors and students observe and abide with the policies, rules and regulations of the hospital and that professional discipline is maintained at all times.

11.11 Joint responsibilities of Affiliating Schools and Colleges of Radiologic Technology and the Training and Teaching Hospitals.
a. The clinical program of radiologic technology students shall be joint responsibilities of the radiologic technology training staff of the hospital and the clinical instructors of each respective school. Assignments and proper rotation of students for each clinical area shall be prepared in accordance with the aforementioned rules. When there are two or more schools affiliating in a hospital, the assignment of students in a clinical area may be on a two-shift bases.
b. Holding of joint conferences with the authorities of the hospital and the school for the proper implementation, evaluation and improvement of the program.
12. Social Work
12.1 Hospitals authorized by the Minister of Health to accept social work student affiliates must meet these requirements:
12.1.1 Must be a teaching and training hospital with at least 150 bed capacity duly licensed by the Bureau of Medical Services.
12.1.2 Must have a well-organized and administered Medical Social Service headed by a registered and qualified Social Worker with at least 21 Units graduate studies from a duly accredited school and supervised field work experience of at least 300 case hours from a recognized social health agency, and/or a staff member (a) with the same qualification, or who is pursuing higher education to meet above qualification. Provided that the incumbent head who lacks the above qualification would upgrade herself either thru formal or non-formal education for the benefit of the service and the student affiliate.
12.1.3 Must have a n adequate office space and facilities which assures privacy for interview, case recording, and filing
12.1.4 Shall accept such number of students for a specified period in proper proportion to the number of qualified medical social workers which shall be 5 students to every one medical social worker (5:1) and, to the number of cases, which shall be a ratio of one social work student to every five inpatient (1:5) and/or one social work student to every ten out-patients (1:10).
12.1.5 Shall provide a well-equipped ward conference room for the holding of regular group work sessions with patients, watchers, and/or family members.
12.1.6 Shall provide a well-equipped conference room for the holding of joint conferences with the staff of the hospitals and school/college of social work for the proper implementation, evaluation and improvement of the training program.
12.1.7 Shall promote cooperation and harmonious working relationship through the leadership of the Head of Medical Social Service and her staff. Hold periodic dialogues between the medical social service and training staff (medical and nursing) and the social work education groups in order to entertain suggestions and thresh out problems for the benefit of the patients and students.
12.1.8 The hospital authorities reserve the right to recommend to the Minister of Health the discontinuance or withdrawal of affiliation privileges of any school when, in its judgment, the school authorities of their respective students have not complied with the requirements of the contract of affiliation or violated these rules and regulations of the students have repeatedly committed errors detrimental to the health, safety and welfare for the patients.
12.1.9 Semi-annual reports in the affiliation fees received showing the percentage for each category of the personnel participating in the affiliation program must be submitted to the Minister of Health. A narrative report on the status and/or problems of the affiliation program must also be submitted to the Minister of Health.
       12.2 Responsibilities of the Affiliating Schools
12.2.1 All colleges and schools of social work must be recognized and duly accredited by the authorities of the Ministry of Education and Culture and the Board for Social Work before, applying for the affiliation of its social work students on a teaching and training hospital of the Ministry of Health.
12.2.2 Must meet the minimum standard requirements for social work education and training set by an Accreditation Committee of the Schools of Social Work Association of the Philippines.
12.2.3 Shall provide qualified social work instructors and field work coordinators to guide, teach, supervise, and evaluate students’ performance at the ratio of one (1) social work instructor to every three (3) social work students. Field instructors must be sent to the Office of the Medical Social Work Adviser of the Ministry of Health and the Hospital for orientation, two weeks before placement of the students to the hospitals.
12.2.4 Shall send students who are in good health as *text missing* 
12.2.5 Shall send students who are in their Junior and/or Senior year or who are on their graduate studies
12/2/6 Shall send only those students with intellectual maturity and emotional stability.
12.2.7 Shall arrange the class schedule of the student affiliates to be able to comply with a minimum schedule of sixteen hours (16 hours)
12.2.8 Shall pay the hospital within the first five days each month, thirty pesos (P30.00) for each student as monthly affiliation fee.
12.2.9 Shall see to it that the field instructors and students observe and comply with the policies, rules and regulations of the hospital and that professional discipline is maintained at all times.
12.2.10 Faculty Field Instruction Coordinator of the school and faculty concerned shall meet at least twice during each semester period with the Medical Social Work Adviser of the Ministry of Health and the Chief of Ancillary Services/Head of Medical Social Services of the hospital to jointly plan and thresh out problems with regard to the affiliation programs.


13. Psychology
	13.1 Hospitals authorized by the Ministry of health to accept students majoring the Psychology as student affiliates in Clinical Psychology must have the following requirements.

	13.1.1 A hospital with an organized Psychological Services Department with qualified Psychologists to teach and supervise students majoring in Psychology
	13.1.2 The number of students for specified period will be in proper proportion to the number of qualified staff which will all be four (4) students to one Psychologist in Acute and Diagnostic Areas, Rehabilitation Areas and Long Term Treatment Areas
	13.1.3 Shall provide qualified psychologists to guide, teach, supervise and evaluate Student’s performance at the ration of 1:4
	13.1.4 Shall provide necessary supplies and materials for the learning experiences of the student affiliates
	13.1.5 During the stay of the student affiliates in the designated hospitals, the hospital will not be held responsible for whatever happens to them in case of accidents, etc.
13.2 Affiliating schools offering majors in Psychology must be recognized and duly accredited by the authorities of the Ministry of Education and Culture before applying for affiliation of its students majoring in psychology in a teaching and training hospital of the Ministry of Health.

13.3 Affiliating schools shall pay or replace any damages or losses of test materials by the faculty and students of the schools and shall enforce that the practicum of each student be set at a minimum of twenty (20) hours per week. 

It shall see to it that all students for practicum in Clinical Psychology must have finished the theoretical aspect for this work: They must be in their senior year and must have taken specifically Psychology or Personality, Clinical Psychology, Abnormal Psychology, Psycholetics, Statistics, and Projective Techniques.

13.4 The school affiliating with the Ministry of Health Hospitals shall preferably, provide a Preceptor to teach and guide affiliating students in their clinical materials shall be made in six (6) months before the start of the Students training.

13.5 All efforts must be made to establish a linkage mutually acceptable between the Affiliating School and the Ministry of Health Agency.

14. Occupational and Physical Therapy

14.1.1.1 A well-organized occupational and physical therapy services with qualified staff to teach and supervise students.

14.1.2 An adequate conference room and library facilities for the use of students.

	14.1.3 The hospital shall provide necessary supplies and equipment for the learning experience of students.

15. Dentistry

I. Responsibilities of the Ministry of Health Administered Hospitals


1. Teaching and Training Hospitals duly accredited by the Bureau of Medical Services upon recommendation of the Bureau of Dental Health Services shall be authorized to conduct Affiliation Training for Hospital Dentistry.



2. The admission of affiliate for dental students will depend upon the number of available dental staff in the hospital. However, all efforts must be made to establish a linkage mutually acceptable between the school and the Ministry of Health Hospital. 


3. The dental service of the teaching-training hospitals must be organized with at least a Chief of Dental Clinics and an adequate staff of dentists covering no less than twenty four (24) hours service/on call.

4. The Chief of Dental Clinics in the hospital must be involved in the evaluation and planning of suitable education and training program in coordination with the Bureau of Dental Health Services and the Training Coordinator/Officer of the hospital for professional staff development. 

5. The Chief of Hospital must make available modern and adequate facilities including multiple function dental units, hydraulic chairs (motorized or pump chair), high speed turbine drill, ultrasonic scaling/polishing devices among others in addition to at least a complete set of dental instrument for every dental operatory.

6. Hospital administration must provide adequate floor space for each of a dental operatory with good ventilation, lighting, water system and electrical supply following standard ratio for teaching dental clinics.

7. Standard operating procedures for dental services and its hospital related function must be in print and upgraded periodically subject to review by the Bureau of Dental Health Services. 

8. Library and Record Filing System a An adequate compilation of dental literature, journals and books updated on time should be made readily available for students’ use. A record keeping, filing and recall system is supportive of the program for purposes of orientation and reference.

9. Records of training for each participant must be prepared by the Chief of Dental Clinics concerned, approved by the Chief of Hospital following the termination of each training group. An evaluation of the dental students’ affiliation must be a part of the training report.

10. Training hospitals should submit quarterly report to the National Committee on Training and Affiliation of Students of the Ministry of Health.

II. Hospital should provide necessary supplies and materials for the use of the undergraduate dental students.

1. The undergraduate dental students should comply strictly with the rules and regulations of the hospital.

2. Undergraduate dental students of affiliating dental colleges who cause damages and/or losses of hospital property, equipment, supplies, etc., are required to replace or to pay for the same.
3. Each undergraduate dental student through the sponsoring dental should pay the hospital P200.00 per student per semester within five (5) days to start of training. Payment is due within the first five (5) days at the start of this semester.
4. Dental colleges affiliating with the hospitals under the Ministry of Health shall be required to send a preceptor to teach and guide dental students in hospital dentistry practice. The ratio of dental students to a preceptor shall depend upon the number of students to be sent to the hospital depending upon mutual agreement to be established by the college and hospital authorities concerned. As preceptor coming from the dental hospital’s policies, rules and regulations for a period of one week before the semester or before the actual affiliation of dental students starts.

16. Collection and Disbursement of affiliation fees
16.1 Affiliation fees paid by the students for their training in government hospitals/rural health units intended for honorarium or incentive pay for staff members who participated in such training or other personnel directly engaged in patient care shall be deposited in an authorized government depository bank as a trust liability, and the hospitals, rural health units concerned is authorized to withdraw the amount deposited on the joint signatures of the authorized representatives of the hospital/rural health unit and the Commission on Audit without the need for Cash Disbursement Ceiling. Provided, that if the amount is deposited in a savings account, the interest shall accrue to the general funds. Provided, further, that the quarterly reports of income and payments of such fund shall be submitted to the Ministry of the Budget in such format and following such procedures as the Ministry of the Budget may prescribe

17. Special Provision
	Other government owned or controlled schools or colleges shall be required to pay the affiliation fee to the Ministry of Health hospitals and rural health units, except the University of the Philippines. However, the University of the Philippines shall be required to accomplish the necessary contract of affiliation

18. Repealing Clause
Administrative Order Nos. 87 and 93, s. 1980, and all other orders inconsistent with the Provision of this Administrative Order, are hereby repealed.
19. Effectivity
This Administrative order shall take effect immediately.
