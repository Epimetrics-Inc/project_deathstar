<subject>SUBJECT: Creation of a Committee on Accreditation of Urban Pest Control Operators</subject>

<body>
Pursuant to PD 856, the Code on Sanitation and the provisions of the Memorandum of Agreement entered into and signed between the Ministry of Health (MOH) and the Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority (FPA) on July 15, 1980, the MOH shall accredit all government and commercial urban pest control operators.

The objective of such accreditation is to ensure proper control and supervision over urban pest control operators, both commercial and government, and to ensure further that all official transactions made by this Ministry, all its agencies and other private institutions, shall only be with legitimate pest control operators.

For this purpose, a Committee on Accreditation of Urban Pest Control Operators is hereby created to be composed of the following Head of Agency or their duly authorized representative:

a. Bureau of Health Services
b. Bureau of Quarantine
c. Food and Drug Administration
d. Malaria Eradication Services
e. Fertilizer and Pesticide Authority

The Committee is charged with the following responsibilities:

1. On the basis of MOH guidelines and requirements for Accreditation of Urban Pest Control Operators, accept applications, screen, examine, accredit and recommend approval of pest control operator qualifications prior to their pest control activity.

2. Certify periodically and update listing of accredited urban pest control operators showing their names, addresses and specific activity handled.

3. The Committee, upon application of the operator, is authorized to:

a. Make official inquiries to the various government agencies relative to manpower, product, outlet, licensing, registration, etc.
b. Secure from the operator his latest financial statement, tax clearance, physical plant layout, etc.
c. Conduct ocular inspection of the operations, place of business, laboratories, etc.
d. Perform such act or acts as may be necessary to accomplish the objective of the Committee.

4. Draft implementing rules and regulations to implement Section 3 and Section 4, Chapter I and Chapter XVI of PD 856, The Code on Sanitation and the guidelines for accreditation as approved by the Minister of Health. Sanctions and penalties as may be required shall be incorporated in the implementing rules and regulations for the effective implementation of objectives.

The Committee is further authorized to establish Sub-Committees necessary for effective implementation of this Order.

For services rendered, the Committee and Sub-Committee members are entitled to all allowances and reimbursements of expenses, subject to accounting and auditing regulations, chargeable against the funds of this Ministry.

All orders and circulars or parts thereof inconsistent herewith are hereby amended and/or repealed.

This Order shall take effect immediately upon publication.
