<subject>SUBJECT: Prescribing guidelines regarding work schedule and personnel deployment pursuant to Memorandum Circular No. 111 dated January 17, 1995 issued by the President</subject>


<body>

Pursuant to Memorandum Circular No. 111 dated January 17, 1995 issued by the President of the Philippines, a11 Heads of Offices and Programs of the Department of Health which dea1 with public transactions and delivery of services, are hereby directed to plan a work schedule to ensure the effective implementation of the said Memorandum Circular.

Accordingly, the following guidelines are prescribed for the information, guidance and strict compliance by all concerned:


1. All offices which perform regulatory and licensing functions shall prepare a six (6) day week working schedule from Monday to Saturday, 7:00 A M to 7:00 P.M. continuously without break; 


2. All offices with health delivery and public information function shall prepare a work schedule to operate on a 24 hours a day for 7 days a week; 


3. Those already rendering 24- hour services, such as medical centers and hospitals shall continue to do so;


4. For purposes of uniformity and easy monitoring of absences and undertimes of personnel of the offices not falling under Item Nos. 1 to 3 above, the following schedule shall be adopted:

4.1 Under this scheme, there shall be only two (2) groups per agency, each group adhering to either one of these schedules as the regular working hours of the employees concerned

4.2 At least one (1) responsible officer of the agency shall be included in each group


5. All offices are hereby directed to submit to the Office of the Secretary (Attn. The Chief of Staff) their schedules of work and personnel deployment that will ensure compliance with the 40 hours a week law;


6. The Administrative Service shall be responsible for the consolidation of work schedules and personnel of offices, which in turn shall be submitted to the Office of the President through the Presidential Management Staff as required under M.C. 111.


The deadline for the submission of the work schedule duly approved by the respective Heads of Offices is on before February 15, 1995.
