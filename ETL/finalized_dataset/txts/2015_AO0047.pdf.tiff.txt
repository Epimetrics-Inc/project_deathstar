I. BACKGROUND/ RATIONALE 

The Universal Health Care — High Impact Five (UHC — Hi-S) Strategy was established by DOH Regional Ofﬁces in April 2015 for implementation in the succeeding 15 months to achieve the goals of Kalusugan Pangkalahatan. Administrative Order No. 2015-0033, or “Guidelines on the Implementation of the Universal Health Care — High Impact Five Strategy for DOH Hospitals,” details the complementary efforts of DOH-Retained Hospitals to intensify their operations to achieve outcomes for UHC. 

Kapit Bisig para sa Kalusugan Pangkalahatan is one of the special breakthrough activities by DOH-Retained Hospitals to extend access to medical and surgical services in priority poverty areas. It is a medical-surgical caravan to be jointly conducted by DOH Hospitals, DOH Regional Ofﬁces, and LGU Hospitals, and done in conjunction with the KP Caravan of DOH Regional Ofﬁces. Select major and minor surgical procedures, medical consultations, and community teaching/ lay fora will be conducted by DOH retained hospitals in priority areas. 

II. OBJECTIVES 

A. GENERAL OBJECTIVE 

To provide targeted elective major and minor operative care to patients with poor access to specialized surgical services in Hi-5 priority areas. 

B. SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES 

1. To increase access of patients to medical and surgical services; 

2. To provide a venue for collaboration and social responsibility among the public health sector and local ofﬁcials; and 

3. To provide a venue for health promotion and advocacy on Hi-S key interventions. 

III. SCOPE 

This Order shall apply to all DOH Cluster Heads, Hospital Chiefs and all DOH staff of Bureaus, Ofﬁces, Services, Regional Ofﬁces, and DOH-ARMM, and Philippine Health Insurance Corporation. All other key health players, such as Local Chief Executives and LGU Hospitals and health facilities, and others concerned are likewise included. 

IV. DEFINITION OF TERMS/ ACRONYMS 

A. Visiting Hospital — DOH-Retained Hospital deployed to a speciﬁc province to assist the Host Hospital in the conduct of “KB para sa KP. 

B. Host Hospital — DOH-Retained or LGU hospital that will serve as the venue for the “KB para sa KP."

C. Institutional Health Care Provider (IHCP) Portal — a system deployed by PhilHealth in Health Care Institution-s which is capable of providing membership and  eligibility details necessary for beneﬁt availment 

D. Cataract Pre-Surgery Authorization (CPSA) — authorization granted by PhilHealth prior to cataract surgery/ies to ensure the quality and appropriateness of the proposed service/s and to safeguard against possible abuses and unethical practices. CPSA must be granted for a cataract surgery to ensure that the procedure is reimbursable. 

E. Medical Assistance Program (MAP) — a DOH program for granting medical assistance to indigent and poor patients 


v. GENERAL GUIDELINES 

A. All DOH Hospitals and selected LGU Hospitals shall participate in" the conduct of “KB para sa KP.”

B. The conduct of “KB para sa KP” shall be held simultaneously nationwide. 

C. DOH Regional Ofﬁces, DOHHospitals, LGU Hospitals, LGUs, and PhilHealth shall  collaborate for: 


1. Fund/ resource sharing 

2. Identiﬁcation and clearance of beneﬁciaries 

3. Implementation arrangements 

D. Priority beneﬁciaries are families from the identiﬁed priority provinces. Annex A lists”the priority provinces and the identiﬁed Visiting Hospitals. 

E. The target number of surgeries for “KB para sa KP” should take into consideration the need of the province being served and the capacity of both the Visiting and Host Hospitals. At least a total of .100 major and minor surgeries may be done in a mission visit, but major surgeries shall not exceed 100 per province. 

F. The following surgical cases may include but are not limited to the following: 

Major 

- Thyroidectomy
- Cleft lip, plastic repair, cleft lip/ nasal deformity primary bilateral, one of 2 stages 
- Cataract surgery 
- Herniorrhaphy 
- Hydrocoelectomy 

Minor 
- Excision breast mass 
- Excision of sebaceous cyst, pilar- cyst 
- Pterygium 

G. DOH hospitals may opt for additional activities during the “KB para sa KP,” such as previously identiﬁed minor surgeries and advocacy activities. 

H. Host Hospitals shall aim to ensure PhilHealth coverage of its surgical beneﬁciaries. 

VI. SPECIFIC GUIDELINES 

DOH Hospitals shall coordinate closely with their respective DOH Regional Ofﬁces and Local Go'vernments for the synchronized and effective conduct of “KB para sa KP.” The following activities shall be carried out: 

A. Pre-mission activities

Visiting Hospitals, DOH Regional Ofﬁces, and LGU hospitals shall meet and coordinate to plan out all logistics including food, accommodations, local travel, and security arrangements. The following pro-mission activities shall be undertaken: 

1. Preparation of Action Plans -- All Visiting and Host HOSpital Teams shall submit Action Plans, to include targets and needed resources, to their respective DOH Regional Ofﬁces. See Annex B for the Action Plan template.

2. Identiﬁcation of the Host Hospital — which shall be done by the DOH Regional Ofﬁce based on accessibilityand capacity, and in coordination with the Visiting Hospital 

3. Ensuring the preparedness of the Host Hospital — 'which shall be done collaboratively between the Visiting Hospital, Host Hospital, and DOH Regional Ofﬁce 

a. The Visiting Hospital shall conduct- an ocular visit to the Host Hospital (when indicated) to identify needs for “KB para sa KP.” 

b. The Host Hospital shall prepare its facility (in terms of health human resources, supplies and medicine, and-equipment as agreed upon), including ensuring that its facility is capable of PhilHealth’s Point-of-Care Enrollment. 

i. PhilHealth shall facilitate PhilHealth coverage of “KB para sa KP” beneﬁciaries in close coordination with Host Hospitals and PhilHealth Regional Ofﬁces (PROS). 

ii. PROS have been requested to facilitate installation of the Institutional Health Care Provider (IHCP) Portal and the activation of the Cataract Pre- Surgery Authorization (CPSA) module. However, this module will be deactivated once the caravan has ended. Furthermore, the Host Hospital ‘ will still need to formally request CPSA module installation/activation from their respective PRO. 

iii. Authorized physicians and surgeons should request for the CPSA using the IHCP Portal.  

4. Initial enlistment of surgical beneﬁciaries — DOH Regional Ofﬁces shall initiate the enlistment of potential surgical beneﬁciaries, in coordination with .the Host _ Hospital (which will conduCt the initial screening of these beneﬁciaries). A survey form shall be used for the initial enlistment of targeted patients in each Region (see Annex C), while Annex D lists the minimum laboratory work—up required (NB, the Visiting and Host Hospital shall ﬁnalize the needed laboratory/diagnostic tests) 

5. Finalization of list of surgical beneﬁciaries - Visiting Hospitals shall issue ﬁnal clearance of patients, which will be the basis for ﬁnalizing the list of surgical beneficiaries. 

a. For identiﬁed surgical beneﬁciaries that exceed the target number, those that cannot be accommodated shall be referred to the nearest and most capable DOH Hospital for management. In this case, their management shall be subject to the prevailing rules/ regulations of that hospital. 

b. In extreme cases, targeted patients with special needs (e.g., complicated case or with medical complications) may be brought to Visiting Hospitals for management. 

6. Preparation of Memorandum of Agreement - Shall be done by Visiting Hospitals with their counterpart DOH Regional Ofﬁces, Local Governments, and LGU Hospitals to outline expected roles and respOnSibilities and counter parting of resources for “KB para sa KP,” including the optional activities (e. g., community teaching and lay fora) (see Annex E). 

7. Orientation and brieﬁng of medical-surgical teams — Medical- Surgical teams must be oriented and briefed on “KB para sa KP” prior to their deployment by their team leader/s. 

B. Logistics 

1. The Host Hospital shall be responsible for preparing the facility for the mission and for identifying additiOnal human resources to support the Visiting Hospital team. 

2. The Visiting Hospital shall be responsible for funding its own travel needs, but should coordinate closely with its DOH RegiOnal Ofﬁce and LGU for other related logistic needs (e. g., local transportation, food, and accommodation). The DOH Regional Ofﬁce and/or LGU shall ensure the security of the Visiting 

3. Hospital throughout the duration of “KB para sa KP” (i. e., from arrival to departure). 

4. Required equipment, supplies, and medicine must be identiﬁed prior to or during the ocular visit of Visiting Hospitals, and their transport shall be arranged by the Visiting Hospital in close coordination with the ROS and Host Hospital. 

5. Transportation of patients shall be provided according to an agreed arrangement by the LGU, ROS, and Host Hospital. 

C. Human Resources 

1. Visiting Hospitals shall send medical— surgical teams consisting of the appropriate medical and surgical specialists, nurses, and technicians. All members of the Visiting Hospital team must be PhilHealth-accredited. 

2. Host Hospitals shall also identify and commit additional human resources (both medical and non- mediCal) to support the Visiting Hospital. 

3. Duly afﬁliated private physicians (including PAO ophthalmologists) of the Visiting Hospital or the Host Hospital may participate in the “KB para sa KP.” 

4. Physicians of Visiting and Host Hospitals that participate in the KB para sa KP shall not receive any direCt compensation or incentive for their service (e.g., PF reimbursement from PHIC). 

D. “KB para sa KP” Proper 

1. Visiting Hospitals shall be paired with their counterpart physicians from the Host Hospital (whether surgical or medical) in the surgical and post- operative management of surgical beneficiaries. Surgeries must be performed by the appropriate Specialists (e. g., ophthalmologists to perform eye surgeries). Residents may perform surgeries only under the supervision of the consultants. 

The diagnosis and management of surgical cases shall adhere to clinical guidelines set by or adhered to by specialty societies. 

a. The Philippine Cornea Society recOmmends that pterygium surgeries should only be done when the pterygium has involved both eyes involving the pupillary area and with uncorrected vision of 20/200 or worse. (full text of the recommendation is in Annex F). 

b. Additional guidelines, recommendations, and related issuances for cataract surgeries are included here as Annexes F- J. 

3 Suspicious excised masses must undergo histopathologic analysis to be done by the Regional Medical Centers or by another health facility as mutually agreed. upon by the RO, Visiting Hospital, and Host Hospital. 

4. All PhilHealth claims to be made under “KB para sa KP” shall be clearly marked as “KB para sa KP” using the stamp design to be provided by the DOH-CO. 

5. The Visiting Hospitals Shall attend to the immediate post-operative care (including complications) of all surgical patients with the assistance of Host Hospitals, after which care must be formally transferred to the Host Hospital.

6. Visiting Hospitals Shall document the conduct of “KB para sa KP” with the assistance of the DOH Regional Ofﬁces. 

E. Post- “KB para sa KP” activities 

1. All Host Hospitals must conduct a post- activity evaluation and debrieﬁng of their “KB para sa KP” with DOH and LGU Hospitals and DOH Regional Ofﬁces. 

2. Departure of Visiting Hospitals must be closely coordinated with the relevant DOH Regional Ofﬁce'and Local Government (if necessary). 

3. The following are the Reporting Mechanisms:

a. M&E reporting for the Hi-S Strategy shall adhere to the guidelines set forth in Administrative Order No. 2015— 0043, “Monitoring and Evaluation Framework on the Implementation of the Universal Health Care High Impact Five (UHC- HIS) Strategy.” 

b. Visiting Hospitals shall submit an accomplishment report to the Hospital Core Group through the Ofﬁce for Health Operations within 1 week after the mission. 

F. Financing 

1. “KB para sa KP” Shall be carried out using existing DOH Hospital budgets to be augmented by the DOH Central Ofﬁce. 

a. Authorization to use hospital income is given through DO 2015- 0153, “Guidelines on the Use of Hospital Income for the Major Launching of the Hospital Hi- 5, Conduct of Kapit Bisig para sa Kalusugan Pangkalahatan (KB para sa KP) and National Hospital Week Celebration.” 

b. Sub-allotted funds are to be used according to DO 2015—2015-0161, “Guidelines on the Sub-Allotment and Utilization of Funds from the 3 Department of Health~Central Ofﬁce (DOH-CO) to DOH Hospitals for the Major Launching of the Hospital Hi— 5, Conduct of Kapit Bisig para sa Kalusugan Pangkalahatan (KB para sa KP) and National Hospital Week Celebration.” 

2. Surgical procedures done during medical misSions using government funds and in accredited government facilities are compensable with PhilHealth. Annex K provides guidance on this. 

3. Hospital income or the Medical Assistance Program may be used to pay PhilHealth premiums of beneﬁciaries enrolled through PhilHealth’s Point of Care Enrollment Program. 

a. Annex L details the implementing guidelines for the POC Enrollment 

b. Annexes M and N authorize the use of hospital income and MAP funds for payment of PhilHealth premiums of indigents. 

4. Other laboratory and diagnostic procedures, drugs, medicines, and supplies, and professional fees incurred pre- or post- operatively may also be covered by the MAP funds. 

a. AO 2015- 0025 or, “Amended Guidelines for the Implementation of the Medical Assistance to Indigent and Poor Patients in Government Hospitals,” provides guidanCe on the use of MAP funds. 

VII. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES 

A. DOH Retained, Special and Specialty Hospitals 

1. Ensure successful conduct of “KB para sa KP” in coordination with ROs, LGUS, and other stakeholders, 

2. Coordinate with ROS and LGU Hospitals for logistical requirements, 

3. Provide technical expertise (medical and surgical services and post-operative care) - and resource augmentation for “KB para sa KP;” and 

4. Report accomplishments to the Hospital Core Group and fund utilization to DOH—CO. 

B. Regional Ofﬁces 

1. Facilitate all coordination activities (pre-mission, mission, and post-mission activities), including case- ﬁnding and logistical arrangements, 

2. Augment resources as needed and agreed upon by all parties in the “KB para sa KP; ’ 

3. Participate in post—activity evaluation of “KB para sa KP; ” and 

4. Collate all reports of the Visiting and Host Hospitals and submit to the DOH—CO. 

C. Central Ofﬁce — Support “KB para sa KP” through: 

Health Policy Development and Planning Bureau: 
Provide technical support in the preparatory activities of “KB para sa KP” 

Ofﬁce for Health Operations: 

1. Provide technical support in the pre— mission, mission proper, and post— mission activities for “KB para sa KP” and 

2. Monitor and report overall performance of “KB para sa KP. ” 

D. Local Government Units 

1. Engage all local health ofﬁcials and personnel to support the conduct of “KB para sa KP;” 

2. Augment resources as needed and agreed upon. with the DOH Hospitals and DOH Regional Ofﬁces; 

3. Assist in the logistical arrangements for “KB para sa KP, ” including security arrangements for the Visiting Hospital, and 

4. Facilitate transportation for patients as agreed upon with the DOH Regional Ofﬁces and Host Hospitals. 

E. PhilHealth 

1. Facilitate enrollment and claims reimbursement of beneﬁciaries of “KB para sa KP” through their .PROSand ' 

2. Participate in the monitoring of the conduct of “KB para sa KP."

VIII. REPEALING CLAUSE 

All Orders, rules, regulations, and other related issuances inconsistent with or contrary to this Order are hereby repealed, amended or modiﬁed accordingly. All other provisions of existing issuances which are not affected by this Order shall remain valid and in effect. 

IX. EFFECTIVITY DATE 

This Administrative Order shall take effect immediately.
