<subject>SUBJECT: Regulation Part C-7 DRUGS AND DEVICES: Certification of Antibiotics.</subject>

<body>
C - 7.1.0 Section 22(a) of RA 3720, otherwise known as the Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act, provides for the certification of batches of drugs composed wholly or partly of any kind of antibiotic or any of its derivatives. Under authority of said section and Section 26(a) of the same Act, this regulation is hereby promulgated.

C - 7.1.1 All kinds of antibiotic and antibiotic containing products, whether in bulk, semifinished or finished form, and whether imported or domestically produced, are subject to batch certification, before they can enter the country or can be released for sale.

C - 7.1.2 Samples of each batch of antibiotic and antibiotic containing products shall be submitted to the Food and Drug Administration by the importer, repacker or manufacturer.

C - 7.1.3 Sample submitted for certification shall be accompanied by documents, stating among other things the following:

a. Name and address of the foreign manufacturer of the antibiotic.
b. Name and address of the local importer, repacker or manufacturer.
c. Date of manufacture of the antibiotic and expiration date.
d. Certificate of report of analysis from the producer of the antibiotic.
e. Methods of tests and assays used.
f. Batch number, size of batch, and packaging of batch.

C - 7.1.4 The quantity of sample to be submitted for a semi-finished or finished antibiotic containing product shall consist of one unit immediate container for each 3000 units of the same batch number, but in no case shall such sample consist of less than 3 or more than 10 unit immediate container.

C - 7.1.5 In order to check properly the implementation of this regulation, proper recording of the quantity of each antibiotic received, imported or repacked for distribution or processing, shall be kept for a period of not less than 3 years, and such records shall be open for inspection at any time.

C - 7.1.6 This Order supersedes other administrative issuances inconsistent herewith.

C - 7.1.7 This regulation shall take effect thirty (30) days after publication in the Official Gazette.
