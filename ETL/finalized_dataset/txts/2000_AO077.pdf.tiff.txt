<subject>SUBJECT: REQUIRING ALL PHYSICIANS, DENTISTS AND VETERINARIANS TO ISSUE ORDINARY PRESCRIPTIONS FOR PRESCRIPTION DRUGS IN DUPLICATE.</subject>

<body>

In line with the policy of the state as enunciated in the provisions of RA 6675 (Generics Act of 1988) to encourage the use of generic names or generic terminology in all transactions related to importation, manufacture, distribution, marketing, advertising and promotion, prescribing and dispensing and in relation to AO No. 62 s. 1989, Rules and Regulations to Implement Prescribing Requirements under the Generics Act of 1988, the following policy and guidelines are hereby issued for the strict compliance of all concerned:

1. All prescription drugs, which require ordinary prescription, issued by duly licensed physicians, dentists and veterinarians, shall be written only in ordinary prescription forms.

2. Said ordinary prescription forms must be issued in duplicate copies, one copy to be retained by the drug outlet and another copy by the patient.

3. For monitoring purposes and consistent with the provisions of Sec. 32 of the Pharmacy Law (RA 5921) as amended, all prescriptions shall be preserved by the drug outlet for a period of not less than two (2) years.

This Order shall take effect thirty (30) days from the date of its publication in two (2) newspapers of general simulation.
