<subject>SUBJECT: Policies and Guidelines Sale by Drug Outlets of Generic Alternatives at Discounted Price</subject>

<body>

Pursuant to Sec. 9 of Republic Act No. 6675, otherwise known as the Generics Act of 1988, the following policies and guidelines for the implementation of the said Act are hereby promulgated to govern the sale by Drug Outlets of generic alternatives at discounted prices.

Section 1. Definition of Terms. The following terms as defined under Sec.3 of the aforesaid Act for purposes of this Order are hereby adopted:

Drug Outlets refer to Drugstores, Pharmacies and any other business establishments which sell drugs or medicines;

Generic drugs are drugs not covered by patent protection and which are labeled solely by their international non proprietary or generic name;

Branded generic drugs which carry a brand or proprietary name in addition to its generic or official name. 

Section 2. Guidelines on Sale of Generic Alternatives. The following guidelines shall be strictly observed on sale of generic alternatives at discounted prices:

1. All drug outlets, through their registered pharmacists, shall have full discretion and responsibility in dispensing the generic alternatives to the prescribed medicine in accordance with the provisions of the Generics Act.

2. Discounts granted by the Senior Citizens Act or RA 7432 and relevant laws on purchases of medicines and other drug products shall be given by drug outlets on their sale of generic drugs.

Section 3. Repealing Clause

All Administrative Orders, Rules and Regulations and other Administrative Issuances or parts thereof, inconsistent with the provisions of the Administrative Order are hereby repealed and modified accordingly.
 
Section 4. Effectivity

This Order shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in two (2) newspapers of general circulation.
