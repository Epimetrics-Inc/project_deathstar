<subject>SUBJECT: Provisional Accreditation of DOH Drugs and Medicines Suppliers</subject>


<body>

“Provisional accreditation”, for the privilege to participate in the bidding processes for the procurement of drugs and medicines conducted by the Department of Health, and so long as they possess a valid and existing “License to Operate” (LTO), shall be granted pharmaceutical/drug suppliers whose:

1) application, or the renewal thereof, for accreditation has been ﬁled on or before 31 July 2001; 
2) earlier accreditation is about to expire on or before 31 December 2001.

Said “provisional accreditation” shall remain valid up to and until 31 December 2001 but does not cover those whose:

1) application or renewal was earlier denied for cause;
2) LTO was earlier revoked or suspended and no clearance has been given by the concerned ofﬁce/agency;
3) Products, subject matter of the bidding, are deemed to be violative, as so provided under LB. No. 4 (a-c) of AO 14, series of 2001 and no clearance as provided form I.B. No.5 has been given.

In any case, no payment shall be made to the winning bidder unless and until BFAD has conducted the requisite and satisfactory laboratory analysis except only in instance when III B, AO 14, s. 2001 is applicable.

This Order shall amend, modify or change all existing issuances and orders related to the subject.

Effective immediately.


</body>
